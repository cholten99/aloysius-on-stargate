Bloodlines

 

Story: Mark Sarceni

Teleplay: Jeff King

Director: Mario Azzopardi

 

Most of the time, I tend to forget that Junior is even around, so watching the implantation of a Jaffa child is really more than I want to know!  I share Jack’s distinct distaste when it comes to the Goa’uld in their natural form – I’ve never been keen on slithery things…

 

Anyway, once the dream sequence is over, we move straight into team solidarity, with the other three all huddled together looking on anxiously as Janet carries out tests on Teal’c.  Then, when Teal’c behaves strangely in the briefing, Daniel expresses concern and Jack immediately goes to find out what’s wrong.  After that, all three of the others fall over themselves to present strategic advantages, risk assessments and so on, on order to persuade General Hammond to let them go to Chulak.  There’s a very strong sense of the SG-1 family here.

 

Hammond demonstrates what a lovely CO he is, once again – he isn’t swayed by the military proposals but agrees to let them go once he knows about Teal’c’s family.  I’m not sure his superiors would take kindly to his attitude, but it’s very sweet.

 

Introduction of Bra’tac – woohoo!  I just love this guy – he is so cool and very funny, particularly when interacting with Jack.  They set up several running jokes in this episode – their one-up-man-ship with regards to fighting, the use of “not bad”, Bra’tac referring to Jack as “human”, and of course the whole Hammond of Texas thing.  All classic!

 

The entrance of Teal’c’s wife is pretty dramatic (I’d use her name, but I don’t know how to spell it!) but Teal’c’s family soap opera has never really interested me.  I’m not keen on his wife or Rya’c – and Teal’c himself is my least favourite member of SG-1.

 

Sam and Daniel on a quest for a Goa’uld larva, on the other hand, is a great subplot.  I’m not sure why Daniel says, “I’m ready” when Sam is preparing to take one out of the tank, though, because he doesn’t actually do anything!  Directly afterwards, however, our mild-mannered archaeologist turns into a vicious baby-killing machine.  I’m guessing this is why they don’t normally give him a big gun – he’s clearly trigger happy, since the first time he lays hands on a rifle, he immediately starts spraying bullets about!  I don’t think any of us mourn the passing of the larvae, though, so I guess we can let him off.  I do like the way Sam carefully takes back possession of her weapon.

 

Then we get the fleeting moment that has spawned an obsessive Daniel characteristic in fanfiction.  On their way back to the gate, Daniel is seen with a chocolate bar in his hand.  He demonstrates clear chocoholic characteristics, since he manages to keep hold of it whilst fleeing Jaffa in the forest, only throwing it away when he has to get out his gun to defend his life!  (There’s the cookie corroboration in Forever in a Day, too.)

 

The tearful family farewell scene just doesn’t do anything to me at all.  I see absolutely no chemistry between Teal’c and his wife (even less in later eps when she’s played by a different actress).  I also don’t understand why she and Rya’c don’t go back to Earth with them there and then!  I do like her outfit, though.

 

Teal’c as a supporting character, acting as foil, straight man or loyal friend, is responsible for some of the best moments in Stargate SG-1, in my opinion.  However, when he has to carry an entire episode as the central focus, I’m afraid I tend to find proceedings rather tedious.  What saves me from actively disliking these episodes is that they generally also include Bra’tac, who is fantastic.

 

Characteristic Moments

 

Jack’s continuing attempts to make himself look stupid, here with an instance of characteristic malapropism.

During the scene where they meet Bra’tac for the first time:

Bra’tac: How could you bring these hashak with you?

Jack: Hey, hey, hey!  Who’re you calling a hassock?

 

Jack confusing stolid Jaffa by using Earth idioms.

When planning their approach to the outcast camp:

Teal’c: If we are captured, you will be brought before Apophis along with us.

Jack: Well, we’ll just have to cross that bridge when we come to it.

Bra’tac: No, the bridge is too well guarded.

Jack: Well, actually, there I was using a cliché…

 

 

Best Bits

 

How the whole Hammond of Texas thing got started:

Bra’tac: This Hammond…

Jack: Just a man.  A very good, very bald man from Texas.

 

Classic Jack/Bra’tac interaction:

Jack: Alright, here’s the plan…

Bra’tac: You will do as I say.

Jack: Right.

Jack has finally met his match!!
