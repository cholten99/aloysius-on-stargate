Brief Candle

 

Story: Steven Barnes

Teleplay: Katharyn Powers

Director: Mario Azzopardi

 

Ah, the episode of the beautiful people!  How many attractive young extras wearing flimsy clothing can they fit in one episode?  Quite a few!

 

I’m not sure why Cynthia chooses Jack, though, since he’s wearing the silly hat.  It doesn’t suit him any better than it does Daniel, though it looks much sillier on Jack because we see him in it a lot less often.

 

We have two staple plot devices in this episode – it starts with the heroes being confronted by a pregnant women about to give birth (something that happens at least once in every TV show known to man) and ventures quickly onto the sci-fi tradition of rapid ageing.  I love the way all the guys automatically look to Sam when faced with the imminent birth and I love her complete lack of knowledge in the area even more.

 

Daniel, however, demonstrates just how multi-talented he is – as Jack points out afterwards.  Is there anything this guy can’t do?  :O)

 

First question of the episode: why is there no-one over forty in the village?  Just because they age rapidly, it doesn’t follow that they would all be young – quite the contrary!  The only reason there would be no old people is if Pelops had only set up the experiment less than two months ago, but the guy they meet first talks about ancestors, and their theory that the gods live in the sky suggests that no Goa’uld has been there in some time.

 

Nobody can argue Jack/Sam shippiness in this episode.  Sam finds the advances of Cynthia extremely amusing – and later on, Jack is only thinking about Sara when he believes he will never leave Argos.

 

Jack is the only one of the four to get any action across all five seasons (though I guess we don’t know what goes on when Teal’c goes to visit his family in the Land of Light later on…).  Cynthia is the first of two liaisons Jack gets to enjoy.  And his love interests don’t die, either – he just always leaves them.  Daniel’s of course all get taken as hosts by the Goa’uld, and Sam’s path is strewn with the bodies of guys who have made the mistake of fancying her.  One of the drawbacks to being the hero of a TV show is having an abysmal love life!

 

Daniel calls Jack “Colonel” again!  This tendency is really weirding me out – I was quite sure it never happened; there’s obviously something wrong with my memory!

 

I always like episodes where Sam and Janet get screen time together.  As the only two regular female characters, it’s nice to see them without the guys every now and then.  They’re both great characters and they interact very well with each other.

 

There’s a great team solidarity moment when Jack orders the others to go back to Earth to work on the problem.  Sam wants to set up a lab on Argos, Teal’c volunteers to remain behind and Daniel is clearly very unhappy about abandoning Jack.  Jack of course is only thinking about protecting them – great team spirit from all of them here.

 

Then we get the Super Sam plot device!  Whenever they need someone to have knowledge of something scientific in order to advance the plot, it miraculously turns out that Sam studied that exact field sometime in the past.  In this case, she luckily worked on nanotechnology for a year during her time at the Pentagon.

 

Jack’s bitterness next to Cynthia’s wide-eyed innocence is a great contrast.  I really like Cynthia – in fact, I think the minor characters are generally very good in Stargate.  She has fantastic clothes too – I believe the correct word would be diaphanous!

 

Jack’s hair seems to grow rather more quickly than his beard – and it’s a good job is nails don’t follow that trend too!

 

In the scene where the other three report that Hammond has ordered Argos off limits, there’s something I’d never noticed before.  Jack isn’t actually talking to them – he’s watching a recording of their farewell message.  Right at the end, you can hear him rewinding it, presumably so he can watch it again, which is rather sweet!

 

When they all go outside right at the end, I noticed Jack manages to arrange it so that he’s supported by Sam and Cynthia – the wily old dog still manages to surround himself with women even when old and decrepit!  However, it is Sam who signals to the others to leave Jack and Cynthia alone to say goodbye – no shippiness evident at all!

 

I’m not sure the science/logic behind the nanotechnology really works, but that kind of thing never actually bothers me.  My mind is a bit like Jack’s – I have a total inability to assimilate science, so I never know if the technobabble makes sense!  I just nod and agree!

 

This is the first episode where I wish we could see what happens next – I’d love to see how General Hammond and the rest of the SGC personnel react to Jack coming back all old – I bet it would have been really funny!

 

Characteristic Moments

 

The catchphrase is back!

When the Argosians gather to pray to Pelops:

Jack: For crying out loud!  Old Pelops doesn’t give a rat’s ass about things like love!

 

First instance of Jack referring to the other three as “kids”, and also rather appropriate in this case!

When the others return to Argos at the end:

Jack: Welcome back, kids!

 

Best Bits

 

When looking around at the beginning (apologies for the clearly dodgy spelling):

Daniel: It’s like we just stepped into the citadel at Maecenea.

Jack: I thought you said it was Greek.

Daniel: Oh, er, Maecenea was an ancient city in the southern Pelopponeus region.

Jack: Where’s that?

Daniel: Greece.

Jack: Why do I do that?

 

After Jack collapses for the first time, we get a glimpse of Forceful!Daniel.  The way he whips off his glasses and says, “What the hell is going on here?” is just amazing!  Be still my heart!
