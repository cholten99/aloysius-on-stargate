Cor-Ai

 

Writer: Tom J Astle

Director: Mario Azzopardi

 

Sam and Daniel seem to struggling in the dialogue department at the beginning of this episode.  Sam gives her ‘pointing out the obvious’ hat a good dusting off with her opening remark about the village.  “Looks deserted.”  Ya think?  Then Daniel objects to Teal’c’s choice of words when he says the Goa’uld come to this planet a lot.

 

Daniel: You know, I wish you wouldn’t say ‘harvest’.  We’re talking about human beings here, not – Brussels sprouts.

 

He pauses slightly before uttering the words ‘Brussels sprouts’ and then immediately looks apologetically at Jack, who winces at his words.  I get the impression neither of them were particularly happy about that line…

 

Then we get classic ‘many weapons all pointing at people’ tension.  Despite previous hostile reactions to Teal’c from natives of one-time Goa’uld occupied planets, nobody predicts that these people aren’t exactly going to be happy to see him.  He’s already told the others that he’s been there before in First Prime capacity, but this didn’t cause undue concern.  Jack does advise “heads up”, but this is for the possibility that the Goa’uld might be about, not worrying about the natives.

 

Daniel’s reaction to the situation is completely typical.  Teal’c’s just been accused of murder and has multiple crossbows aimed at his head, and Daniel is happily analysing the linguistic origins of the native term for the Stargate.

 

There’s a lot of exploration of inter-team relationships in this episode.  Obviously, the main theme is solid support of Teal’c, but there are several layers to this, involving their varying attitudes to one another and how they function as a team.

 

There’s classic disagreement between Jack and Daniel on how to handle the situation.  Jack shows trust in Daniel’s judgement by backing down immediately when he says force is not the solution, but the argument flares up again when Jack later suggests breaking Teal’c out, while Daniel wants to proceed with the trial.

 

The exchange of glances between Sam and Daniel, and Sam’s surprised “You?”, when Jack volunteers to be Teal’c’s ‘voice’ in the trial are great – way to show confidence in your commanding officer, guys!  Jack in fact demonstrates perfectly adequate reasoning abilities during the trial – he just has a tendency to let his anger and frustration get the better of him when one of his team is in danger.

 

Jack and Daniel get to practice their legal teamwork in preparation for Pretence in Season Three – their differing approaches actually work quite well.

 

There’s a great scene between Jack and Teal’c, with mutual acknowledgement that their past careers have been less than spotless.  They really do have a lot in common, and Jack’s understanding of Teal’c’s previous role as First Prime to Apophis is very clear here.  We also get Jack’s total refusal to allow harm to come to one of his team, regardless of how they feel about it.  Nobody gets left behind, no matter what.

 

There’s also brief exploration of the issues surrounding the relationship of Daniel and Teal’c.  We get an acknowledgement from Daniel of how hard it was for him to accept Teal’c as an ally to begin with, making their eventual friendship all the more meaningful and showing what amazing men they both are. 

 

I love the way Daniel bounds down the stairs to the cell, hair flying all over the place, when he’s figured out why Teal’c chose Hanno’s father as his victim.  Daniel often reminds me of an overly exuberant puppy, and this is his spaniel impression at its best.

 

Daniel complains in Need that Jack never gives him any respect, but their conflict over how to handle this situation shows that respect is a two-way street that Daniel has to work at as well.  When Jack decides to head back to Earth for troops, Daniel automatically assumes he’s planning an all-out assault, and Jack’s indignation here is very telling.  He may not always have a lot of time for Daniel’s theories, but Daniel occasionally fails to see him as anything more than a trigger-happy soldier, and Jack is quite right to object to this.

 

Daniel’s passion when arguing for Teal’c’s life in the courtroom is just as strong as that of Jack and Sam trying to persuade Hammond to let them break him out.  Despite the whole Sha’re business, the SG-1 family is incredibly strong at this point.  Daniel has one of his most beautiful moments when he realises that he’s failed to convince Hanno that Teal’c deserves to live.  His expression of defeat and sorrow after he takes his glasses off is just gorgeous.

 

Then we get Teal’c without his shirt on – quite an impressive musculature he has there!

 

The addition of Shak’l as the leader of the Serpent Guards who arrive to ‘harvest’ the non-Brussels sprouts is a nice touch.  It does confuse me, though.  He says that Teal’c’s death will assure his position as First Prime.  But he’s already sporting the gold emblem on his forehead – doesn’t that signify that he already is First Prime?  Maybe he’s just worried about his job security…

 

Obviously, the main point of this episode is to demonstrate once and for all that Teal’c is ‘one of us now’.  We’ve kind of already touched on this at the end of Thor’s Hammer, but the way the other three unite in total support of him here is nice.  They may not always see eye to eye on strategy, they may have difficulty understanding one another’s passions and points of view, but they act as one whenever one of them is in trouble.

 

Best Bits

 

Daniel being totally clueless:

Daniel: You have a very lovely existence.

Bursa woman: [immediately going all coy] Thank you.

The boy just has no idea about the effect he has on women, does he?

 

The final convincing of Hanno:

Hanno: You are not the same man as he who murdered my father.

Teal’c: I am the one.

Hanno: No, that Jaffa is dead. You have killed him.

This sums up the point of the episode rather nicely, I think.
