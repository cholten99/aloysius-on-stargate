Gatecon 2003
Tale of a Gatecon Virgin - Day 4
Oh Stargate, My Stargate

For my stargate-related activity of the day, I ate Fruit Loops for breakfast on Sunday – they are horrible!  Terry ended up finishing my bowl as well as her own and then blamed me for getting her addicted to them.
Corin Nemec had the dubious honour of entertaining us first thing, and it was obvious he was pretty tired.  However, he showed it in such a cute way – the answer to every question turned into a several minute ramble that more often than not ended up on a totally different subject from where he started.  It was very funny and absolutely adorable.  Towards the end, someone asked him if he could remember any good bloopers from his career and he couldn’t think of any.  He asked if he could tell us some life bloopers instead, and proceeded to relate an entire litany of childhood accidents whilst pointing out the accompanying scars.  It was brilliant and he seemed to really enjoy telling the stories.  They involved bikes, steep hills, rotten rope swings, roofs and cliffs – he evidently had quite an adventurous childhood, not much sense and a tendency to fail spectacularly at every stunt he tried!

Then came the most exciting bit of the whole trip – the studio tour!  They had auctioned off 145 tickets over the internet in August and I was away the weekend the auction closed, so I asked my best friend to watch my bid, and he secured me a place with ease and style – what a star!
I had to miss the last three guest panels and the closing ceremony, but it was soooo worth it.  Most of the set was a little bit disappointing, since it’s all small, hollow and just a bit shabby, but the gate itself is absolutely gorgeous.  I just stood and looked at it for over half and hour.  It’s just as huge and magnificent as it looks on the screen.  (When I got home a few days later, I stuck an episode in the DVD player, the first scene was in the gate room, and I yelled, “I’ve been there, I’ve been there!!” – even though there wasn’t anyone else in the room…)
By the time I got back to the hotel, the only thing left to do was get autographs.  I hadn’t originally planned to get any, but my mother had revealed a certain fondness for Kawalsky before I left, so I decided to get Jay’s autograph for her.  I joined the queue and settled in for a long wait.

At one point, one of the volunteers came out and asked if anyone only wanted Dougie’s autograph, since he had nobody in his line (awwww), and nobody said yes (awwwww).  Having become somewhat familiar with Dougie over the previous few days, I turned to the person next to me and said, “I bet you, Dougie will be out here within five minutes.”  And, sure enough, about three minutes later, Dougie was out of the autograph room and working the line!  He’d been calling me Hewie since the skits on Friday night (that’s his affectionate name for Heru’ur) so I got him to sign my programme “To Hewie”.

A bit later on, Colin Cunningham came out and started making calls on the payphone right next to me.  He seemed to be trying unsuccessfully to get someone to come and pick him up and was a bit stressed, so I stepped away a bit and managed not to gawk at him – though there were other people down the line who were taking photos of the poor guy.

Anyway, I queued for four hours and was about three people from the door, when it opened and Jay came out and promptly disappeared off in a taxi!  Thus ended Gatecon for me.

However, all in all, I had a fantastic time, made several great new friends and gained critical acclaim (!) as Heru’ur in the skits.  I am no longer a Gatecon Virgin and I loved every minute of being deflowered!  I’m even planning on going back again next year…
