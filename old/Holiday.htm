Holiday

 

Writer: Tor Alexander Valenza

Directory: David Warry-Smith

 

Now, I have issues with this episode, purely because Machello in Daniel’s body just does absolutely *nothing* for me whatsoever.  Just goes to show what a good actor MS is, I suppose, but it means I don’t like a lot of this ep – it’s just *wrong*, okay???

 

That said, there are some classic moments, so I guess I’ll get on with the review!

 

The opening shot it pretty cool – wormhole whoosh from a new angle, and it certainly makes the entranceway of Machello’s home look impressive.

 

Several people have mentioned to me that they were really surprised to discover that MS played Machello, but it always seemed obvious to me.  Not that I’m trying to suggest that I’m really clever or anything – I’m just saying!  It’s just that when Teal’c says Machello was younger in the pictures he’s seen, I always think he should turn to Daniel and say, “Hey, wait a minute – it wasn’t him, it was you!”

 

Machello’s senile act is very clever – and he picks a good victim for the body switch.  The look exchanged by Jack and Sam when he starts rambling and mixing up his pronouns effectively shows that they’ve dismissed him as a threat.  Of course, Daniel would be much more likely to believe someone trapped in Machello’s body, so Machello shows great perception in his choice.

 

I don’t think I figured out what had happened the first time I saw this, but Daniel is so completely different from the moment they switch, it must have been obvious that something was going on.  But then, this is Stargate – there’s always something going on!

 

When Machello asks Janet how Daniel is as he leaves the infirmary, I don’t see any remorse whatsoever, just calculation – he wants to know how long he’s got to get out before Daniel wakes up, and he seems satisfied with the knowledge that Daniel will die soon.

 

I love the way Sam narrows her eyes slightly when Daniel says he doesn’t recognise the symbols on the electronic pad.  It’s so unlike him just to say ‘no’ like that, without theorising or getting excited about trying to figure them out, and it’s nice that Sam notices.

 

Similarly, Jack shows some surprise when Daniel says Janet told him to go home – the Daniel we know and love would never tell Hammond that when there’s a cool alien script to translate!

 

I like the fact that Jack has to ask Sam if Daniel’s given them the correct address for Cassandra’s home planet.  Jack’s question to ascertain Daniel’s identity is just brilliant, as is Daniel’s response.

 

And then we get Machello wandering around in his stolen body – it doesn’t help matters that even if Daniel was still in possession he’d have trouble looking attractive in that hideous outfit he’s wearing.  However, as much as I don’t like the scene with Machello out and about in the city, I have to say that I am rather fond of Fred.

 

Back in the briefing room, the close-up of Sam after Janet says Daniel’s neurons have been mapped onto Machello’s brain is great – it looks like she’s thinking, “Okay, now that’s a bit far-fetched even for this show!”

 

I’ve always thought it was a shame that Sam is kept out of the whole body-switching thing.  Crichton in Aeryn’s body was absolutely hilarious in Farscape, but I guess it would be going a little too far for Stargate, and wouldn’t really fit in with the dynamic between the characters.

 

This episode contains my mother’s favourite ever Stargate quote – since watching it for the first time, any time anything goes wrong, she automatically says, “It did not go well, General Hammond.”  (Another reason for me not to be overly fond of this ep – the constant quoting can get a little annoying…)

 

I would say that CJ is better at being Jack than RDA is at being Teal’c, but they’re both very funny together, and Sam trying to mediate and figure out how to switch them back is brilliant.

 

Jack needing to figure out how to do kel-no-reem is quite funny, but it doesn’t make much sense.  I know it’s established elsewhere that Teal’c needs to meditate much like humans need to sleep.  However, I don’t think he’s needed to perform kel-no-reem in order for Junior to effect repairs before – and it would be rather inconvenient!

 

Fred’s really sweet when the cops drag Machello off.  It makes me glad he got to eat a fudge sundae, because he seems like a nice guy.  So maybe I’ll forgive Machello for making Daniel’s body undesirable… then again, maybe not…

 

I love the scene where Sam is angsting over Daniel being in a coma – it’s lovely to see her depth of feeling for him, and there’s a really sweet moment between them when he actually wakes up.  This is backed up nicely by Hammond and Sam refusing to give in to Machello’s offer of knowledge for freedom – I just love it when the characters do their loyalty thing.  And Sam’s idea for Machello to get to know Daniel is genius – she knows nobody can resist the Daniel Effect!

 

The scene between Daniel and Machello is great, particularly considering they couldn’t act it out together, since they’re really both the same person!  The wonderful acting skills of MS are showcased here very well.

 

The whole end section is absolutely classic.  MS does a great Jack, and RDA does a great Daniel.  Their game of musical bodies is just so funny, and I bet they had a blast filming it.

 

It’s interesting to see what they each perceive as the over-riding characteristics of the others, too.  MS and CJ both do the close-lipped sarcastic smile as Jack, and CJ does great Jack body language of hands in pockets when he visits Hammond in his office.  RDA does Teal’c’s upright posture and the classic raised eyebrow, and he also nails Daniel’s self-hug very nicely.

 

It was actually reading a Sam/Daniel fic (and a very nice one, at that) which pointed out to me that Sam has her hand on Daniel’s back in the final shot, which is really sweet.  It also goes to show that I should be more open-minded about het fanfiction – this is certainly one of the best episodes for advocating the Sam/Daniel relationship, not that I do…

 

This episode contains some of the funniest moments of all five seasons, but still doesn’t rank among my favourites, because I just don’t like the idea of someone else in Daniel’s body.  I wasn’t even overly keen on meeting MS in real life, simply because he’s so completely different to Daniel, so Machello certainly doesn’t do it for me!!  Just call me crazy, I don’t mind…

 

Characteristic Moments

 

We get a fun variation of one of Jack’s catchphrases when they return from Machello’s planet with the machine:

General Hammond: How did it go, Colonel?

Teal’c in Jack’s body: It did not go well, General Hammond.

Jack in Teal’c’s body: Ya think?

 

CJ gets to utter another Jack catchphrase when he wakes up from kel-no-reem:

Jack in Teal’c’s body: Whoa!  Sweet.

 

Then MS gets a turn when Jack switches with Machello at the end:

Sam: Colonel?

Jack in Daniel’s body: I’m here.

Sam: How do you feel, physically?

Jack in Daniel’s body: Peachy.

 

Best Bits

 

Jack’s tactics when trying to ascertain Daniel’s identity:

Jack: Okay, describe for me the dress your sister wore last week when I took her out.

Daniel in Machello’s body: I don’t have a sister, Jack – and if I did, I wouldn’t let you near her.

Cut to Sam trying desperately not to laugh.
