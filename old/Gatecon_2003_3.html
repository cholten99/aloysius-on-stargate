Gatecon 2003
Tale of a Gatecon Virgin - Day 3
Silliness, Surprises and Stockings

Saturday morning started off with Jay Acavone, JR Bourne (Martouf), Alex Zahara (Michael in 1969/Zailes in Spirits) and Frida Betrani (Lya).  I had been looking forward to seeing JR Bourne and I wasn’t disappointed.  He’s even more gorgeous in real life than as Martouf and his smile is just as bright.  Unfortunately, he claimed his hair was messy and so refused to take of his hat, but he was still lovely.  This was Alex’s first convention and he was having lots of fun, despite dire warnings from the other guests.  The best moment came from a question asked of Colin the day before – someone asked him what questions he’s like us to ask Jay and JR, and he said we should ask them both about the twinkie story.  So, this morning, someone did, and their confused response was really funny.  Alex made several suggestive comments about why they could be claiming ignorance, and much cursing of Colin ensued.
Next up was the adorable combination of Teryl Rothery (Dr Janet Fraiser) and Don S Davis (General George Hammond), who seem to be very good friends and are obviously very fond of one another.  Don had just got married the month before and Teryl had been instrumental in getting he and his wife together.  That was mostly what they talked about and it was all very sweet, but Don also came out with one of my favourite quotes of the weekend: “I’m from Ozarks, where the men are men and the sheep are scared.”

Then came the comic double act of Colin Cunningham and Corin Nemec (Jonas Quinn).  Corin (who has the most adorable smile I’ve seen in a long time) said he thought the mini stargate on the stage was really cute.  Then, in a leap of surreality, he said, “They should make Stargate toilet seat covers,” which prompted Colin to mime flushing a toilet while calling out, “Unscheduled offworld activation!”

There was much discussion of Jonas’ banana – which he apparently produces randomly out of his jacket in one of the episodes.  At one point, someone asked which of Jonas and Major Davis would have most chance with Sam, and Colin suggested they could have a threesome.  He said, “Davis could bring his briefcase and Jonas could put his banana in it.”  Now, there doesn’t sound like much room for Sam in that scenario, if you ask me!
The System Lords were up next and actually got a set change, giving them Goa’uld thrones to sit on.  There was Jacqueline Samuda (Nirrti), David Palffy (Sokar/Anubis), Dougie Arthurs (Heru’ur) and Peter Williams (Apophis) – David was the new boy as far as conventions go, but Dougie pointed out that he was also the only one whose character was still alive on the show.  Jacqueline was very smiley and gracious, Peter was very cool and pretty funny, while Dougie seemed remarkably subdued and also rather embarrassed by whatever had happened at the auction the night before – I don’t know what it was, but the after-effects were very entertaining to see.

Then came the big surprise of the weekend – there was an undesignated guest spot on the Saturday afternoon and who should turn up to fill it but Michael Shanks!  He looked really, really good, and was the most relaxed and happy I’ve ever seen him.  He’s obviously comfortably at home in Vancouver, since this was also the first time I’ve heard him sound noticeably Canadian.  Up until this point, I had been joining Terry in cynical heckling of the groupie mentality, but the second Michael stepped on stage I turned into an instant puddle of goo and grinned inanely for the full hour.  I don’t know what it is about this man that has such an effect on me, but it’s really good fun!

He did his rendition of the ‘puff and ruffle’ (what the regular male cast members do when a beautiful female guest star comes on set) and the ‘wounded lamb’ (the regular female members’ reaction to the puff and ruffle).  He showed us both his underwear and his appendix scar and revealed a theory that Daniel Jackson is really a night-time ninja who fights crime in Colorado Springs when everyone thinks he’s working late at the mountain.

However, the best thing he said is that he thinks the vision of Daniel in light loafers and the white sweater in the episode Abyss is actually Jack’s fantasy version of Daniel!  (And the slashers went wild…)  He also talked about his lack of comprehension of the phenomenon of Daniel-whumping in fanfiction, and the actual insertion of Daniel/Janet ship into episodes by himself and Teryl.
