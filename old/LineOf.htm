In The Line Of Duty

 

Writer: Robert C Cooper

Director: Martin Wood

 

Action-packed opening – lots of cannon blasts, death gliders screaming overhead, people flying all over the place – and Sam being invaded by a Goa’uld!  This is another episode where the impact is rather lessened by having seen it before, and I can’t remember what I thought the first time I saw it, but it’s close enough to The Enemy Within that we still don’t know that much about the Goa’uld/host relationship – so the idea of a member of SG-1 being infected is a pretty shocking one.

 

When the scene switches to the gate room, it looks like one of the medical staff actually shoves Hammond out of the way.  I guess this is one occasion when this would be forgivable though!

 

This is the first instance of the “entry through the mouth” technique of Goa’uld symbiotes.  It makes the Goa’uld that much scarier, even as the Tok’ra are introduced as a counterpoint.  The threat is all the greater because they can infiltrate a host without detection.

 

Having this scenario as the introduction of the Tok’ra is an interesting one.  It goes a long way towards explaining Jack’s mistrust of them, even without the bad association of the Goa’uld themselves.  Teal’c has already shown us that bad guys can switch sides, so we should be prepared for the possibility of ‘good’ Goa’uld, but Jolinar doesn’t do much to present herself as a warm and fuzzy friendly symbiote, does she?  She’s in a tough position and has no reason to trust the Tau’ri, but the first impression we get of the Tok’ra isn’t a particularly good one.

 

I love the little eyebrow raise Jack does when Sam starts spouting tactical philosophy in the briefing.  It’s definitely a “weird, but hey, it’s Carter!” expression.  The issue of symbiote control is a little ambiguous at this point – is Jolinar fully in control and pretending to be Carter, or is she just influencing Sam a bit?  The interaction between host and symbiote within the Tok’ra is never fully explained.

 

Who are the two random guys sitting next to Teal’c and what are they doing in SG-1’s debriefing?  The director presumably wanted Sam, Daniel and Jack all on one side of the table for some reason so brought in extras to balance out the numbers, but it is a bit odd, specially considering they don’t say anything?  But then, what would they have to say?  They weren’t on the planet!

 

Another question – why does Janet have an office at the hospital?  Isn’t she Chief Medical Officer at the SGC yet?  Does she moonlight outside the mountain?  Well, obviously she does, because she treats the Nasian patients.

 

It seems odd that Cassie chooses Jack to confide in.  She didn’t spend that much time with him in Singularity, because he stayed on the planet to monitor the black hole.  I would have thought Daniel would be her next choice after Sam, based on that ep, but I guess it’s likely Jack has spent more time with her since then.  He’s also more authoritative and more the kind of person to turn to when there’s a Goa’uld on the loose!  He is great with her, as well.  I love the way he slides down the wall to sit on the floor next to her, and the way she scoots across to be closer to him – very cute!

 

There’s a great shot of Jack with Daniel and Teal’c flanking him as he faces off against Jolinar in the gate room.  The expressions of the three guys as they realise Sam’s been invaded are wonderful.  It must be their worst nightmare – and one Jack and Daniel have already suffered with people they love.

 

It’s actually a pretty good premise for an episode – up to this point, it looks like a repeat of The Enemy Within but then it spins off in a totally different direction.  As far as we know at this stage, there’s no way to save Sam, and the way in which the situation is resolved is an interesting one.

 

There’s great Jack angst in this ep, as well as a very nice moment between Jack and Teal’c when Teal’c goes to check on how Jack is reacting.  He shows his understanding of Jack’s pain, but makes sure he accepts the reality of the situation and warns him not to let his emotions cloud his judgement.  Teal’c is a very good friend here – supportive but practical.

 

Jolinar obviously doesn’t like having big hair – as soon as she’s been revealed and doesn’t have to pretend to be Sam any more, her hair goes totally flat!  Wonder where she got the blow drier from…?

 

Ruminations: if the symbiote has access to all the host’s knowledge, why doesn’t Jolinar see that she can be allies with the humans?  Can’t she talk to Sam and sort stuff out with her?  I guess Sam wouldn’t exactly be open to conversations with a symbiote – automatic prejudice against being made a host probably gets in the way.  Jolinar is in fear of her life as well, so she’s probably not in the best position to conduct rational introductions either.

 

Nobody is safe from Daniel’s charms! (And who would want to be?)  Even when he’s telling a Nasian woman that her husband was a Goa’uld, she can’t resist him!  When she tells him about the scar on her husband’s neck, she shows him where it was by reaching out and stroking his neck, when most people would probably demonstrate on their own body.  Any excuse to get her hands on Daniel!

 

Another thought – Daniel goes outside the mountain with the SG1 designation still on his uniform.  He’s obviously still in a secure, military location, but they normally remove their patches when they venture out into the world.

 

The Goa’uld assassin guy is obviously really dedicated to his job – that weapon he regurgitates looks like it must have hurt when he swallowed it – it’s huge!  And pointy!!

 

I’ve always wondered if the extra time gained by fooling the hospital staff into thinking he’s still in the bed is worth the risk of being caught while painstakingly wrapping the unconscious doctor in the bandages.  And how does he know how to hook up all the machines correctly?

 

Again, we get ambiguity as to the control of Jolinar over Sam.  When Sam apparently pleads with Jack to believe Jolinar – did she really relinquish control or is she just pretending?  I wouldn’t have thought a Tok’ra would pretend like that, but she does call him Jack, which Sam wouldn’t do.  It’s interesting, though – if it is Jolinar pretending, she must have taken Jack as the most likely thing for Sam to call him, which means Sam must think of him as ‘Jack’ rather than ‘Colonel O’Neill’ in her mind…  Shippiness??? Aargh!

 

And a big EWWWWWW with regard to the assassin’s scanny thing – the way it reveals the skull underneath the victim’s skin really creeps me out!

 

Janet trying to run down the corridor in her giant high heels is always amusing!

 

Then we get an adorable bit when Daniel apologises to Sam when he won’t agree to let Jolinar go – he is such a sweetheart!

 

I have more issues with the assassin’s infiltration plan.  He allows his palm to be scanned on entry to the mountain, thus setting off an alarm at the checkpoint.  He zaps the guard, but wouldn’t that alarm register somewhere else in the mountain and be investigated?

 

Daniel makes a very cute hostage!  He has this great ‘oh, here we go again’ expression on his face when the assassin grabs him – he’s obviously getting used to being whumped by this stage!  Teal’c’s reaction to the crisis is great too – doesn’t waste any time, though he does apologise to Daniel before zatting him!

 

As with lots of the episodes, I’ve always thought the ending of this one is a little abrupt.  We find out a lot more about Jolinar and the Tok’ra later in the series, admittedly, but it might have been nice to have more about Sam’s perspective at this point.  I guess TPTB want us confused and intrigued at this point – Goa’uld symbiotes can be good?  Jolinar didn’t seem all that nice, but she gave her life to save Sam?  Hmmmm…. Interesting….

 

In my opinion, the whole Tok’ra thing really picks up with the introduction of Martouf – cos he’s lovely!!

Great word!
