Point Of View

 

 

Writer: Jonathan Glassner and Brad Wright

Director: Peter DeLuise

 

Starting in Area 51 certainly makes a change, and the mysterious music does it’s trick.  It’s interesting to see all the junk they’ve collected over the years, too.  Of course, the real surprise comes in seeing the alternate versions of Sam and Kawalsky turn up.  It’s always good to have Kawalsky back for a guest spot.  Sam, though, is much better off with short hair in my opinion – and, even a civilian consultant, I can’t see her wearing a pastel twin-set in any universe!

 

Jack’s looking good again – maroon jumper and leather jacket combo is very nice!

 

And so the apparent shippiness begins, with AltSam making goo-goo eyes at a very confused Jack.  However, surely it’s not ship unless it’s our Jack and our Sam, and we shall see as the episode plays out whether this scenario affects how they act towards each other.

 

Jack: Is he talking about that alternate reality thing Daniel futzed with on 233?

 

Way to talk about the events that led to Daniel saving the planet!!  And how does Jack remember a planet designation from 18 months ago?  Is his usual inability to remember them part of his dumb façade?

 

Sam’s totally dumbfounded expression while they watch the tape of AltSam’s interrogation is great – I’ve never seen her look so idiotic, though I guess they are mitigating circumstances!

 

I love the way Jack rolls his eyes and then they glaze over when AltSam starts talking about Quantum Theory.  It’s as if he’s thinking “The hair may be different, but the stuff underneath it is totally the same!”  A clear indication that he’s not interested in Sam, in my opinion…

 

Daniel has a similar moment when Jack asks if there’s possibly an alternate him who understands this stuff – Daniel’s expression clearly says “Don’t think so!”

 

I love the fact that the two Sams fight – the differences between them probably throw in their faces things that they’re uncomfortable about themselves – our Sam’s possibly self-perceived lack of femininity, AltSam’s insecurity about not being tough enough perhaps?  It’s realistic that they’d be defensive and antagonistic around each other.

 

Seeing a wedding photo of Jack and Sam must be every shipper’s dream, and it is very sweet.  I certainly have a great deal of sympathy for AltSam – it must be really awful to have what seems to be the person you love returned to you, only to find out they don’t love you back.  However, I do like the fact that the last thing AltSam says in the scene between her and Jack is “I miss him” – at least she’s managing to keep the realities separate, which must be hard under the circumstances.

 

I’m not going to go into any huge discussions on shippiness, since I know I’m not going to be able to persuade anyone whose mind is already made up, and I don’t think that’s the right attitude to have anyway.  I’m quite sure the shippers can see Jack thinking about Sam in all sorts of new and interesting ways when they meet in the corridor after he leaves AltSam.  I personally choose to see him thinking “Me and Sam?  Well, I do think she’s great as a friend, and she’s certainly a valuable member of the team, but that’s just too damned weird – not a chance!”  I firmly believe you can see the inter-relationships of the characters however you like, and I also firmly believe that people should be allowed to interpret them however they choose.  Me a cowardly wimp?  Maybe – but it makes life a whole hell of a lot more peaceful!

 

You’ve got to give Daniel credit for thinking big!  Presented with the problem, his immediate thought is to save the entirety of the other reality.  He obviously likes the role of intergalactic hero!

 

Sam says she had enough trouble figuring out how to make the Asgard generator work the first time – but isn’t it the Ancients’ generator from Fifth Race, and didn’t Jack make it work the first time?  As I remember it, Sam wasn’t even on Earth at the time…

 

I love Kawalsky explaining the quantum mirror to Daniel – it’s a fantastic role reversal from the Jack-like Kawalsky and lecture-giving Daniel we know and love.

 

Despite my speech about avoiding the ship issue, I will just say this – Jack’s frustration with the way the two Sams equal twice the technobabble really doesn’t suggest burgeoning desire for either one of them to me…  :o)

 

Obviously AltSam’s home is a beardy reality – AltApophis and AltTeal’c both look remarkably silly with their bizarre facial hair.

 

The fact that AltTeal’c sends all his Jaffa off in the opposite direction and follows the noise of Jack and Kawalsky on his own is just a smidgen unrealistic, don’t you think?  It makes him completely vulnerable to attack, and really isn’t very good tactical sense for a planet-conquering First Prime.

 

I love the way Teal’c offers to spare AltTeal’c if he renounces his allegiance to AltApophis, and then just shoots him point blank when he doesn’t go for it – it’s a fantastic moment and says a lot about Teal’c’s character.  The look exchanged between Jack and Daniel afterwards is great too – you can see them thinking they’re awfully glad Teal’c’s on their side!  They haven’t seen him being ruthless for a while.

 

There’s a very cute moment when Daniel turns on the mirror and tells Jack the reality has shifted.  Jack tells him it’s okay and pats him on the shoulder – oodles of support and faith in that one little moment – wonderful stuff!

 

As Jack climbs through the hatch and up the stairs, something sticking out of his pack catches at the top of the door just before the scene cuts.  I can just imagine him getting yanked back down and landing in an undignified heap on the floor – it’s a good job the Jaffa are too busy watching AltApophis torturing AltHammond to hear the thud!

 

This is our first view of Goa’uld pain sticks, I believe – truly lovely things, that our guys still have to look forward to in later episodes.

 

I love Daniel reality-surfing with the mirror.  It must be a bit like trying to tune in a new TV – only with a bit more pressure involved.  Similarly frustrating, though!  I always wonder what the other AltSam must think when Daniel waves at her through the mirror and then disappears again.  This whole quantum mirror/countless realities is so complicated!

 

Has AltSam ever been through the gate before?  Judging from the outfit she’s wearing for the conquering of her world by Apophis, it doesn’t seem like she’s attached to an SG team, or she’d be wearing fatigues like Daniel does.  I figure she must be a stay-at-home consultant.  Also, the way she jumps into the wormhole rather than simply running through suggests a lack of expertise in that area.

 

The shot of Daniel on his knees surrounded by Jaffa is wonderful.  Poor little honey pie looks so upset to have failed Jack, but the cut on his cheek attests to the fact that he didn’t surrender quietly.

 

I love the way Jack passes the buck to Daniel when AltApophis demands an explanation for them being there.  And Daniel fighting through the absurdity of explaining quantum mechanics to a Goa’uld is very funny – up until AltHammond gets killed, of course!

 

Jack’s snake-baiting is usually an attempt to deflect attention away from his team, but here it results in Daniel getting zatted, which is a bit of a shame.  What he says – that Apophis is dead in our reality – isn’t actually true, though of course he doesn’t know that at this point.

 

And woohoo!  Deus ex machina by the Asgard yet again – they really are very useful little guys, aren’t they?  Even AltAsgard are really cool!  Obviously quite shy, though, since none of them come down to introduce themselves.

 

Kawalsky saluting Teal’c at the end is a nice touch – the message of the episode being, of course, children, that we should be tolerant of those who are different from us and not prejudge people by making rash generalisations.

 

We end on an anti-ship note, with AltSam coming out with the line “You’re really not him, are you?”  This just after kissing him – no chemistry, see? (Sorry!)  And our Sam doesn’t look jealous so much as uncomfortable – she literally squirms at the sight of effectively herself kissing Jack.  Oops!  Wasn’t going to do that, was I?  :o)

 

It is very sad for AltSam, though, and I do remember crying the first time I saw this episode – but then, I was a semi-shipper myself back then.  Wow – don’t times change!

 

 

Characteristic Moments

 

Jack, or rather AltJack, makes another conquest in his mission to spread his catchphrases across the universe:

Interrogator: Well, let’s go through it again!

AltSam: Oh, for crying out loud.

 

 

Best Bits

 

Jack’s mind isn’t quite on the job at hand, but Daniel’s happy to give a lecture any place, any time:

Jack: Alright, I’ve gotta know.

Daniel: Yes, I’m about to activate it.

Jack: No, no, no – not that.  What the hell does “Kree” mean?

Daniel: Well, actually, it means a lot of things.  Loosely translated, it means – attention, listen up, concentrate…

Jack: Yoo-hoo?

Daniel: Yes, in a manner of speaking…

Jack: Huh.

 

AltApophis learns just how annoying Jack can be:

AltApophis: My First Prime killed you before my very eyes.

Jack: I’m feeling much better – thank you.
