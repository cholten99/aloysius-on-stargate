Prisoners

 

Writer: Terry Curtis Fox

Director: David Warry-Smith

 

This ep starts off with some classic banter between Jack and Daniel, gentle conflict that speaks more of tolerated differences between friends than deep-seated disagreement.  By this stage, Daniel has resigned himself to Jack’s lack of exploratory enthusiasm, only protesting up to a point before giving way.

 

I’m guessing Jack wishes the planet really was as boring as it first seems, once they are detained.  Right from the moment of transportation, though, he demonstrates how good a leader he is – his first words in the sentencing chamber are “Everybody alright?”  He asks Sam the same thing after she is attacked on arrival on Hadante, and reassures Daniel that everything will be okay.  Throughout the episode, he continually checks on his team and keeps them calm and focused on the escape plan.

 

The Taldor are very skilled at intimidation – the spotlight, surrounding darkness, disembodied voices – all very effective and unsettling.  Jack actually does pretty well in the role of spokesman, but it’s impossible to argue with the Taldor since their decision is immutable.  It seems odd that Jack is the one to talk to them, even having to prompt Daniel into joining in the conversation.  Daniel seems a lot quieter and more withdrawn than usual, showing how unsettling the experience is for him.

 

Daniel’s innocence is demonstrated by his reaction to the smoking feet – “Why would anyone do that?”  He can’t imagine an existence where dying in the kawoosh effect would be preferable to going on – this is an attitude that doesn’t last much longer.  The Daniel of Season 5 would react to Hadante a great deal differently, I feel.

 

Again, it’s normally Daniel’s job to do the meet-and-greet on a new planet, but here it’s Sam who approaches the first prisoner they see and tries to talk to him.  Daniel stays well back behind Jack, clearly very uncertain and rather unnerved by what has happened to them.  Once presented with a seemingly rational authority figure in the form of Linnea, though, he regains his balance and steps up to do the usual introductions.

 

I’ve always thought Linnea is one of the strongest minor characters in the show.  Her initial ambiguity, obvious intelligence and the revelation of her true nature make her a formidable opponent, actually succeeding in defeating the entire SGC.

 

We get a little bit of Sam characterisation, harking back to Children of the Gods and Emancipation with her assertion that she can take care of herself.  She has learned to accept the protection of the rest of her team, as an equal member, but doesn’t want an old woman suggesting that she needs special treatment.

 

Then comes a great demonstration of the relationship between Jack and Daniel, as well as their reaction to the crisis:

 

Jack: Okay, kids, this is our situation.  What do we need?

Daniel: Well, a Dial Home Device would be nice.

Jack: Okay, let’s assume we don’t find one of those.

 

Daniel’s nervousness manifests itself in the inappropriate sarcasm we usually expect from Jack, while Jack resists the urge to indulge in more of the same, instead replying in a soft, reassuring tone that shows understanding and tolerance of Daniel’s fear.  Right after this comes one of my favourite bits in any episode – Daniel babbling about Botany Bay and Jack patting him on the arm, saying, “It’s okay, Daniel, it’s okay.”  This is so sweet!

 

Daniel fluctuates between random babbling and meek reserve in this ep, demonstrating two different reactions to the situation.  After the weasly prisoner offers Sam the necklace, Daniel just stands there with his mouth open and then nods silently before wandering off in accordance with Jack’s instructions.  Later, he goes back to waffling, telling them his thoughts about the structure of the prison when they are clearly of no use at all.

 

In contrast to Jack’s solicitous concern for his team, he shows a much more callous side when the prisoner they met on the Taldor planet is being chased.  He shoves him roughly away and abruptly declares, “Not our problem.”

 

The discussion with Linnea covers a lot of interesting ground – philosophy, politics, science.  It also gives us more characterisation of Sam, in that her enthusiasm for the organic cold fusion briefly supersedes the immediate predicament and Jack has to pull her back to the matter at hand.  He shows pride in her abilities, though, when he tells Linnea that she figured out the Stargate system.  It’s not that he doesn’t respect and admire her intelligence, it’s just that sometimes she gets carried away with the moment when she ought to be concentrating on something else.

 

Then comes the scene where Teal’c has one of the other prisoners up against the wall, while Daniel stands in the background looking very uncomfortable.  Shameless plug alert: If you want my take on what happened here, you can read my story “A Simple Mistake”, archived at Heliopolis and The Alpha Gate (link to Alpha archive on site home page).

 

Once again, when Daniel does his pointless speech about the prison being an ancient alien gate terminal, Jack refrains from cutting him off, instead simply cocking his head to one side and raising one eyebrow.  He’s really very tolerant in this episode.

 

I love the shift in Daniel’s expression when he realises that Jack actually has been in prison before.  And Jack’s response of “Oh yeah” speaks volumes as to his previous experience of prison.  This adds an extra dimension to Jack’s character, hinting at greater depth in his background, which is explored further in A Matter Of Time.

 

All the stuff back at the SGC is interesting, too.  We are introduced to two more SG team commanders, showing that SG1 don’t operate in a vacuum and giving a more solid feel to the SGC.  There’s also some great characterisation of General Hammond, as well as his first trip through the Stargate!

 

The way in which Linnea describes her crime is particularly cunning.  She doesn’t actually lie, even going so far as to admit her guilt, but she presents it in such a way as to capture Sam’s sympathy.  She really is a very clever woman.

 

Daniel’s usual compassion and tendency to interfere finally come shining through when he realises that the prisoners think being disintegrated in the kawoosh is an escape route.  He’s obviously been mingling in the interim, as well, since he knows the name of at least one of the other prisoners.  His anger at their deaths is so strong that he even tries to stand up to Vishnor – not a smart move, but typical Daniel!

 

Question:  What has Teal’c been doing all this time?  Why isn’t he with the others when they get attacked?  Seems odd that he would have wandered off on his own in such an environment.

 

Another question: How did Linnea smuggle her zappy device onto Hadante?  (Although I shouldn’t really quibble about this, since I rely on the ability to smuggle stuff past the Taldor in order to make my Prisoners-based story work!)

 

The way Sam kneels down and strokes Daniel’s hair after he’s been strangled is really cute.  We also get another cute family-type moment when he regains consciousness, with Sam and Jack surrounding him and checking that he’s okay.  Teal’c isn’t a part of it though, and in fact seems rather separate from the others throughout this ep, which is a shame.

 

The scene where Daniel organises the food is great, particularly his and Jack’s expressions.

 

This is a pretty well balanced episode – all of them contribute something.  Sam figures out how to power the gate, Daniel provides them with a destination, Teal’c is strong enough to dial manually, and Jack holds the whole team together.  It’s a great team ep.

 

The roots are a bit dodgy – they appear to be bits of fluff wrapped around wire, as shown when the fluff falls off as Sam is tying it round the gate!

 

It would be a perfectly good episode if it merely consisted of SG1 being sent to the prison planet and figuring out how to escape, but the whole extra section about Linnea’s background and departure from the SGC adds another dimension to the plot.  It also allows future exploration of the character in Past and Present.

 

When they arrive back on Earth, Jack manages to get out of the visit to the infirmary.  General Hammond orders them all to report there, but Jack hangs back and then slopes off in the opposite direction!

 

Then of course we get the revelation that Linnea is the Destroyer of Worlds, and the end of the ep, with Sam saying they let her out, is really quite chilling.  It’s interesting to have them do something wrong and not be able to rectify it within the same episode – makes a change from their usual amazing heroism.  They dismissed the possibility of her being dangerous because it was easier for them to believe she was as undeserving of imprisonment as they were.  They deceive themselves as to her true nature in order to justify accepting her help to escape the prison.

 

This is definitely my favourite episode of Season Two, and probably my favourite episode full stop.  It encompasses everything I love about Stargate and shows SG1 as a cohesive and bonded team.

 

Characteristic Moments

 

Jack is definitely in parent mode in this episode:

Jack: Alright, kids, we’re due back!

 

Jack: Okay, kids, this is our situation.  What do we need?

 

The Jack interruption strikes again:

Jack: Captain?  Any sign of radio traffic in the last 24 hours?  Any chemical traces in the air to indicate civilisation?

Sam: No, sir.

(Daniel starts to protest.)

Jack: Ah! (raises the accompanying finger) Ah! (Double whammy needed to shut Daniel up!)

 

Gratuitous use of catchphrase when the gate activates to disgorge another prisoner:

Jack: For crying out loud…

 

Best Bits

 

Typical Daniel and Jack difference of opinion:

Daniel: We have no idea what this planet has to offer.

Jack: Trees and moss.

 

If in doubt, hide behind the Jaffa:

Jack: Teal’c, look scary and take point.

 

Typically Jack-style informal debriefing:

Jack: Course, that’s about the time Daniel picked a fight with a big, smelly guy – he actually won!

Hammond looks just as sceptical at this prospect as do Sam and Teal’c when Linnea suggests it back on Hadante – poor Daniel!
