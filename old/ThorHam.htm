Thor’s Hammer

 

Writer: Katharyn Powers

Director: Brad Turner

 

Opens on Daniel being a clever bunny with his “there must be good aliens too” theory.  He starts off rather hesitantly and Jack is clearly less than impressed by Daniel in lecture mode.  Jack directly several unsupportive expressions at General Hammond as if suggesting that Daniel should be humoured even though he’s clearly talking rubbish.  Watching this briefing, it’s easy to see where the “lack of respect” comment comes from in Need.  Poor Daniel – even Sam starts to look sceptical when he mentions the Vikings.  They rarely accept Daniel’s ideas straight off, even though he is proved right time and time again.  The “good aliens” theory is only taken seriously when Teal’c backs it up with knowledge of the hammer symbol.

 

However, we then get a rare and wonderful sight – Daniel actually smiling!  His enthusiasm for the box that has been made for friendly aliens is adorable!

 

The monument in front of the gate on Cimmeria is very cool – I really like the carvings depicting a snake entwined in a lightning bolt.  It’s very appropriate and beautifully done in the Norse style.

 

This episode is the introduction of Daniel and his boonie hat, which actually made its series debut on Jack’s head.  I’ve never been a fan of this hat, but I know there are boonie girls out there, so this must be a good episode for them!  Sam gets a boonie hat here too, and it looks better on her than on either of the guys – as does the helmet.  She’s obviously just a person who suits hats!  Jack looks best in the baseball cap that quickly becomes his most frequent headgear, while Daniel of course looks best as Bandanna Boy!

 

The way Sam takes command when Jack and Teal’c disappear is very impressive.  She is strong, practical and refuses to give in to anxiety about the fate of their team-mates.  She even gets to do the introductions for a change, as Daniel is clearly very strongly affected by the loss of Jack and Teal’c.

 

Yet more great minor characters – Gerowyn and Kendra are both well-drawn and interesting.  I particularly like the way Kendra falls back on superstition and belief in magic when she is uncertain.  Her characterisation is excellently done – the portrayal of someone who has been freed from being a Goa’uld host is convincing and sympathetic.

 

I love the way the projection of Thor shakes his head before he starts talking, as if to say, “Silly Goa’uld!  You knew you weren’t supposed to come here and now look what’s happened to you!  Tsk, tsk!”

 

It’s always good when the team gets split up.  When they are separated is when the bonds between them really show.  Sam and Daniel are resolute in their determination to find the other two, while Jack demonstrates true loyalty by refusing to abandon Teal’c, who of course plays the self-sacrifice card by offering to remain in the labyrinth while Jack escapes.

 

This episode sets up a lot of things that are developed later on – the Unas, of course, but also the Asgard.  In particular, it highlights that the Asgard are “too advanced” to think about projectile weapons, which is nicely followed up with the introduction of their battle against the Replicators.

 

It seems fitting that the first Unas we meet has the voice of Darth Vader!  The reaction shot of Teal’c when the Unas first appears clearly demonstrates that childhood nightmares can reduce even the strongest of men to a quivering wreck.  I don’t think anyone can have been surprised to find out that the big scary monster isn’t as easy to kill as it first seems – the age-old tradition of regenerating enemies comes into play with the eye glow – not exactly a big shocker!

 

The scene where Daniel talks to Kendra about Sha’re is very sweet.  She shows great empathy and kindness in her assurances that there is hope, and the reminder of Daniel’s mission sets up what happens at the end very well.

 

Something that really doesn’t happen very often is a fight between Sam and Daniel, which we get part way through the journey to the labyrinth exit.  Interestingly, Daniel shows tremendous restraint, throwing up his hand and turning away when his feelings get the better of him.  Clearly, he doesn’t want to argue with Sam, something that is never a problem when it’s Jack he’s fighting with!

 

We get classic Daniel compassion at the cave entrance, telling Kendra she doesn’t have to go in with them.  Sam, however, looks less than impressed by this show of understanding – she’s really irritable in this episode!

 

I always thought it was rather convenient the way they all arrive at the Hammer at the same time – but hey, I guess I’ll let it go…

 

It seems rather harsh that Jack makes Daniel shoot the Hammer and so destroy the first real glimmer of hope he’s found for saving Sha’re.  I suppose Jack feels Daniel needs to make the decision to save Teal’c and making Daniel destroy the Hammer is the only way he can be sure Daniel accepts what they have to do.  But still – rather mean in my opinion.

 

Altogether, a great team bonding episode.  Lots of loyalty and dedication and family spirit.

 

Characteristic Moments

 

The Jack noise that it is impossible to represent in text – but you know the one I mean!

Teal’c: I no longer serve the Goa’uld.

Unas: We shall see.

Jack: Ah! (with the obligatory finger in the face of course!) Just stay put right there!

 

The Jack catchphrase – so many occasions, four much-repeated words!

Teal’c starts jumping at shadows in the labyrinth:

Jack: Teal’c will you relax?  That thing’s dead, for crying out loud!

 

Classic O’Neill sarcasm in the face of an enemy:

Unas: I know the secrets of the labyrinth.  I could help you escape…

Jack: A map’d be nice!

 

Teal’c’s failure to grasp human idiom and Jack’s reference to clichés :

Teal’c: Are you considering the same tactic as I?

Jack: Teal’c, the cliché is “are you thinking what I’m thinking” – and the answer’s yes.

 

Best Bits

 

Fantastic introduction of new character – sums up Gerowyn’s personality in one phrase.

On laying eyes on Sam and Daniel for the first time:

Gerowyn: You’re a little short for gods!

 

The Jack/Teal’c double act – two macho men trying to reassure one another:

Teal’c: It was dead… I believe.

Jack: You believe?

Teal’c: I am certain.

Jack: Positive.

Teal’c: I am.

Jack: Just a myth.

Teal’c: A myth.

Jack: Good.

 

The scene at Thor’s Hammer is wonderful – some great moments between Jack, Daniel and Teal’c.  Jack forcing Daniel to see that they have to destroy the Hammer to save Teal’c, but clearly appreciating what it’s costing Daniel.  Teal’c expressing his guilt at being in the service of Apophis when Sha’re and Skaara were taken, and offering to remain in the labyrinth rather than have Daniel lose the hope of saving his wife.  Daniel torn between hope for Sha’re and desire to save his friend.  All culminates in a great sentiment expressed by Jack:

Jack: You’re part of this family now.  We’re not leaving you behind.

This scene has some fantastic acting – wonderful expressions, very subtle but very powerful.
