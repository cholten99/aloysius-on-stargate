Show and Tell

 

 

Writer: Jonathan Glassner

Director: Peter DeLuise

 

A classic unauthorised traveller opening – I’ve used it many a time in fanfic myself.  There’s tense music, General Hammond ordering the iris closed, the poor technician unable to obey because the computer isn’t working – it could be the end of the world as we know it, and what do we get?  Pyjama bottoms with furry feet!  The picture scans up to include a wide leather hood – and I’d swear the unauthorised traveller is an Ewok!!

 

Sam’s attitude is a little worrying – the kid has somehow disabled the iris and come through the gate unnanounced, but she automatically assumes he’s friendly and helpless just because he’s a little kid.  The Goa’uld have used children to endanger Earth before – twice – does she not remember Cassie and Ry’ac?

 

I’m always annoyed when the kids answers Jack’s question of “What’s he doing here?”  I’m just waiting for Sam to respond, “Well, actually, sir, I studied telepathy during my time at the Pentagon, and…”

 

Jack once again demonstrates how great he is with kids in this episode.  He automatically plays along with the invisible mother, but talks to the kid as an equal.  He doesn’t even bat an eyelid when the kid asks if he can be called Charlie, though I guess it’s not too uncommon on this show!

 

On a side note, Daniel’s looking blonder than ever, and his hair’s a little more bouffant than usual, too.  Jack’s also now completely grey, and looks all the better for it.

 

Despite my dislike of children in general, I’ve liked most of the kids on the show so far.  Charlie3 unfortunately falls into the same category as Ry’ac – I don’t find him interesting as a character and he actually annoys me to a certain extent.

 

Jacob, on the other hand, is very cool.  I love his entrance in this ep, joking that they only want to see Selmac.  I also like the way Sam winces slightly when Selmac takes control – it’s still early and she hasn’t got used to it yet.

 

Jack deals with the kid wanting to be called Charlie and become his son extremely well, though you can see that it’s getting to him.  This is a tough episode for Jack, but there isn’t much exploration of this side of the story.

 

The name repetition syndrome strikes again when Jack is designating teams to sweep the base – one of the soldiers is called Rothman, who of course turns up as a peripheral character in Season Three.

 

I’m not sure why Daniel is part of the group sweeping the base – not to dis the guy, but he’s not really up on military manoeuvres yet, and he only gets a diddy hand gun.  He also distracts Jack with a stupid question and nearly gets him killed.

 

I just love the little conversation Jack and Daniel have outside the infirmary using lip reading and hand signals.  The contrast between Jack’s military precision and Daniel’s artistic representation is really cool.  I did have to watch the scene three times before I figured out what they both said (once to watch their hands, once to watch their lips and once to put it all together), but that’s probably just me being an idiot!

 

Daniel actually redeems himself quite nicely in the infirmary, despatching one of the reetu with great aplomb after Jack is incapacitated.  He does get a little trigger happy and nearly shoots Sam when she turns up, though.

 

Jack shows great character in his decision to allow Charlie to go with the Tok’ra – he gets past all his hang-ups about them and just does what’s best for the kid.  The promises to visit back and forth are all very well, but we never see or hear from Charlie again and, despite Selmac’s warnings, the reetu never reappear either.

 

And so Jack has to say goodbye to another Charlie – at least this one’s still alive, which is more than we can say for the other two, but poor Jack just never cuts a break, does he?  Also, considering the fate of the Tok’ra base in Season Five, I don’t rate Charlie’s chances of surviving Anubis’ attack – though we are never told if he’s even there, so I guess we don’t really know.

 

Characteristic Moments

 

Charlie gets one of Jack’s catchphrases when he hears people approaching the infirmary:

Charlie: For crying out loud…

Jack actually looks inordinately proud of his influence on the boy!

 

We also get one of Jack’s less frequent catchphrases when Jacob is telling them about the reetu:

Jacob: I hate to tell you this, but these reetu could level a couple of cities and take out a lot of people with them.

Jack: Sweet.
