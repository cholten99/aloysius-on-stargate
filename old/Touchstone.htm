Touchstone

 

 

Writer: Sam Egan

Director: Brad Turner

 

Now, something went wrong with the video recorder or the satellite link on this one, so I have no sound for the first few minutes and no picture for a while after that.  So, you’ll have to bear with me while I improvise…

 

Daniel and Sam are explaining something to General Hammond, and looking all earnest about it.  Jack throws in the odd comment as per usual, and even Teal’c contributes, making this a very team-oriented meeting.

 

We get a picture of what looks like one of those executive toys people have on their desks to play with when they get bored at work.  Apparently, this one controls the weather of an entire planet, though – impressive!

 

Daniel does a ridiculous kind of squat to greet the grass-skirted natives when they arrive on the planet.  The things you have to do as an anthropologist, eh?

 

The natives all look really annoyed, and SG-1 look shocked and indignant for a bit.  So far, the only word I’ve managed to figure out is a “What?” from Sam when the head native guy is telling them what’s happened.  (Really must learn to lip read…)

 

And then – oh my gosh – hand of statue empty – weather doohickey gone – natives seem to think that SGC people took it – ack!  Why do relations with offworld cultures always go horribly wrong?

 

Judging by the somewhat scanty clothing of the natives, I’m guessing they’re annoyed because it’s getting chilly and they have no way of covering up their wonderfully toned midriffs.

 

Daniel and Sam look upset, Jack looks sceptical and Teal’c is his usual inscrutable self for a bit – and then the screen goes blue!!

 

Then, suddenly, we’re back in the temple, Jack says, “Trust us?” and – WOOHOO – we have sound!!

 

And once again, the fate of an entire planet rests in the capable hands of good old SG-1.  And this must be the longest pre-credits sequence of the show – or maybe it just seems that way to me because I can’t hear what anybody’s saying…

 

Luckily, Jack sums up the whole premise of the episode with his first sentence in General Hammond’s office – thank heavens for debriefings!

 

I’m not sure why there’s a dead silence and several confused expressions after Jack gives his gun and train analogy – seems perfectly clear and appropriate to me!

 

And so the NID conspiracy thickens yet again – did anybody really think they’d let the second gate go unused?  Area 51 had to show up sometime, and it seems fitting that Maybourne’s ended up there, playing with all the alien stuff.

 

It seems really weird to shoot an entire scene with SG-1 in silhouette, which happens when they arrive at Area 51.  Maybe there was just really bad light – I certainly can’t think of a reason for doing it like that on purpose.

 

When Teal’c talks about dismemberment, Maybourne looks even more scared than when Jack requested permission to beat the crap out of him in Bane.  He has good reason – Teal’c is far more likely to follow through, and Jack is far more likely to let him!

 

The way the others smirk to each other over Maybourne’s discomfiture is great, and Teal’c practices his looming skills to great effect in this scene.

 

Once Sam discovers that the gate is fake, you can actually see the cogs turning in Maybourne’s head as he tries to figure out how to get rid of them.  He gains an amazing amount of confidence when he’s got two armed and burly soldiers standing behind him.

 

Question:  Their plan to find the other stargate makes sense – but why go to Medrona to carry it out?  They know the conditions there are less than inviting, and they know there are angry natives there too, so why not go somewhere else, where they can work on the gate in peace and without getting snowed on?  I know the MALP is already on Medrona, but they must have more than one.

 

Hammond’s last marker is an interesting guy – I really want to know the history between those two.  Who exactly is Whitlow, and how come he owes Hammond a big favour?

 

Jack’s very hot when he’s in stealth mode, and Daniel is endearingly apprehensive when they’re walking up to the NID guys in a scarily open warehouse.

 

I love the way the NID guys spreadeagle themselves and belly flop into the horizontal event horizon.  This technique is all very well – but what if they come out the other side upside down?

 

Once back on Medrona, the Touchstone is certainly a very impressive doohickey, and the planet is very pretty when it’s not snowing.

 

Maybourne is sickeningly upbeat at the end, and we just know we haven’t see the last of him – which, in my opinion, can only be a good thing!

 

Best Bits

 

Hammond trying to get in touch with the President:

Hammond: Son, do you know what colour this phone is?

 

On arrival at Area 51:

Maybourne: Teal’c.  It’s good to see you well.

Teal’c: In my culture, I would be well within my rights to dismember you.

 

How do you find a missing stargate?

Sam: Now, if we can’t track the Touchstone, there may still be a way we can track the second gate on Earth.

Jack: Oh, sure!  Let’s just put out an APB for a huge, honkin’ two-storey metal ring with thirty-nine little pictures nicely engraved on it.
