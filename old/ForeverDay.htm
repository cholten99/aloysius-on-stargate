Forever In A Day

 

 

Writer: Jonathan Glassner

Director: Peter DeLuise

 

I wasn’t keen on rewatching this episode – but I took a deep breath and plunged in anyway…

 

Jaffa armour doesn’t protect them from zat blasts, staff weapon blasts or Tau’ri bullets – so why do they wear it?  There’s quite a serious body count in this episode – a lot more of a battle than we usually see.

 

And then we get the final showdown between Daniel and Ammonet.  The conflict in Daniel’s face as he trains his gun on her is heartbreaking – in a choice between killing Sha’re and letting Ammonet kill him, there’s no way he’s going to shoot her.

 

I like the way the eye glow fades just before we enter the dream.  It’s a subtle nod to the fact that Sha’re is gaining control for a moment, and one I hadn’t noticed before.  Whether it’s deliberate or not is neither here nor there!

 

And so we enter the dream…

 

It’s almost painful to see Sam and Teal’c’s expressions when Daniel wakes up in the infirmary and doesn’t remember what happened.  Having Teal’c be the one to kill Sha’re is so harsh – hasn’t be been through enough guilt being the one who chose her as Ammonet’s host?

 

I’m just glad the whole thing turns out to be a dream.  I know Daniel’s grieving and I know Teal’c’s actions must be really hard for him to accept, but his animosity towards Teal’c and his coldness towards everyone else is out of character if you ask me.  It works as a demonstration of how he *shouldn’t* deal with Sha’re’s death, but I don’t think it’s an accurate portrayal of how he’d react.

 

One more example of something that’s mentioned only in passing but that crops up in fanfiction all the time –

Sam: I brought you some of those chocolate walnut cookies you love so much.

 

Daniel’s rejection of Sam’s offer of a shoulder to cry on is so sad.  It’s so obvious that she desperately wants to be there for him, but his lack of response drives her away, when really I’m sure deep down he’d want to accept her comfort.  It’s one of those awful miscommunications that I hate so much – she ought to press the issue and he ought to let her, but instead they both end up suffering alone.

 

The second awakening in the infirmary is so sweet, and so sad.  It’s a testament to the many disappointments Daniel has suffered in his lfe that he can’t quite bring himself to believe Sha’re’s really there and alive.  You can tell from his expression and tone of voice.  And it’s heartbreaking that it’s not really true.

 

Daniel does have the most beautiful eyes, though, and they’re shown to great advantage in this episode.

 

Upon his resignation, it’s actually a good point that Daniel makes – with Sha’re finally dead, there’s no reason for him to stay with the Stargate program, though again, it’s not a reaction borne out by his enthusiasm and passion for exploration as evidenced in all the episodes *not* focused on finding Sha’re.

 

I love that Jack comes to see Daniel off and find out how long he’s going to be gone.  We get classic Jack fidgeting, half-strength Jack/Daniel banter that gradually warms up as the scene progresses, and Jack’s absolute confidence that Daniel will be back, which hides a determination not to let him go.  And I love that it’s Jack that Daniel comes close to opening up to.  Jack also comes up with all the same arguments as me, as to why Daniel will come back – he knows Daniel so well, and Daniel feels comfortable enough with him to talk about his feelings.

 

Daniel obviously takes away more than just Sha’re’s message from the dream, since he brings Rothman into the SGC in this episode, which means it doesn’t really happen, but then Rothman is actually there in Crystal Skull.  Unless Rothman was already floating around the SGC before this and we just hadn’t met him yet…  Whatever, it’s interesting that the introduction to his character into the show happens within Daniel’s head.

 

Jack and Sam present a united front in the leather jacket brigade in Daniel’s apartment – a good look for them both.

 

And then we get the all-too-rare hugs.  I love the way Sam turns to leave and then clearly changes her mind, walks all the way around Jack and practically forces Daniel into a hug.  It’s the assertiveness she should have used earlier in the infirmary and I’m glad she discovers it now – particularly considering it’s what prompts Jack to do the same!  Jack’s expression as he backs away is so full of emotion; you can tell there's so much more he wants to say – damn these macho types who suppress their feelings!!

 

This epsiode is so sad I can’t even get excited about the lovely Daniel skin on display in the bedroom scene…

 

The funeral is just beautiful – the ceremony, Daniel’s words, the close-ups of all the others who are there to support him.  It’s a great scene.

 

I love the brief moment of levity with Jack’s reaction to Rothman and Daniel’s advice to Rothman for dealing with Jack, though I always feel a bit sorry for Rothman – Daniel’s shoes are impossible to fill and he hasn’t got a snowball’s chance in hell.

 

Daniel’s forgiving of Teal’c may be somewhat perfunctory, but Teal’c’s obvious relief is wonderful to see.  You can tell he’s really cut up about what happened.

 

Once again, it’s Jack who goes to see Daniel in order to reinforce Sha’re’s message.  Either she can tell that he’s the one Daniel will respond most to, or he’s providing an environment within his own mind where her message will most likely be heard – either way, it’s an affirmation of their closeness.  After all, when Kasuf tells Daniel to go through the door and ask Sha’re her wishes, it’s Jack’s eyebrow raise that prompts him to actually do it.

 

Teal’c really has to make do with very little from Daniel – five words of vindication in the gate room, and a brief glance as he’s leaving Teal’c’s room after asking him about the harsesis child.  Once again, I’m glad this is a dream.

 

The déjà vu thing is cute – even in this situation, Jack and Daniel just can’t resist goofing around!

 

The whole scene where Daniel comes back is lovely – Daniel knows Jack well enough that he’s just coming up with excuses to get rid of Rothman, Jack wants to get rid of Rothman purely because he’s *not* Daniel, and everybody is clearly delighted when Daniel agrees to come back.

 

The reactions from the other three when Daniel turns up in the gate room are brilliant – a smile of unbridled joy from Sam, a slight nod and an almost smile from Teal’c (pretty much an ecstatic reaction from him!), and Jack’s whispered “thank you” for replacing Rothman.  Classic, characteristic, and very sweet.

 

Daniel takes his penchant for wandering off into trouble to a whole new level when he dials up the Stargate and disappears through the wormhole without the others!

 

I’m so glad Daniel gets to kiss Sha’re one more time before the dream is over – but that scene where he promises to find the child always makes me well up.  You can tell he’s realised he’s going to have to say goodbye to her for good, and the love and passion in his eyes is heartbreaking.

 

The final scene is even worse – and Sam’s reaction when she sees Daniel lying next to Sha’re’s body is pretty much how I feel, too.

 

Teal’c’s pronouncement that “Daniel Jackson will be fine” is a bit psychic!  He’s almost reacting as if he knows what Daniel saw in the dream – though I guess he could just be interpreting what Daniel said about him doing the right thing.

 

This is a really interesting episode, and it’s got loads of great Jack/Daniel moments I’d forgotten about, and I realise that thinking Daniel and Sha’re could ever have a happy ending was unrealistic – but come on!  With his parents dying, and Sha’re being taken, and delivering Apophis’ baby – hasn’t the poor guy been through enough?  I mean, having one of his best friends kill his wife is taking things a bit far if you ask me!
