The Broca Divide

 

Writer: Jonathan Glassner

Director: William Gereghty

 

Opens on dress uniform – they’re still going for the stiff military protocol approach at this point.  The smartness in briefings doesn’t last long, which I think is a good thing from Sam’s point of view – I think she looks better in her fatigues.  Jack, on the other hand, looks great in dress uniform!

 

Anyway, I can’t believe that General Hammond would send them through the Stargate with no idea what lies on the other side, particularly since they suspect the planet may be a Goa’uld stronghold.  I know Makepeace says that’s why SG-3 are going too, but I just don’t buy it – way too reckless!

 

As for Makepeace, this is not an appealing introduction of this character.  Within two lines of dialogue, he shows contempt both for the Air Force and for Daniel – not a great way to endear himself to the viewers!

 

However, his opinion of Daniel is slightly supported right afterwards by our favourite civilian being late for departure through the gate.  Yet again, we get Daniel’s civilian status shoved in our faces unnecessarily.  He ought to be allowed to show his uniqueness by expressing his different point of view, not by being portrayed as geeky or undisciplined – just my personal viewpoint!

 

I do like the brazen cheekiness of dealing with a plot hole by having one of the characters point it out.  Jack asks why the MALP doesn’t have night vision goggles – good point, that colonel!  Why indeed?

 

I think Richard Dean Anderson is still enjoying the military aspect of his character a little too much in this episode.  I love the way he rolls out of frame when they split up to confront the Untouched in the forest.  It’s so ridiculously over the top, especially with Daniel plodding awkwardly behind him, obviously very uncomfortable with his gear and helmet!

 

Sam’s comment about decorating her living room always jars a bit for me.  It seems as if she’s still trying too hard to fit in – attempting to join in with Jack’s sarcastic humour and, to my mind, failing rather miserably.

 

Sam tries to be funny and isn’t; Tuplo isn’t meant to be funny, but he always makes me laugh.  I think it’s the way his pompous attitude tries to assert itself from underneath that very silly hat – I just can’t take this guy seriously at all!

 

Once they’re back on Earth, we get a full exploration of the science vs military debate for the first time, and Daniel finally gets to shine.  He expresses the strength of his feelings very physically – when presenting his argument, he is on his feet, leaning forward, hitting the table to emphasise his words.  I love him for his passion, even though it’s actually unnecessary here.

 

There are subtle hints to the infection of the main characters that I didn’t notice the first several times I saw this episode, but they are there, and very clever.  After Sam calls the medics to the gate room when the soldiers fall through the window, she reaches up and scratches at her neck as if her skin is bothering her.  The very next scene, she leaps on Jack.  Similarly, Jack rubs at his chest and back when Janet is showing him the isolation rooms, which leads directly into his attack on Daniel.

 

I always feel a little bit sorry for Janet, having this episode as her introduction.  She does get a huge number of lines, but every single one of them is full of medical techno-babble, so we really don’t get much of an idea of her personality.  She’s kind of a medical version of Sam, excitedly babbling incomprehensible terminology at high speed.

 

Jack, on the other hand, gets to demonstrate his screeching skills!  I have no idea what animal he thinks he’s trying to be, but it’s a very odd one.  I think I see him as a jungle cat – lithe, agile, predatory – grrrrr!  Ahem, sorry!

 

It’s interesting to have Daniel and Teal’c going off-world together without the other two.  This is a pairing we don’t get to see very often.  Teal’c’s practicality is contrasted with Daniel’s impulsive emotional response to finding Malosha in the forest.  However, Teal’c gets to demonstrate his compassion and human feeling in his confrontation with Tuplo regarding his attitude to the Touched.  When Teal’c turns round after the attack by the Touched and finds Daniel missing, I think this is the only time he calls him just “Daniel”, without automatically adding his surname.

 

Poor Jack really has a tough time of it in this episode!  He has enough trouble dealing with techno-babble from Sam when he’s at full mental capacity.  Here, he has to put up with Janet spouting her histamine argument at him when his poor brain is already being attacked by the virus!

 

It’s very rare that Daniel manages not to look gorgeous, but boy does he achieve it in this episode!  Neanderthal is SO not a good look on him!  Interestingly, he seems to get a lot more make-up than the others.  Jack in particular doesn’t actually look that bad under the influence of the virus.

 

There is some great evidence of Jack/Daniel friendship towards the end.

 

Sam: So we’re going to tranq all the Touched?

Jack: Just the ones that get in the way of us finding Daniel.

 

This relates back to the previous episode when Sam is kidnapped and Jack’s focus immediately narrows just to doing whatever is necessary to get her back.  He really is totally devoted to his team and will do anything to protect them.  However, the sentiment here goes deeper than that, as evidenced by the moment Daniel wakes up.  The first word on Daniel’s lips is “Jack”, to which Jack responds with, “There’s our boy!”  This is really cute – the sense of family is strong, as is the protective feeling for Daniel, also demonstrated by Teal’c standing guard over him in the same scene.

 

Okay, so I’ve avoided it all the way through – now comes time for the Jack/Sam ship debate!  I’m not sure if I really want to get into this one, but hey – let’s give it a go!  Obviously, there is strong evidence of Jack/Sam shippiness in later seasons (although it is possible to dismiss it altogether, just as it’s possible to read it into scenes where it doesn’t exist), but I don’t see it starting here, despite Sam’s seduction of Jack.  Janet explains that one away with her comments about females choosing the strongest males with whom to mate.  Also, Jack’s reaction to her leaping on him doesn’t suggest any kind of sexual feeling on his part.  There is Jack’s fight with Daniel, though – not sure I can get around that one – it was the virus wot made him do it!!

 

However, the very end scene seems to me just Jack teasing a member of his team.  He’s winding Sam up, just like he does with Daniel and Teal’c – I don’t see any real sexual undertones here at all, and her smile when he says he was concerned is just her appreciating the anxiety of her commanding officer, a man she respects and admires.

 

Oh, I don’t know!  It must be obvious by now that I’m not a shipper!  I think I might refrain from comment on this aspect from now on.  I think it’s quite possible to argue these things many different ways – and I’d be happy to hear the shipper viewpoint, as it’s not one I really get!  The floor is open!

 

 

Characteristic Moments

 

Jack goes catchphrase mad in this episode – we get a total of four utterances of his favourite phrase:

 

When the Touched kneel before SG-1 in the forest:

Jack: Oh, for crying out loud, we’re not gods!  Come on, get up!

 

During the briefing when Daniel is trying to put across his viewpoint:

Hammond:  The President agrees with you.  He’s asked that we evaluate the scientific and cultural value of each mission from now on.

Jack: Oh, for crying out loud!

 

From underneath Sam when she leaps on him:

Sam:  Don’t you want me?

Jack:  No!  Not like this!  For crying out loud…  Carter!  What’s gotten into you?

 

Upon recovering from the virus:

Teal’c: I cannot be certain you are back to being yourself.  You referred to me as Lucy.

Jack: Oh, for crying out loud – will you just open the door!

 

 

We also get the first instance of one of Jack’s other characteristic utterances – the ever-so-eloquent “Ah!”

 

As they are about to set out through the gate for the first time:

Makepeace: We’ll take point.

Jack: Ah! (With the upraised finger in the face, of course!) We’ll go through first.

 

 

One of my favourite Daniel mannerisms makes it’s first appearance too.  I love the way he stutters when he’s taken by surprise.

 

When Hammond is trying to shut him up in the debriefing:

Hammond: Dr Jackson, you’re wasting your breath – you’ve already won the argument!

Daniel: But I have to insist that you – wh… wh… wh… what?  I’ve already won?

 

 

Best Bits

 

Right at the start:

Jack: Thank you sir, I pride myself on my deductive reasoning skills.

The look Sam gives him at this point is hilarious.  She looks as if she can’t believe he’s being flippant to the general, as well obviously having trouble accepting the notion of Jack and deductive reasoning being associated with one another!

 

Teal’c’s utter calm in the face of the attack by Johnson:

Teal’c: General, I would prefer not to hurt this man.

He is way cool!

 

Daniel’s response to Teal’c’s implacable practicality:

Daniel: Maybe I have a natural immunity.

Teal’c: Perhaps you will develop symptoms later.

Daniel: Thank you for the moral support.
