Seth

 

Writer: Jonathan Glassner

Director: William Corcoran

 

Okay, so the iris opens, a wormhole forms, and Jacob steps out onto the ramp.  I know they must have previously arranged for him to stop by, but shouldn’t they keep the iris closed and wait for a code, just in case?  And if he did call ahead, why didn’t he come through then?  There’s definitely something wrong with the procedure here!

 

Daniel looks incredibly cute now that his new haircut has settled down a bit.  I fluctuate as to whether I prefer him with long or short hair, but I think Season Three as a whole is bis best look.

 

I love the immediate reactions of the Science Twins to Jacob’s holographic doohickey – both are fascinated, but Sam by the tech and Daniel by the content.  Jack, on the other hand, just looks confused, poor lamb…

 

I do like the premise of this ep – the idea of an active Goa’uld still on Earth is an interesting one, and the way they figure out where he’s hiding is clever.  It’s different enough from Hathor not to seem like a repeat, and Seth makes a great new variation on the Goa’uld front.

 

The three-way conversation between Sam, Jacob and Selmac in the elevator is very well done.  The switches between host and symbiote work very well, and Sam’s reactions are spot on.  I love the exasperation in Selmac’s voice when she says Jacob’s issues with Mark are annoying her.  It’s good to see some Tok’ra characterisation, especially since we don’t see a lot of Selmac.

 

The look Daniel gives Jack when he interrrupts the flow of his explanation is great – Jack obviously succumbs to the expression of hurt surprise, since he does a little eyebrow wiggle of apology.

 

Teal’c telling a joke is just wonderful – I’m never sure if the others really don’t find it funny, or if they’re just stunned by the sight of Teal’c laughing.  Poor Teal’c!

 

Seth turning up in the US is just a bit of a co-incidence, dontcha think?  At least they acknowledge it, though.  It’s quite funny, considering Selmac orchestrated the whole thing to get Jacob back to Earth to see Mark, and then Jacob gets to actually drag Selmac on a mission to delay things.  Of course, the way events turn out is what prompts Jacob to give in, so Selmac turns out victorious in the end – the whole thing is very nicely structured.

 

Considering Daniel found the information on the cult on the ATF webpage, it was a fairly safe bet that the agents would be ATF, so Jack’s FBI guess is a bit silly.  Still, it makes for a characteristic first impression of Jack for the ATF guy – and it goes steadily downhill from there!

 

I love the double act of Jack and Jacob winding up the ATF guy.  Jack just refuses to pass up any opportunity of rubbing people up the wrong way, and the way Jacob plays along as his commanding officer is great.

 

It’s nice to see Jack deferring to Daniel’s superior knowledge when he mouths “Fargough” at him, but it’s also good to see that Daniel doesn’t have all the answers, responding with a confused head shake.  And then Daniel returns the favour by deferring back to Jack when the ATF guy starts demanding explanations.  This shows Jack and Daniel interacting very smoothly, and using their differing areas of expertise to the advantage of the situation, rather than clashing.

 

I love the way the ATF guy rubs his forehead after talking to the President.  He learns pretty quickly that it’s not worth trying to mess with Jack, and that Jack has enough high-level support to back up his cockiness.  And Jack certainly doesn’t let up when the ATF guy puts him in charge – he just can’t resist rubbing it in!  I feel sorry for *anyone* who has to deal with Jack after they’ve annoyed him!

 

They discuss the electric shock plan and the effects of the nishta fairly loudly in the presence of the ATF guy.  I know they’ve told him there’s secret technology involved, but it does seem as if they’re being a bit lax with the information they’re throwing around.

 

I’ve always found the uncanny resemblance between Seth and Daniel’s father rather disturbing – it’s a good job Daniel doesn’t seem to notice!

 

I love Teal’c’s confusion over the idea of a father and son not speaking – he may be stoic and seem unemotional at times, but he’s a softie at heart and he’s really keen on good parenting.  This scene is similar to his confrontation of Tuplo in The Broca Divide.

 

Jack gets ot show off his anti-mind-control abilities again, looking less than convinced by the chanting Seth gets them to do.  Sam, on the other hand, looks positiviely delighted to be on thron-polishing duty.  She does ‘adoring disciple’ very well!

 

They take quite a chance when they rush into the main room and start zatting people.  Considering they’re each carrying two zats (and so shooting simultaneously with both hands), it’s fairly lucky that they hit everyone, and only once.  I’m hoping they stopped outside and each agreed on their targets first!

 

I know they need to have difficulty locating Seth in the tunnel, but the way the disciples put their hoods up as they leave the rings looks awfully contrived – couldn’t some of them just have had their hoods up beforehand, or in the next scene without the shot of them actually doing it?

 

Daniel reacts remarkably quickly in following Jack’s lead and shooting the attendants when Seth catches up with them.  His transition from scholar to soldier is well on its way by this stage.

 

I really don’t think Jack’s flippant Wizard of Oz reference is appropriate on discovering Sam has killed Seth.  Sam looks pretty upset about it – as well she might, considering she’s just used Goa’uld technology to kill someone.  Daniel’s shock and Jack’s flippancy really don’t help much – it’s not a very good team solidarity moment.

 

I always feel as if the epilogue to this episode is a bit abrupt.  I guess we don’t need to explore the Carter family reconciliation in too much depth, but Mark’s welcome just seems a bit too easy after what was said in the elevator scene at the beginning.  I also wonder how exactly they explain Jacob’s miraculous recovery, and what he’s now doing with himself…

 

Characteristic Moments

 

Jack gets a ‘yadda’ and two ‘sweet’s, and an Oz reference in this episode – a good workout of the less common catchphrases:

 

Jack showing that patience is not his best attribute:

Daniel: I did a timeline Boulian search for religion, cult, Set, Setesh, Setec, Seth…

Jack: Yadda

 

Jack being smug about his influence:

ATF Guy: I just got off the phone with the President.

Jack: - of the United States of America?  Sweet.

 

Discussing what Seth is likely to do to them:

Jack: Dare I ask about the men inside the compound?

Daniel: They were turned into eunuchs.

Jack: Eunuchs – as in snippety-doo-dah?  Sweet.

 

On discovering Sam standing over Seth’s corspse:

Daniel: You killed him.

Jack: Hail, Dorothy.

 

Best Bits

 

Jack: Jaffa jokes? Let’s hear one of them!

Teal’c: I shall attempt to translate one, O’Neill.  A Serpent guard, a Horus guard and a Setesh guard meet on a neutral planet.  It is a tense moment: the Serpent guard’s eyes glow, the Horus guard’s beak glistens, the Setesh guards nose drips.

(Teal’c laughs uproariously, everyone else stares at him for a moment.)

Daniel: Okay, so…
