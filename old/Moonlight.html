Moonlight


by


Aloysius


I am the moon

Shining through the sheltering branches of a leafless tree

I am a light in the darkness

Obscured by the shadows of the world

You are drawn to me

Drawn by fascination and by longing

You find your way through the obstacles

Searching for my brightness

Which shines amidst the dull depression of your mundane life

You seek out that light

Not knowing – or perhaps in spite of the knowledge

That it will burn


Witness my full unshielded splendour

And you will be consumed

Dying in ecstasy to feed my light

Scream as I extinguish your feeble flame

Growing only more luminous in the act

Scream and laugh at the fear and joy

That envelop you in equal measure

This is an experience that cannot be transcended

Fitting then that it should be your last

You will become a part of me

Your essence subsumed but living on forever

After the empty husk of your mortal body has been discarded


Join the countless others

In eternal worship of my glory

Release your memories

Your loves

Your pain

The stolen experiences that give me meaning

Give up your life

So that mine will never end.
