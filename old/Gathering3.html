Gathering 2005 report continued...
Princess Allegra was made Harts Ambassador to the Tarantulas, a matriarchal tribe who wouldn’t allow large groups of men into their camp, so I was assigned as part of the Princess’ entourage for her ambassadorial visit.  As a scribe, I faithfully took notes in the meeting, which was an excellent way of looking while I was involved in the plot without having to actually think of anything to say or do.  I was told that the Tarantulas were not to be trusted and were very dangerous, but their dark elf leader, Matron Mother Kage, was most welcoming, and their orcish ambassador Og-Nak was very entertaining.

We also visited the Viper camp, since they had information about the state of things in Rus.  This was where we met the were-bears, and also the Vipers’ chief incantor, who was a rather amusing character.  The plot got ridiculously complicated and may need a couple of extra events all its own in order to be resolved.

One of the other things we did was protect a rite that was held on one of the afternoons.  I had very little idea what was actually going on – there was an inner circle of incantors, which couldn’t be broken, and a large group of people milling around outside it.  There were several attacks from outside by people wearing dark masks, and I think one of the characters performing the rite died somehow, but it wasn’t very clear.

On the last night, there was supposed to be an in-character pub crawl, which entailed going round to each of the other camps to sample their bars.  Dave and I had visited the Harts one the previous evening and been presented with orcish confectionery, which turned out to be pickled onions coated in chocolate – actually not bad!  We headed to the Viper camp around 9:30pm, only to be told that the in-character bars (which only charged in-character money – plastic tokens) weren’t allowed to open until 11pm, as this would cut into the profits from the marketplace beer tent, which charged real money.  So, we duly headed over the Dragon camp at 11pm, where we found a huge bar tent, selling delicious home-brewed flavoured wine and presenting live theatre.  After that, we went to the Tarantula camp, at which point our bar crawl was suspended because the Princess had a meeting with the Matron Mother which resulted in her having to report to the Harts immediately.

On the last day, there was a huge battle that involved all the factions.  I didn’t go along, and I heard afterwards that it was a bit disappointing for our guys, who only fought for about 15 minutes and got seriously beaten by overwhelming odds.

The other activity which was available was “monstering”.  This entailed playing monsters or other characters for the various factions to interact with, so they could go on missions or develop their own plot without having to fight other player characters.  I went on one monstering mission, which involved a party of soldiers and villagers from Bishop Auckland arriving at the Harts camp to petition for their leader to be made official baron of the area.  This meant I could play a villager who didn’t need to fight, and it basically involved hanging around the Harts camp for a couple of hours while the Harts leaders deliberated and eventually declined our request.

All in all, it was fantastically organised and really interesting.  I could have been much more involved than I was, but I chose to stay pretty much on the sidelines my first time out, so as to learn the rules and figure out what to do before launching myself fully into the game.  If I go again, I’ll be tempted to play a Flight character, as they are a lot of fun and don’t do any fighting.  Contributing to rituals also looks like quite a lot of fun, though perhaps a little dangerous considering the consequences of over-running.  With my pink dress, I think I’d make a good flamingo – who knows, maybe next year…
