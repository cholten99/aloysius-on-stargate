Solitudes

 

Writer: Brad Wright

Director: Martin Wood

 

Ah, the classic formula where two of our heroes are trapped somewhere, one of them badly hurt, and the others have to figure out where they are and rescue them!  This storyline has been done so many times before, but it never fails.  Whumping and angst galore…

 

The pre-intro teaser for this ep goes rather mad on the excitement levels – power surges, people tossed through the wormhole like rag dolls and only two of the usual four at that, bits of the gate exploding, Gary Jones’ technician describing it all in panicked terms!  When it cuts to Antarctica, the contrast between the noise and chaos of the gate room and the silence and stillness of the fissure is very clever.

 

Daniel-whumping – always a good thing, as far as I’m concerned, since he looks very cute when he’s unconscious.  Jack-whumping is good too, since his snarkiness increases exponentially in relation to how badly he’s hurt, which makes a mortally wounded Jack rather funny!

 

Jack: You wouldn’t think jagged bone digging into raw nerves would hurt, but it does.

 

He’s a really terrible patient, as Janet often has cause to discover.  However, he also proves to be an excellent commanding officer in this ep.  He’s the one that’s hurt but he provides all the morale and encouragement, preventing Sam from succumbing to despair and maintaining the resolute attitude that they’ll get home somehow.

 

There’s good characterisation of Sam, too.  She comes up with all sorts of theories about how they ended up somewhere other than the SGC, and her realistic understanding of the situation makes it difficult for her to subscribe to Jack’s emphatic optimism.  She’s really too smart for her own good, and thinks way too much, as Jack points out to her.

 

Back at the SGC, we have classic team solidarity, with Teal’c and Daniel desperate to do something, adamant that they should have come through the gate, convinced they would never give up information to the enemy, and unbending in their determination to find their missing team-mates.

 

In the two pairings, Sam and Teal’c are the realists, pointing out the impossible odds of the situation resolving itself happily, while Jack and Daniel are the optimists, neither prepared to accept defeat.  Eventually, it’s only Daniel who perseveres until he solves the problem – the other three all give up at some point.

 

Question – how come the original probe they sent through didn’t get obliterated by the enemy laser blasts like the second one does?

 

The introduction of Sgt Siler – he’s the guy they all turn to for information on gate technology when Sam’s not there.  I like the way he corroborates Daniel’s theories about what might have happened – he’s very straight forward and pragmatic.

 

Sam demonstrates in this ep that she’s not quite the wunderkind we’ve always thought.  Admittedly, she’s under stress in a rather difficult situation, but I’ve never been able to figure out why she never thinks to try a different address.  She’s incredibly dogged in her attempts to get the DHD to work, but she only looks at the problem from one angle.  It takes Daniel’s leap of intuition to solve the puzzle, showing the difference in the way they think.

 

I love Teal’c’s expression when he finds Daniel at 4am and Daniel says, “Did you see that?”  Teal’c looks round suspiciously and then raises his eyebrow as if he thinks Daniel’s gone totally mad!

 

Daniel demonstrates his natural teaching skills when he finally figures it out.  He doesn’t come right out with his theory, he prompts his ‘class’ by asking them questions, making them work it out for themselves.

 

Apparently, for this ep, they actually froze the set and had Amanda Tapping climb about on the ice.  She kept slipping and falling, but they just kept the cameras rolling so they’d have an authentic shot of her sliding down the chute at the end!

 

It’s really cute the way Teal’c and Daniel ask for permission to go to Antarctica and then go racing off with General Hammond.  Surely, it would take them rather a long time to get there, though, and it would be much more practical to send some people who are a bit nearer to find Sam and Jack before they freeze to death.  Just a thought…

 

This is another episode where I feel we’re cheated a bit by it ending too soon.  I want more!  There’s some good aftermath fan fiction based on this episode, so obviously a lot of people agree with me, though a lot of it focuses on Jack/Sam ship, which I just don’t see here.  Both times Jack has thought he was going to die in this series so far, he’s been thinking about Sara, not Sam.

 

Characteristic Moments

 

After a bit of a dearth of these, we now get several all at once.

 

Oz reference when Jack sees where they are:

Jack: Unless they’ve redecorated the gate room, I don’t think we’re in Kansas any more.

 

The catchphrase is back!

Jack: Misdialled?  You mean this place is a wrong number?  Oh, for crying out loud!

 

Sam points out the obvious yet again, eliciting a predictable response from Jack when they’re digging in the ice:

Sam: Colonel!  Serpent Guard.  I guess he didn’t make it.

Jack: Ya think?

 

Best Bits

 

Sarcasm under stress – a classic Jack reaction:

Sam: What’s wrong with your chest?

Jack: I think I cracked a rib, too.

Sam: Why didn’t you say something?

Jack: I was afraid you might try to put a splint on it.

 

I have no idea why I love this line so much – I just do:

Daniel: Teal’c and I were flung out of this gate at this end so fast I don’t even remember hitting the ramp.

There’s just something about the way he says it that I really love.  Maybe it’s the way he tries to break the land speed record for dialogue, I don’t know, but it’s one of my favourite lines ever.  Yes, I know I’m weird!

 

Daniel in lecture mode:

Daniel: [to Teal’c] What happens when you dial your own phone number?  Wrong person to ask.  [turns to Hammond]  What happens when you dial your own phone number?

Hammond:  You get a busy signal.

Daniel: Exactly!

I love the way he realises Teal’c won’t be able to answer and just turns straight to Hammond and repeats the question.  His enthusiasm when he’s got an idea is very cute.  I think he’d be the kind of teacher who gives out gold stars…
