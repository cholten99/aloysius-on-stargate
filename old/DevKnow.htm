The Devil You Know

 

Writer: Robert C Cooper

Director: Peter DeLuise

 

This episode is great for Daniel angst – if you’re a Daniel fan, it pays to watch him closely throughout, even when he’s not the focus of a scene.  What am I saying?  It’s always worth doing that, whatever the episode! :o)

 

He starts off by managing to be amazingly cool and cutting in the confrontation with Apophis.  He completely maintains his composure, even when announcing Ammonet’s death, not allowing Apophis any kind of triumph by revealing his own pain.  What makes the performance even more powerful is the way Daniel collapses once they’re thrown back in the pit.  As soon as Apophis isn’t watching, his defiance drains away and we see how he really feels about the situation.  It’s great acting, and very tough on the heartstrings.  I’ve always felt this experience is tougher on Daniel than on any of the others.

 

It’s pretty tough on the other prisoners too, though:

Apophis: How many follow me?

Kintac: Three hundred.  The rest have been banished to the surface.  They will change their minds or die.

It must be hard being a minion – not a lot of great life choices!

 

With the delving into memories we get an explanation of why Sam’s brother isn’t speaking to Jacob at the start of Seth.  The story of her mother’s death gives us some more insight into Sam’s past than we’ve had before and – oh, what a surprise – there was a tragic death in her family.  This moves her up above Teal’c in the personal loss department, but still below Jack and Daniel in the tragedy stakes.

 

Apophis really isn’t very good at the subtle interrogation thing, is he?  His temper and impatience won’t let him use the Blood of Sokar to its full potential.  He just can’t wait out the hallucinations – he has to push it, and then he’s back to yelling and threatening violence again.  You just can’t suppress megalomaniac characteristics!

 

He also does the incredibly predictable tactic of torturing his prisoners in turn and trying to make them believe the others have already broken.  Classic attempt to divide and conquer – Apophis is never in danger of being original!

 

This episode is the first introduction of Aldwin, one of my favourite Tok’ra.  I really like him a lot, though he gets put in a tough position here.  He has to present the practical viewpoint, citing the necessity of destroying Sokar even though it may mean the deaths of the rest of SG-1.  Poor guy – I wouldn’t want to try and stop Teal’c when he’s in determined mode!  Aldwin does have the good grace to look really uncomfortable about what he has to do, and he also allows Teal’c to come with him – basically, he’s a good guy, really.

 

As well as being predictable, Apophis is rather gullible, though Martouf does use his arrogance against him quite cleverly.  When being tortured, you have to hold out just long enough so that the bad guy believes you when you appear to crack.  No matter how many times the good guys use this trick, the bad guys are always fooled, because they are always completely convinced of their ability to break their prisoners!

 

Sam, on the other hand, is just plain idiotic!  How could she think Martouf was telling the truth?  She knows the Tok’ra resistance is on Vorash – she’s just been there the day before!  Dur!

 

The hallucination Daniel experiences is the only one that’s even remotely convincing – Jacob and Charlie asking about stuff to do with the Stargate in the context of those memories is automatically suspicious, but Jack not being able to remember the name of the planet Kheb is actually pretty believable!  There isn’t so much of a jolt to tip off the victim in this memory – so full points to Daniel here for sussing it out anyway.

 

He gets even more points for managing to get the communication device.  I just love his little expression of resignation after his attempt to punch the guard is totally unsuccessful.  And he gets Jack’s approval when he arrives back in the cell with the radio thingie – Jack doesn’t sound surprised, just really impressed, which is nice.

 

Martouf demonstrates that he is just like the other Tok’ra after all, when they find out the High Council’s plan to destroy the moon.  Even though he is one of the ones being sacrificed, Martouf immediately leaps to the defence of the other Tok’ra – they always know best, no matter what the circumstances.

 

Aldwin shows his better side when he realises that Teal’c is going to attempt to pick the others up mid-transport.  He offers his advice to improve their chances of success.

 

Sokar is seriously freaky!  I think he’s been drinking his own “blood” – he really seems convinced that he is a god.  When he says that Netu exploding is impossible, it sounds to me as if he really believes it, rather than just saying it for the benefit of his Jaffa.  He also suffers from the common Goa’uld problem of bad anger management – enraged howling in the face of defeat seems to run in the species.

 

Apophis escapes in the nick of time – again!  He’s really good at those last minute transports to safety, isn’t he?  He must have earned his “Finding Transport Rings When In Mortal Danger” scout badge by now!  The final shot that reveals he survived isn’t nearly as much of a shock as the revelation at the end of Jolinar’s Memories.  By now, it’s more – Apophis survived again?  What a surprise…

 

As they escape in the Tok’ra vessel, all the others are doing the “we survived and we’re back together” happy relief bit, but Daniel’s sitting off at the back looking all tragic and alone.  Poor honey pie!  He definitely has it worst in this episode.  And don’t we love it when he suffers?  Heartless, or what?

 

Best Bits

 

Daniel faces his enemy with impressive cool:

Daniel: Your mate, Ammonet, is dead.  Sorry to ruin your day.  No, actually, I’m wrong about that – I’m not sorry.

Ooh – cutting!  Good on him!

 

Jack likes to keep his team informed as to his status:

Jack: Daniel?

Daniel: Yeah?

Jack: I’m gonna pass out again…

The way he does this is just so funny.

 

Teal’c’s honesty ruins any chance he has of the Tok’ra being impressed with him:

Aldwin: I am sorry I doubted you.

Teal’c: I am as surprised as you that we survived.

See?  Aldwin’s lovely!  Shame he isn’t in it much more until he succumbs to the blood bath of The Last Stand in Season Five.
