The First Commandment

 

Writer: Robert C Cooper

Director: Dennis Berry

 

Action-packed opening presents the opportunity for minor character death as we get people we don’t know fleeing through darkness from scary natives on an unknown planet.  However, the atmosphere and tension of Frakes’ death is totally ruined by the accessories sported by said natives.  What is with those egg-cup helmets?  They make me laugh every time I see them!

 

First question of the episode – why doesn’t Connor go through the gate?  The wormhole is open and I’m pretty sure he has the opportunity.  I know he doesn’t want to abandon his friend, but surely getting some info back to the SGC would be a good idea.

 

Anyway, any such nit-picking thoughts are driven totally from my mind as Daniel makes his first appearance.  Hurrah!!  The introduction of the bandanna/sunglasses combo!!  Please excuse me for a moment while I drool!  Ahem!  Whatever your views on our beloved SG-1, you can’t deny that Daniel looks infinitely better here than in the previous episode.  He moves more confidently and his very presence on the screen has much more impact purely because he’s managed to lose the helmet.  There are very many gorgeous shots of Daniel in this episode – don’t worry, though, I’m not going to comment on all of them – just suffice it to say YUM!

 

Brief aside on names – Teal’c is still calling Sam “Dr Carter” here, and it also turns out I was wrong to suggest Broca Divide is the only time he refers to Daniel without his surname.  A bit later on in this ep, Teal’c says, “There is a problem with your plan, Daniel.”  Obviously, it’s not as unique as I had thought, but it still sounds very weird.

 

The two sides of Jack O’Neill get great juxtaposition when Connor ambushes Daniel.

 

Daniel: Okay, don’t shoot me.

Jack: (stepping up behind Connor) Very sound advice.

 

Jack’s tone as he says this is really frightening – nobody threatens Daniel and gets away with it.  That is, until Jack recognises Connor and sees that he is near collapse.  Then he immediately switches to caring commander – what a guy!

 

Second question of the episode – it took them six hours to come and investigate after SG-9 activated the Stargate and nobody came through?  Surely that occurrence suggests a fairly urgent need for help!

 

During the scene in which both Sam and Connor refuse to return to the SGC, Daniel takes something out of Teal’c’s pack and starts fiddling about in the background.  I can’t tell what he’s doing, and it doesn’t seem to amount to much, as they set off to Hanson’s base camp straight afterwards.  I pronounce myself baffled, and wonder if any eagle-eyed viewers can shed some light on this mystery…

 

Ah, the delight of MREs – cheese that tastes like chicken, yum.  This is the first time we get to see Daniel eating white-ish slop, though it does happen in several other episodes.  He seems to enjoy the slop in later ep’s a lot more than he does here – alien slop is obviously preferable to the Earth variety!

 

Third question of the episode – Connor says that if it had been just one thing that had sent Hanson over the edge, he and Frakes would have seen it coming and been able to do something to stop it.  Correct me if I’m wrong, but how does that make any kind of sense?  Surely, if it had been one thing, it would have come as a total surprise and the transformation would have been so abrupt they wouldn’t have had a chance to do anything!

 

There are several wonderful close-ups of Jack during Connor’s tale of the natives being staked out in the sun to die.  His expression is fantastic – Jack does not take kindly to SG officers playing god and mistreating those they are meant to be helping.

 

I know it’s vaguely shippy, but I just love the way it cuts to a close-up of Jack after Sam says, “I guess I’ve always had a soft spot for the lunatic fringe.”  Very simple trick of associating Jack with those words, but very effective and very funny.

 

Cliché ahoy!  The traditional ledge from which to look down on the enemy stronghold makes an appearance here.  However, I should not mock – these ledges crop up in my fanfiction with alarming regularity!

 

I don’t really know what to say about Hanson – he doesn’t really have any impact on me.  He’s convinced himself he’s in the right, he’s been corrupted by power, he has twisted justifications for his actions – yadda, yadda, yadda.  The whole examination of maniacal power-mad self-imposed godhood rather passes me by, I’m afraid.  It’s probably because I’m too busy rejoicing at Daniel’s bandanna transformation…

 

There’s some great choreography when the three guys go to subvert Jamala to their cause.  The way Daniel casually joins him at the stream, then Teal’c steps imposingly into frame on the other side, and Jamala runs straight into Jack standing behind him – very cleverly worked out.

 

Later on, though, I always think it’s rather silly the way Daniel hustles Jamala out of the way when Teal’c is going to fire his staff weapon to free the second device.  Does he think Teal’c won’t wait until they’re clear before shooting?

 

Hanson certainly has a flair for the dramatic.  I like the way he has the Stargate set up so that Jack and Connor are forced to walk the plank above a horizontal wormhole – very effective.

 

And hurrah again!  Daniel gets to arrive in the nick of time to save everybody for once, not something he gets much opportunity to do.  The subsequent face off between Daniel and Hanson for control of the mob is excellent.  Hanson uses fear and superiority to try to force the people to obey, whilst Daniel of course offers them empowerment and equality through the giving of knowledge.  This is a great portrayal of Daniel’s character – reason against tyranny.

 

The amazing shield that will supposedly enable the people of the planet to thrive outside the caves seems rather small to me.  What happens when the society grows outside the valley?  I guess they do have more room than before, but it’s hardly a permanent solution, and Sam tells Daniel they shouldn’t come back to check everything’s okay.

 

The final exchange between Sam and Jack is classic.  I love the way Jack tries to be comforting and futzes it up by getting the commandments wrong.  It’s very sweet and very Jack!

 

In conclusion, here we have the first instance of Black Widow Sam.  This is slightly different because Hanson is an ex- rather than potential boyfriend, and we don’t mourn him in the least, but it does set the scene for guys getting killed left, right and centre in episodes to come!

 

 

Characteristic Moments

 

Oz references early on, when they decide to set off to find Hanson:

Jack:  We’re off to see the wizard!  (Space for more dialogue) To Oz!

 

Jack’s still not letting up on the catchphrase – I suppose I ought to stop mentioning it and only highlight episodes where it doesn’t appear!

Poised on the plank, about to the thrown through the “doorway to the underworld”:

Jack: We’re not demons, for crying out loud!

 

Best Bits

 

Jack’s reaction to insubordination:

Sam: No, sir.

Jack: No, sir?

Connor: (little bit later on) No, sir.

Jack: No, sir?  Does it say Colonel anywhere on my uniform?

 

At the start of the night-time attack by the natives:

Jack: Come on, we’ve got company.

Daniel: You sure? (Dart thunks into the tree right by his head)

Jack: Pretty sure!

 

Sarcasm in the face of adversity:

Sam: Colonel O’Neill!

Jack: Captain, I see things are turning out just as we planned.
