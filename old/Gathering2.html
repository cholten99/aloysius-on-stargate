Gathering 2005 report continued...
Combat is determined by lots of people running around and hitting each other with latex weapons of various descriptions.  Each character has a certain number of hit points, which it is their responsibility to keep track of, and they fall over when they have been hit a certain number of times.  Mages and incantors have a certain number of spells they can use each day, which they call out when casting them, and those within range are expected to react in an appropriate manner (freezing in place for Hold, running away for Fear, falling over for Strike Down, etc).  There are also healers around to sort people out if they’ve been wounded, though the Ritual of Peace that resides over most of the campsites prevents characters from actually dying most of the time.  There are, of course, various occasions when this isn’t in effect, so it is possible to lose a character – several people were assassinated over the weekend, and we lost a couple of guardsmen in one of the big battles.  However, you can register a new character very rapidly and join back in.
There are feuds between some of the factions, and it’s never a very good idea to antagonise the Tarantulas.  I heard stories before I went of it not being safe to walk the communal areas alone after dark for fear of mugging (only in game, of course), so I always made sure I had someone with me who was armed and knew what they were doing, but I never encountered any trouble.
Princess Allegra (Amy) + Dr Fairfax (Sean)
People’s varying costumes and accents were fantastic.  Amy had a blue dress with gold braid that she’d made herself, as well as a couple of very sumptuous coats and cloaks.  Sean wore a leather ensemble and a three-cornered hat, which was very dashing.  The guardsmen all wore chainmail and tabards in House Grimear green and black.  Evie had a wonderful red kimono, bone daggers, and fabulous ear jewellery.  Dave wore a blue coat that could be transformed into a cloak, and a brilliant furry hat.  The coat proved somewhat annoying, since it was buttoned all down the seams and both sleeves, and all the buttons kept coming undone but a few strategically placed safety pins largely solved that problem.  I had a pink and green medi-eval dress and a really nice brown cloak, which came in very handy after dark when it got quite chilly.

There were dark elves in full black make-up, orcs who were painted entirely green with huge ears, were-bears with fabulous bear heads that must have been incredibly hot to wear, and many, many more.

I befriended three of the guardsmen over the course of the weekend.  Alexander (real name Gred) was the first to be assigned as my bodyguard, but he quickly got bored of the assignment and kept going off with the rest to have exciting battles all over the place.  Kittimer (real name Simeon) was a new recruit – he turned up at the House Grimear tent on the first morning and enlisted, and we spent quite a lot of time together over the course of the weekend.  Then he saved the life of the Princess twice in the course of one battle and was assigned to be her bodyguard for the rest of the time.  Lastly was Philip (real name David), who attached himself to me on the last night because I was handing round honey nut mini Weatabix (a camping necessity), so he became my Snack Monkey for our last trip out to the marketplace.

The guardsmen had a lot to do, since there were attacks on the gate throughout the day, and even into the night – how nobody got trampled in the pitch blackness, I’ll never know.  Luckily for me, it was quite possible to avoid any fighting at all, simply by staying in the House Grimear tent, which was situated in the far corner of the campsite.  This afforded an excellent view of all the excitement without any danger of having to do anything!

There were musters twice a day, which involved everyone in the Harts camp gathering together to hear important news from the high-ups and generally faff for hours at a time.  It was vital to take a chair to these meetings, since they were interminable.

On the first night, Princess Allegra threw a vodka party and invited people from all over the camp to the House Grimear tent.  There was a staggering supply of alcohol, ranging from straight vodka to Mars Bar flavour vodka, the best of which was the vodka-laced chocolate milkshake.  A fantastic array of different people turned up, the most amusing of which were the half-orcs who run the Harts tavern.  Dave and I had visited a Russian delicatessen beforehand to purchase gifts for the princess, which included some rather horrible dried fish.  These were passed on to one of the half-orcs at the party, and he proceeded to eat an alarming amount of it, even enticing three other people to have a taste as well.
