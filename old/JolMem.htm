Jolinar’s Memories

 

Writer: Sonny Wareham and Daniel Stashower

Director: Peter DeLuise

 

It’s always nice to see Martouf, cos he’s a real sweetheart.  He has a wonderfully earnest way about him – he just gives off sincerity in waves.  There’s a good contrast between him and the two ‘extra’ guys he brings with him, who just sit there in total silence looking superior.  Four whole personalities and they’ve got nothing to say – I guess because, if they spoke, they’d have to be paid more…  Martouf, on the other hand, manages to be sympathetic and makes a real effort to relate to and support the humans.  Interestingly, Lantesh doesn’t say anything the entire double parter.

 

Martouf’s sidekicks do an impressive disappearing act.  They’re right with him when he comes through the gate, and they’re sitting on either side of him in the briefing but in the scene where Martouf follows Jack and Sam from the gate room to the briefing room they’re nowhere to be seen!  I guess with there being 28 levels in the SGC, it can be easy to get lost – but the trip from the gate room to the briefing room isn’t really that hard!

 

I love the fact that Martouf can say the fate of the entire galaxy is at stake with a completely straight face and incredibly earnest tone.  Sci-fi shows really are great!  Jack suggesting he’s being overly dramatic is pretty funny too – always the one to point out the clichés!  However, Jack’s attempts at humour throughout this episode demonstrate his grasp of the seriousness of the situation.  His joke about ending up in hell sooner or later anyway falls totally flat – this is not his usual flippant sarcasm, I see this as nervousness rather than irreverence, a subtle but important distinction.

 

When they get to Vorash, we see them walk right up next to the ship.  Then, the very next shot shows them ringing aboard – talk about a waste of resources!  Couldn’t they just have used the door?  They do seem to have suddenly acquired a big trunk, though, so perhaps they had to go to the Tok’ra base to collect supplies and then be ringed up to the ship from underground.  The cutting does make it look a bit silly, though.

 

Martouf’s response to Jack’s complaints about the ship is priceless.  He looks at him, opens his mouth, clearly thinks again, closes his mouth and turns away – smart man!  It’s never worth rising to the bait with Jack!  And Jack’s jokes continue to fall flat – Sam and Daniel look positively embarrassed by him when he replies, “To Hell with us!” when Martouf asks if they should embark.

 

Daniel shows his enthusiasm for alien food during the trip by investigating what I assume are the Tok’ra version of field rations as soon as they hit hyperspace.  Where does he put it all?  Probably all gets worked off in nervous energy and enthusiasm – must take a lot to fuel a hyperactive archaeologist!

 

We get the Jack/Daniel protective double team on the trip too.  I love the glance they exchange when Sam starts talking about Jolinar being tortured – they are such sweethearts.  Then Jack drags Martouf out of earshot so he can chew him out over pushing Sam too hard, while Daniel stays behind to offer the brotherly shoulder – all girls should have a pair of friends like them.  I wish!

 

Daniel’s attempts at Jack-type sarcasm when Martouf tells them about the escape pods are about as successful as Jack’s hell jokes.  He’s really cute when he’s nervous – he gets all hesitant and stuttery, and utterly fails to hide it.

 

Sokar’s interior decorating skills leave rather a lot to be desired.  Netu certainly isn’t going to be featured in the Top Ten Holiday Spots Around the Galaxy any time soon – what a nasty place!  Methinks Sokar went a bit overboard on the Devil act.  When he actually appears, he’s really creepy, though – the robe, the fingernails, the complexion – somewhat reminiscent of Emperor Palpatine from Star Wars? Or is that just me?  And the first view of Neonak in his funky helmet always makes me think of Boba Fett – possibly I’m reaching for the Star Wars allusions there, but hey!

 

Anway, Sam’s prior knowledge of Netu really comes in handy when they’re trying to find their way in.  They stop at a junction – one way shows signs of habitation and a lighted doorway while the other way just leads into molten lava.  Sam stops, thinks, points to the only way they can go, and says, “This way!”  Good job she was there, or they might have got really lost…

 

The first encounter with Binar and Neonak is great – I particularly love Daniel’s repeated glances of slight disbelief every time Jack makes a stupid comment.  You would have thought he’d be used to it by now!

 

Question: Binar’s a Goa’uld, right?  Then shouldn’t his symbiote be able to heal his eye socket?  It’s been a while since Sokar took his eye for allowing Jolinar to escape and, even if it couldn’t actually replace the eye, it really ought to be able to sort out the yellow pus, shouldn’t it?

 

Sam gets to show off her fancy footwork during their attempted escape.  She really likes those high kicks, doesn’t she?

 

And then we get the big revelation that Neonak is really – Apophis!!  I certainly didn’t see that one coming the first time I watched this ep!  He just refuses to die, doesn’t he?  You can just see Jack thinking “How many times are we gonna have to kill this guy?” just before the credits roll.

 

 

Characteristic Moments

 

The Jack noise of interruption crops up when he discovers that – surprise, surprise – the Tok’ra haven’t told them everything:

Jack: Have I mentioned to you that I hate surprises?

Martouf: We have a long journey ahead of us.  There will be much time to inform you of all the details.

Jack: Ah! [and he’s really annoyed this time cos we get both index fingers raised instead of just one!] Now that puts me in a position to be surprised!

 

Oz references upon arrival at the settlement on Netu:

Jack: Well, it’s certainly not an Emerald City…

 

Best Bits

 

Classic Jack indignation at Tok’ra assumed superiority:

Martouf: A full military incursion will be impossible, Colonel O’Neill.

Jack: We do covert…

 

After Sam makes rather free with the attitude and Neonak appears on the scene, Jack attempts to diffuse the situation:

Jack: We’re people of little consequence.  Pay no attention to us.

 

For once, Daniel keeps Jack company in the confusion stakes when Sam and Martouf start reading each other’s minds:

Martouf: Teal’c.

Sam: Is that possible?

Daniel and Jack together: What?
