Out Of Mind

 

(This review and the next one, are a bit of a departure from my usual style, since they were written specifically for the Save Daniel Jackson Campaign, at Biblio’s request.  She wanted people to look at episodes and examine the presence of the original premise of exploration, the sense of SG-1 as a family, and the inclusion of Daniel in a meaningful role.  Hopefully, that will explain the skew of the reviews!  Up until I wrote these reviews, I actually really liked these episodes…)

 

Original Premise:

Largely set off world, but you can’t argue that they’re attempting peaceful exploration, since they originally went to investigate and probably attack a Goa’uld base.  Doesn’t this seem more like an SG-3 marines type mission?  Not the best situation to make use of Daniel – see later.

 

SG-1 Family:

All four members of the team ask what’s happened to the other three at some point, but their phrasing is interesting.  Jack wants to know what happened to “my team”, the first words out of Sam’s mouth when she is revived are “SG-1”, but both Daniel and Teal’c ask about “my friends”.  I guess the distinction is Air Force/Non Air Force but it’s interesting all the same.

 

Teal’c obviously demonstrates real loyalty and dedication to the SG-1 family by quitting the SGC and actually returning to Chulak to recruit people for a rescue mission, despite the fact that he has no idea where the others are being held.  You can’t really fault his attitude in this one!

 

A lot of family feeling comes from outside the team in the form of Hammond’s conversations with Major Davis.  Poor Paul gets a rough deal here; he has to deliver the “SG-1 is only one team” point of view, so that Hammond can shout him down.  Teal’c and Hammond are totally focused on rescuing the other three throughout the two episodes – at any cost it seems (and more people die on the rescue than are actually rescued…).

 

Character Focus:

The first of the two eps is very Jack-centric – he gets the first half of the show all to himself.  Daniel is the second of the team to be revived, but we just get one shot of him looking confused (and gloriously naked!) before we’re whisked off to Sam’s area of the fake base.  Sam, of course, gets straight into being astute and clever by asking lots of questions and picking up on the mistakes made by their captors.  By the time we finally get back to Daniel being questioned, we’ve already heard the cover story of Hathor’s minions twice so it cuts straight to the flashbacks without any of the preamble, seriously cutting Daniel’s screen time compared to the other two.  Bizarrely, Daniel’s flashback to their previous encounter with Hathor is of Jack being made into a Jaffa – Daniel doesn’t even get to be in his own flashbacks, which makes no sense at all, since he shouldn’t be able to recall an incident he did not witness!!

 

Anyway, once Jack figures out it’s all a ruse and makes his escape, it comes as no surprise that he finds Sam first, and this is where the Jack/Sam shippiness comes into play.  I know the way Jack looks over Sam’s naked body probably wasn’t in the script (so we can’t really blame BW!) but it’s the first of many shippy moments.  Jack and Sam get to wander round the base (still without Daniel) for a while until Sam accidentally sets off her flashback device, forcing Jack to bundle her into a corner and hold onto her very tightly.  Fair enough, he has to keep her quiet but the snuggly closeness continues on long after she has regained control.

 

By the time they find Daniel, we’ve already had a “what’s going on” scene between Sam and Jack, so Daniel’s screen time gets cut once again.

 

This is where Sam seems to take on the role of “being really condescending to Daniel”.  She kindly explains to him that the base they are in is fake (Ya think?) and then, when Hathor appears, she warns him not to let her breathe on him – does she really think he needs to be reminded about that??

 

Hathor does initially focus her attention on Daniel – as is to be expected given their relationship in her previous episode – but, from the moment she appears until the end of the episode, he says absolutely nothing at all.  Just before the cliff-hanger ending, Hathor lines them all up on the ramp to interrogate them.  She asks Jack for info about the Asgard so that he can make a wisecrack, she asks Sam for the iris codes so that she can look all superior and unintimidated and then… she turns and walks away, completely ignoring Daniel altogether!
