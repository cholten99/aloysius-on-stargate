Secrets

 

Writer: Terry Curtis Fox

Director: Duane Clark

 

And it’s back to Abydos we go!  Daniel looks very cute when he’s all serious in the opening scene of this episode.  And I love the depth of emotion in Jack’s eyes when he questions the sense of telling Kasuf about Sha’re’s possession by Ammonet.  It’s also nice that Daniel reaffirms his trust in Teal’c’s protection in the context of talking about Sha’re.

 

The splitting of the team doesn’t happen all that often, especially not to follow two completely separate storylines, and I always forget about the whole Washington bit.  It just doesn’t stick on the mind like the Abydos situation.  The splitting of the team does allow for some interesting exploration of the separate bonds within the group, though.

 

I really like Kasuf, always have – he’s a great guy and his relationship with Daniel is very well portrayed.  The reveal of Sha’re in the tent isn’t actually all that dramatic, since we’ve got a bigger revelation right around the corner – it’s the pregnancy aspect that’s the real shock, and Daniel’s expression just before the opening credits is positively painful.

 

Basically, I’m really not keen on this episode.  I mean, I’m al for a bit of Daniel angst, but finding Sha’re, pregnant with Apophis’ child, and then losing her again is just too excruciating a turn of events for me.

 

The intensity of the confrontation scene with Sha’re is pretty amazing, though – Teal’c’s staunch presence at Daniel’s side and Daniel’s refusal to be convinced of Sha’re’s control is all very powerful stuff.  I always used to think that Daniel’s aggressive questioning of Sha’re was a bit harsh on the poor girl, but you can see he’s totally thrown by the whole situation and I can forgive him his reaction considering all the emotions he’s battling.  It’s not exactly a scenario for which you can really be prepared!  In the long shot of Daniel when he’s trying to come to terms with it all, I just want to give him a big hug and make all the pain go away.

 

The cut away to Jack joking about getting lost in Washington is such a contrast, but it works pretty well, and interest in both storylines is well maintained throughout the episode.  Jack and Sam seem very relaxed and at ease with each other here, which is nice to see and shows development of their relationship from the stiffness of the early episode.

 

And then we get our fist glimpse of Jacob – yay!  He quickly turns into one of my favourite subsidiary characters, though he isn’t shown at his best in this episode, and the change in his character when he joins with Selmac is quite pronounced.

 

Back on Abydos, Teal’c echoes the role Daniel played for him in the previous episode – telling him harsh truths for his own good.  Much like Teal’c in ‘Family’, Daniel isn’t overly grateful but he does act on the advice.

 

I do like the way Jacob pretends to accept the deep space telemetry cover story – he’s way too smart and knowledgeable about the military to fall for it, though.  The series of knowing smiles from Jack, Sam, Jacob and General Hammond after this conversation is really cute, too.

 

Now, I’m not usually a Jack-babe, but he looks damn hot in his fancy uniform and sunglasses – YUM!

 

There’s actually an awful lot that goes on in this episode, and the way it cuts back to the other storyline every time something interesting happens is very clever.

 

Much as I hate the situation with Sha’re, I do love the scene where Daniel convinces her he still loves her.  It’s nice that they get this moment together and the way he looks when he’s holding her is so bittersweet – it always makes me cry because I know what happens next.

 

The introduction of Jacob is an interesting insight into Sam’s background.  This is the last of the four families to be introduced and completes the set with ‘Cold Lazarus’, ‘Bloodlines’ and ‘The Gamekeeper’.  I feel really sorry for Sam here, trying to deal with Jacob without being able to tell him what she’s actually doing at Cheyenne Mountain.

 

It’s unsurprising that Jack escapes and goes off for a beer.  Obviously, he needs to leave in order to be approached by the reporter, but his departure is very believable – he’d hate having to schmooze at the reception for long.  I love the way he doesn’t even blink at the mention of the Stargate – very smooth!

 

However, glaring continuity error ensues, when the dialogue on the reporter’s tape is different to what was actually said!!  On the tape, Jack responds to Sam’s admission of knowing Washington well by saying, “My condolences”, but in the actual scene, he says, “I’m sorry to hear that.”  Bad, bad, bad!!  I also always thought it was rather indiscreet for Jack to be discussing the Stargate with Sam in public, anyway…

 

Kasuf is so funny – the way he orders Sha’re to go back to Earth with Daniel is hysterical, though he does show good leadership skills in ensuring the safety of the village first.

 

I really hate the scene where Daniel and Teal’c try to take Sha’re home with them.  I just sit there, screaming at the television, “Just press the symbols, for crying out loud!!!”  It’s just too much stress – it’s not good for me, you know!

 

Back in Washington, there’s a weird bit where Sam suggests Kinsey as the leak, to which Jack replies, “Well, if he knows…”  Of course he knows about the Stargate – he’s been there!

 

Then the fact that Daniel actually has to deliver Apophis’ child is just too much – the poor guy has to go through so much in his life, is it really necessary to do this too him as well???  His expression when Teal’c shoots Ammonet with the zat is just awful – it’s so unfair!

 

And Jack’s expression when Hammond assures him the death of the reporter really was an accident is just as bad – deep stuff in this episode!

 

Daniel may be cute in his Abydonian robes, but he’s much cuter in the olive pants and black t-shirt, in my opinion.  You can see more of his muscles, too….

 

Poor Jack just doesn’t know what to think when he arrives through the gate straight into a firefight, but the way he takes out Heru’ur with a knife through the hand device is very cool.

 

I do like the very end – Jack’s “You alright?”, Daniel’s “No, no I’m not, but I will be”, and Teal’c’s continuing support – great team solidarity moment.

 

So, I have to admit this is a very good episode.  Very well structured, lots of intrigue, lots of important stuff going on, and all very emotive.  But I don’t like it – just too horrible for me!

 

Characteristic Moments

 

Sam: Two years spent at the Pentagon trying to make the Stargate program a reality.  I’d say it was time well spent, considering…

Jack: Ya think?

Subtle one, this – nearly slipped by me unnoticed.

 

Best Bits

 

Great conversation where both participants know the other knows it’s all really a lie:

Jack: Retired myself one time – couldn’t stay away.

Jacob: From your analysis of deep space radar telemetry?

Jack: Well, it’s just so damn fascinating!

Jacob: I’m sure it is…

I love these two guys together – there’s always great interaction between them when Jacob’s in it.

 

Jack’s usual irrepressible attitude:

Reporter: When this story breaks, you’re going to be a hero.

Jack: Cool!

 

Daniel snake-baiting – gotta love it!

Heru’ur: I will make slaves of your people.  You will serve me.

Daniel: No, I don’t think so!
