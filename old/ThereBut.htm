There But For The Grace Of God

 

Story: David Kemper

Teleplay: Robert C Cooper

Director: David Warry-Smith

 

Considering this is an ep where Daniel gets separated from the rest of the team and somewhat whumped, you would have thought it would be one of my favourites, but it’s not.  I’ve never been keen on “alternate reality” episodes in shows – I don’t like it when the characters we know and love are in it, but not themselves.  However, watching the ep again in order to write this review, I actually enjoyed it a lot more than I remembered and, putting aside my alternate reality prejudice, it’s a pretty good ep!

 

Promising start with a creepy, deserted alien world – Goa’uld weapon damage, threat assessment from Teal’c, lots of cool doohickeys.  Then we get a demonstration of where Daniel’s reputation for falling into trouble comes from!  Jack utterly fails to use the right technique to get Daniel to leave – he just marches in and orders his kids to head back.  Then, when Daniel protests and asks why, he says, “I don’t want to hear it!” and disappears.  Daniel is the kind of guy who needs a decent explanation before he’ll do what he’s told, and Jack’s total dismissal of him really isn’t going to encourage him to follow orders! 

 

I do like the little sympathetic shrug from Sam – she understands Daniel’s desire to investigate further, but is constrained by chain of command.  Daniel looks totally crushed – Christmas has just been cancelled, but he’s still going to take as many presents with him as he can!  So, we get a classic occasion of Jack trying to drag Daniel away from something interesting, and Daniel ignoring him, touching something he shouldn’t and getting himself into all sorts of trouble.

 

Of course, the fact that Daniel fails to do as he is told actually leads to them saving Earth from Goa’uld attack, so it’s a good job really!  This ep should actually be called “There But For A Hopelessly Inquisitive [And Incredibly Cute] Archaeologist” !!

 

Okay, so the alternate reality theory is a real one, so the idea is theoretically possible – I have no problems with the story itself.  But – there are significant differences between the two realities, including the command structure of the SGC, so I find it incredibly difficult to believe that the SG-1 iris code would be the same!!

 

Brief aside:  I think the marine who asks Daniel to identify himself is the same guy who plays Tommy in the Season 3 ep Seth, but I could be wrong.  It’s not a problem even if he is the same guy – this is an alternate reality, after all, so he could be a marine!

 

Now we get to see Daniel in “confused as hell” mode.  This has several similarities to Daniel’s “pissed as hell” mode, which we have seen in Fire and Water.  (This would be the US meaning of pissed – though I guess the British version would probably have some similarities too…)  In confused mode, Daniel says, “Unless the last two years have been some wacky, wacky dream, I am a member of SG-1” and then he does his best impression of a Thunderbird!!

 

“Confused as hell” mode quickly gives way to “pissed as hell” mode, with the now familiar waving of the arms whilst yelling, “God, no!”  You would have thought a linguist of 23 languages would have a more extensive anger repertoire, but I guess we do lose our higher brain functions when really angry and generally all resort to yelling and arm waving in the end!

 

I always find the introduction of the alternate reality characters very unsettling.  The role reversal of Hammond and Jack is just plain weird, and I really don’t like AU Jack at all.  It’s an interesting portrayal though – basically showing us what Jack would be like without Daniel’s influence and friendship.  He is hard and uncompromising, very much like the Jack O’Neill of the movie, in fact.  Just goes to show that he really needs Daniel, because not even being engaged to Sam softens him up!

 

We get classic Jack/Daniel conflict – the military vs humanitarian argument over sending a bomb to Chulak.  It’s a military view taken to extremes by a Jack who has absolutely no respect for or reason to listen to Daniel, though, which is quite chilling.  This whole episode really shows Daniel’s importance to the Stargate programme – they need his alternative viewpoint and compassion.  Like Jack says in his eulogy in Fire and Water – Daniel is their conscience.  Despite neither of them being in the military, both Katherine and Sam are much harder than their counterparts in our reality, too – probably comes from spending too much time with AU Jack and none at all with Daniel.

 

They need Daniel’s special brand of intelligence, too.  As in Solitudes, Sam only gets so far – identifying the pulses in the deep space transmission as numbers – but it takes Daniel’s intuitive genius to see them as a Stargate address.  Sam’s thought processes are too linear, whereas Daniel is a lateral thinker, which is why he so often gets to an answer when she can’t.

 

Continuity error when they’re trying to dial out:  Gary Jones announced “Chevron Five encoded!” but when we see the monitor, the dialling sequence is only up to chevron four.

 

It’s interesting that the wormhole time limit of 38 minutes is established as canon in an alternate reality!  This becomes a standard assumption on the show (and presumably Sam does work it out in our reality at some point) but the first instance of it doesn’t happen for anyone but Daniel.

 

The Daniel Effect makes a big appearance here!  He’s appeared from nowhere when they’re in the middle of being wiped out by the Goa’uld.  They have no idea who he is and have no reason whatsoever to trust him.  He asks for their help and turns his puppy-dog expression on them all, saying, “Look, if you don’t want to help me, I understand.  It’s your lives, and your world.”  But one look at those pleading eyes and they all jump to sacrifice themselves for him!  That boy could take over the world and no-one would object – oh wait!  He does that in Season 4…

 

Jack’s surrender to the Jaffa is a little silly.  He wanders up, tells them he’s unarmed and takes off his hat.  It’s as if he’s saying – Look! No concealed weapons under the hat! Honest!  Just looks silly to me, is all…

 

And it’s actually AU Jack’s unbending military attitude that dooms them all.  The sending of the bomb to Chulak is what starts the Goa’uld off attaching the gate, and it’s also what prevents him from being able to persuade Teal’c to help them.  Another reason they need Daniel – he’s generally right!

 

We’re introduced to a different version of everybody – and they promptly all get wiped out!  This becomes rather a trend, because the robot versions of SG-1 all get killed when they reappear…  This show ain’t big enough for more than one of anybody!!

 

And so the gradual build-up to the end of Season One begins.  Great climactic ending to this episode – “They’re coming!!”

 

Characteristic Moments

 

We get a “Good morning, campers!” from Jack as the first line of the ep, but the rest of it is necessarily uncharacteristic!
