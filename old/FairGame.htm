Fair Game

 

Writer: Robert C Cooper

Director: Martin Wood

 

Doesn’t the Secretary of Defence likening SGC mission reports to bedtime stories rather diminish the danger and loss of life involved?

 

I like the series of close-ups when he mentions the rescue of SG-1 from Hathor’s clutches.  Jack winces as if he’d rather not be reminded, and Daniel just looks absolutely gorgeous.  Teal’c looks supremely satisfied that people have finally come to agree with him that SG-1 are really cool and worth rescuing, while Sam just looks rather excited that the Secretary of Defence is there to speak to them.

 

They’re all really cute with Sam gets her promotion, too – Teal’c even smiles as she takes her place back next to him, and I think he says ‘congratulations’.

 

I love the way Jack vanishes just as he’s starting his speech – it’s so totally unexpected.  The close-up of Daniel afterwards is great, too – he’s completely stunned and has absolutely no idea what to do.  All the military personnel start running about, but I’m not quite sure what they hope to achieve!

 

Then we get the start of the Jack/Thor friendship – always good entertainment value and a great entrance from the little grey guy here.  The way Jack talks to Thor is really funny – he just accepts whatever Thor says quite willingly and casually responds as if he’s talking to an old friend.  You can see that behind the calm exterior, his brain is looping the loop, but he handles it all brilliantly.  And the way Thor looks at Jack when he starts to get out of line is hysterical – their double act gets off to a great start here.

 

I’ve always been annoyed that Jack’s reappearance back on Earth isn’t shown – it’s ripe for comic potential, but I guess it would drag out the plot too much.

 

Daniel looks remarkably serious throughout most of this episode.  He doesn’t really smile when Sam’s promotion is announced, and the look he gives Jack when he’s being flippant about the events of The Fifth Race is very solemn.  Maybe he’s trying to counteract the way his haircut makes him look so young – or maybe he’s working too hard.  I don’t know – he just doesn’t look very happy.

 

He doesn’t even look pleased when Jack recommends him as an excellent diplomat – rather, he looks alarmed at the prospect of having to take over the negotiations, which is fair enough considering what’s at stake and whom they’re negotiating with.

 

And so begins the run of Yu jokes, that lasts right through to Season Five and probably beyond.  Jack just can’t resist this, but his silly interruptions at a briefing containing rather a lot of important people are maybe a little bit over the top.  Once again, we get a cute shot of Sam trying not to laugh at him, though.  I’ve noticed that she seems to find Jack much funnier than the rest of them do – so maybe they do belong together after all – ACK!!  I didn’t just say that!!

 

Of course, as soon as Nirrti is mentioned, we get an emotional response from Sam, prompted partly by a glance from Janet – both decidedly not keen on allowing the killer of Cassie’s people into the SGC.

 

This is actually a very important episode, since Cronus, Nirrti and Yu all reappear at one time or another.  Obviously, it also sets up Earth as a protected planet and cements the relationship with the Asgard – lots of stuff going on.

 

Tempers are running high as they prepare for the arrival of the Goa’uld.  It’s Teal’c’s turn to get annoyed, refusing to have anything to do with them.  You can tell things aren’t going to run smoothly – everybody’s tense and they’ve got Jack speaking for the entire planet – not the best of set ups!

 

When Sam hands over the Goa’uld weapons, one of them is a za’tarc gun – I didn’t think they appeared until Divide and Conquer in Season Four, so where did they get it from?

 

I like the way Teal’c looks to Jack before handing over his staff weapon – his allegiance is to Jack and not Hammond, and he’ll only follow distasteful orders if they come from Jack.  Must be a bit of a scary moment for Hammond, though!

 

Daniel has a tough job in this ep – the Terrible Trio aren’t exactly the easiest of houseguests and the addition of a vengeful Jaffa doesn’t help matters much.  Considering his personal feelings about the Goa’uld, Daniel does a remarkable job of keeping calm and trying to get everything under control.  He mediates between the Goa’uld and Hammond on the issue of the security cameras, and even manages to lend a friendly ear to Teal’c’s troubles – what  a guy!

 

Jack’s attempts to be polite to the Terrible Trio are really funny – he has a tendency to ramble when he’s nervous, and it doesn’t bode well for the negotiations.  You can tell he’s more than relieved when Thor appears to take charge.

 

I love the way Daniel comes rushing in to find out what happened after the Goa’uld storm out – I bet he hates not being in on the negotiations.

 

We get the first reference to the Replicators here, although only a veiled hint – there really is a lot going on in this episode!

 

I love the way Thor’s eyes widen when Jack accuses him of bluffing the Goa’uld.  He’s not used to Jack’s blunt candour yet!

 

The Goa’uld may be overdressed and full of cliches, but they certainly know the extent of their power.  They must realise that they can demand almost whatever they want and get away with it.

 

The scene between Jack and Thor on the ship is great – Jack trying to get Thor to tell him what to do is really funny.

 

I love Daniel’s sheepish expression when he tells the others about the history between Teal’c and Cronus – he knows he’s about to get chewed out by Jack for not mentioning it earlier, and sure enough, he is!

 

Jack’s definition of Asgard policy as ‘limited benevolence’ is great – I do feel sorry for the Asgard, though, since at this point they do seem rather distant.  Once we actually meet the Replicators, things become a lot clearer.

 

Jack’s not very supportive of Sam trying to use the healing device.  I know she succeeds after his little anti-pep-talk, but it doesn’t seem like a particularly helpful attitude to me.

 

I do like Nirrti’s outfit – the Goa’uld don’t usually have nice clothes, but she is generally an exception to that.

 

Woohoo!  We get a rare Daniel smile right at the end, at the prospect of Nirrti’s punishment at the hands of the other System Lords.  It’s nice to see he’s not totally serious all the time – and he is soooooo cute when he smiles here!

 

All in all, this is a very important episode:

-                     it continues several previously established storylines

-                     it sets up several more

-                     there’s tons of stuff going on within this one story, and it’s pretty much confined to the SGC – it’s hard to pull that off successfully, but it’s very nicely done here.

 

Characteristic Moments

 

The FCOL of exasperation:

Daniel: Apparently, Cronus killed Teal’c’s father.

Jack: Oh, for crying out loud, why doesn’t he tell us these things?

 

 

Best Bits

 

Jack’s levels of silliness crank up a notch at the briefing:

Daniel: Uh, the second Goa’uld representative we’re expecting is – Yu.

Jack: Me?

Daniel: Yu is the name of the Goa’uld.

Jack: Ah.  Sorry.

 

Jack lets us know how he really feels:

Teal’c: I did not attack Cronus.

Daniel: We believe you, Teal’c, but I just don’t think anyone else is going to.

Jack: Certainly not those lying, scheming, no-good-for-nothing, slimy, over-dressed, style-monger…

Sam: Sir!  I’d like to try something.

Jack: I wasn’t finished.
