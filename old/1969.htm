1969

 

 

Writer: Brad Wright

Director: Charles Correll

 

Okay, so call me a spoilsport, but why don’t they just pick a planet that’s not in the way of the sun, and go to this one at a different time of year?

 

I love the way Hammond grabs and stretches Sam’s hand before asking how it is – I’m impressed by her restraint in not saying, “Aaargh! It was fine until you just did that!”

 

I’m not going to go into the problems with time travel – suffice it to say that I have yet to see a show or film dealing with the theme that even remotely makes sense – this episode of Stargate included.  However, it’s good fun nonetheless, and I’ve suspended disbelief many a time for other stuff, so I’m just going to go with it.

 

Teal’c disabling the rocket while all the others are scrabbling around trying to get out is so cool!  Talk about keeping your head in a crisis!

 

Jack does his sarky prisoner routine yet again, and it doesn’t go down any better with US military officers than it does with Goa’uld captors.

 

Question: Since when is Teal’c in danger of being taken over by his symbiote when it becomes mature?  That’s never been mentioned before, and I was under the impression that symbiotes couldn’t take over jaffa – besides, couldn’t he just pull it out and kill it before that happened?

 

I really like the young version of Hammond – he’s very convincing, and he deals with the situation remarkably well.

 

However, I have to admit that I’m not sure how a zat would be able to unlock a padlock.  And I’m not sure about it vaporising metal, either – if that was the case, wouldn’t they cause hull breaches every time they have a zat fight on a ship?

 

I wonder if RDA had it in his contract that he would get a decent outfit.  The hat is a little suspect, but he doesn’t look nearly as stupid as the other three when they change into 60s clothing.

 

Daniel supposedly gets to showcase his amazing linguistic skills – but in actual fact, his German accent is fairly atrocious…

 

When they get to the armoury, I’m not sure how they incapacitate the guard, but it’s certainly not by way of the zat, since the blast actually hits the door of the truck behind him!

 

I really don’t think I wanted to know that Daniel loses his hair in later life – it’s not a good image…  However, I guess we could have taken the end of this ep as proof that he was going to return for Season Seven, since he has to live long enough to go bald!!  Of course, you could argue that it’s possible for them to change the future before it reaches that point, but then how would that affect Cassie’s ability to send them home when they go too far into the future – now you see why I didn’t want to get into the time travel thing…

 

The end is really sweet, and shows yet again that Hammond is just a big old softie at heart!

 

Characteristic Moments

 

The first Oz reference in a while, when they come through the gate in the future:

Jack: Auntie Em!  Auntie Em!

 

Best Bits

 

Not Daniel’s shining moment:

Soldier [in Russian]: You Soviet spies?

Daniel: Nyet.

Jack: Daniel?

Daniel: He just asked if we were Soviet spies.  I just… [realises what he’s just done]

 

Jack’s charming prisoner routine:

Major: I’m Major Robert Thornbird.  And you are?

Jack: Captain James T Kirk of the Starship Enterprise.

Major: Your dog tags say otherwise.

Jack: They’re lying…

 

Daniel – linguist, archaeologist, babe!

Daniel: So we go in disguise – pretend to be foreigners.

Jack: How’re you gonna do that?

Daniel: Well, I speak 23 languages – pick one!

 

Teal’c is a hit with the hippies:

Michael: I’m Michael.

Teal’c: I am not at liberty to reveal my identity.

Michael: Far out!
