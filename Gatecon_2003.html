Gatecon 2003
Tale of a Gatecon Virgin - Day 1
How It All Began

Attending Gatecon was a bit of a spur of the moment thing for me - but that moment was way back in January so, by the time September 10th rolled around, I'd had time enough to get way too over-excited.  A fifteen hour journey after half a day at work soon cured me of that and I finally fetched up at the hotel late on Wednesday night feeling rather apprehensive and wondering what the hell I was doing.

I woke up really early on Thursday morning, all prepared for hours and hours of queueing, since the only thing on the itinerary for the day was registration.  I wandered down to the lobby at 9am, alone and somewhat nervous, and, within two minutes, I had my convention pass and all the info, which goes to show how good the organisation was.

The whole day stretched ahead with nothing to fill it except a vague plan to meet up with someone I had met online a few weeks before.  So, I parked myself on the sofa in the hotel lobby in the hope that I could spot her coming in.  Her name was Ina, and she had sent me a description of what she would be wearing.
Not long afterwards, several other people sat down around me, clutching scripts, and one of them turned to me and asked, "Are you here for the rehearsal?"  In a moment of courageous madness (which seems to happen a lot when I'm at these kind of events), I said, "No, but do you want me to be?"  Seconds later, I had volunteered to play Heru'ur in the skits on Friday night and was in the midst of a read-through!
And so I met Mel - the writer/director of the skits - and Leslie - my co-star for my scene - both Gatecon veterans and great people to know.

After the rehearsal I found Ina and we spent the rest of the day wandering round downtown Vancouver with another Gatecon Virgin named Holly, whom Ina had met in the lobby and whom I recruited to play my first prime in the skits (for the next two days, whenever I spotted Holly, I yelled, "Jaffa - Kree!!").
We managed to occupy ourselves until the cocktail party at 7pm, which reminded us all why we were in Vancouver by presenting us with an Unas, who wandered around in true Unas fashion, loping and snarling but occasionally stopping to pose for photos.

Holly started off the evening in a state of extreme nervous excitement, her face stuck in a manic grin and her camera in danger of being crushed by vicelike fingers.  However, the moment Dougie Arthurs (Heru'ur) and David Palffy (Sokar/Anubis) arrived, she turned into a consumate groupie and went independent on us - every time I saw her after that, she was in conversation with one or other of the guests and they all seemed to know her name within five minutes of being in the building.  It was a very impressive transformation!

I had my picture taken with Dougie (it was actually quite hard not to...) since I was going to be playing his character in the skits the following night.

I also made the acquaintance of Terry, another Gatecon Virgin, who quickly became a firm friend and my main companion for the rest of the week.

I had been told that I would not get any sleep at Gatecon, so I was extremely surprised when the party closed down at 11pm.  Ina had headed back into Vancouver for the night, so Terry and I were left to our own devices.  We repaired to the hotel bar to drink Jack Daniels (it had to be done...) and trade life stories until we got kicked out at 12:30am, but we carried on talking in my room until 2am before heading to bed.
