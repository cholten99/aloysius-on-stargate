Cold Lazarus

 

Writer: Jeffrey F King

Director: Kenneth J Girotti

 

This is one of my least favourite episodes, so this review is probably going to be shorter and less interesting than most – because I didn’t actually watch the whole episode…

 

Well, those hills certainly are yellow, aren’t they?  It’s good to see a planet that doesn’t look exactly like Earth for a change, and a really alien life for that isn’t even humanoid.

 

However, I find it hard to believe that Jack would just wander off on his own, even if there isn’t any obvious risk.  The team rarely split up intentionally and then generally at least in pairs.  This is clearly where Jack gets his “don’t touch anything” attitude from, though – at least he learns from the experience here!

 

Daniel has a weird tendency to refer to Jack as “Colonel” in this episode.  He does it three times, once when asking Teal’c where he is and twice when addressing Jack directly.  Where this suddenly came from, I have no idea, but it sounds very odd and I’m glad it seems to be confined to this one episode.  The problem with addressing Sam is still ongoing, and rather unequal.  Daniel starts calling her “Captain Carter” here, but she’s happily calling him “Daniel”.  It’s all very awkward and disconcerting and I’m looking forward to the time when it’s no longer an issue.

 

The lack of easy companionship within the group at this stage is actually important to the story, though, since later on none of them would have failed to notice that Crystal Jack is acting very oddly.  The burgeoning relationships between the characters are clearly shown here, as Sam tries a bit nervously to make small talk with Jack in the locker room, then chats comfortably with Daniel, who is obviously a lot closer to Jack due to their longer acquaintance.  Teal’c is still very much on the edge of the interaction and it’s still Sam and Daniel who get along best.

 

Then comes the story thread I have the problem with.  I really don’t like all the scenes between Crystal Jack and Sara.  A lot of important stuff goes on to do with their relationship, but it’s not really Jack so the whole thing seems rather pointless to me.  I don’t like the fact that some alien entity is having such intimate conversations with Jack’s wife.

 

The one thing that saves this episode for me is the return of the Science Twins!  The way Sam and Daniel skid into Teal’c’s room, all breathless with excitement, is absolutely adorable, and they look so alike!  I also love their little guilty expressions when they lie about getting permission to fire a staff weapon in the gate room.  Both of them are terrible liars, but clearly prepared to do pretty much anything in the pursuit of science.

 

I also love the way Daniel completely loses the ability to articulate when he’s excited.  It’s a great character trait for a skilled linguist – take him by surprise and he’s instantly reduced to incoherent stuttering – very, very cute.

 

General question: all those soldiers who pile into the gate room with guns at the ready when there’s an unscheduled offworld activation – where are they and what are they doing just before the alarm sounds?  They just appear as if from nowhere every time and I can’t help wondering what they do with the rest of their time…

 

The end of this episode always leaves me dissatisfied.  How on earth is Jack ever going to explain what happened to Sara?  Surely she’s not just going to forget about it, especially after her dead son is presented to her!  And what was the point of the episode in the first place?  We don’t learn anything we didn’t already know, and nothing is actually achieved.  Basically, the story reminds us of Jack’s personal history and that’s it!  There’s no progression, and Sara never appears again, so the whole thing seems rather pointless to me.  And depressing, too!

 

Oh well, there are one or two bits worth highlighting…

 

Characteristic Moments

 

First episode when Jack doesn’t say “for crying out loud”!  Can’t really see it coming from Crystal Jack…

 

However, we get the Carter family catchphrase for the first time instead.

When the crystal forms Sam’s face in the lab:

Sam: Holy Hannah!

I’ve always liked the way this gets picked up with the introduction of Jacob.

 

Best Bits

 

When the alarms start wailing after Teal’c fires at the crystal:

Teal’c: You received permission for me to fire my staff weapon in the gate room?

Sam: Oh, yeah.

Daniel: Absolutely.

Naughty Science Twins!!

 

Real Jack in the holding cell removes all doubt of his identity:

Jack: Come on, get me out of here!  Tell you what, let me put it nicely – get me the hell out of here!

Hammond: If that isn’t O’Neill, I want to know who the hell we’re looking at.
