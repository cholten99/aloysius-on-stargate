Past And Present

 

Writer: Tor Alexander Valenza

Director: William Gereghty

 

I’ve always thought it’s a bit callous to give Daniel a love interest in the episode after Sha’re finally dies.  I know he’s not really seen her in three years, and I know we sort of see him deal with her death as it happens, and I know there'’ not way to tell how much time has passed between the episodes – but I still think it’s a bit soon.  And to present Daniel with a viable love interest, only for her to turn out to be The Destroyer Of Worlds, and then take her away from him is just cruelty to archaeologists!  How much more is this poor guy going to have to deal with??  Well, by the end of Season Three, a fair bit more…  Anyway, back to the review.

 

Classic exploration opening with the team arriving on an obviously inhabited planet.  However, once again it seems as if their preparation is a bit lax.  They seem surprised by what they find, as if they’ve never seen the other sid of this gate before, and Sam has to life up a tarpaulin in order to find the DHD – surely they wouldn’t go on a mission without identifying the presence of a DHD!

 

I like the byplay between Orner and Layall at the beginning – they’re really cute, and so obviously a couple, even if they don’t remember it yet.  As usual, Jack is part bemused, part frustrated by the antics of their new acquaintances.  In some ways, I think he’d be happier dealing with an army of rampaging Jaffa.

 

Daniel’s reaction to Kira (despite my objections to this entire storyline) is so cute.  He immediately goes into nervous stuttery mode, and looks positively gobsmacked when she singles him out as being ‘most welcome’.  He really has no idea just how adorable he is, and that just makes him more so.

 

Everyone on the planet seems very open – I guess losing their memories makes them eager to share what little knowledge they *do* have, even if it’s personal.  Sam and Jack, on the other hand, look quite uncomfortable when Kira tells them of Orner’s feelings for her.

 

Kira certainly knows how to get Daniel’s attention – a bright smile, a sincere welcome, a room full of books, and an obvious thirst for knowledge.  The boy’s got no chance whatsoever.

 

The revelation that the visitor’s name was Linea is a brilliant one.  I always hoped the consequences of their actions in Prisoners would be explored, and the exchange of glances between Sam and Daniel at hearing Linea’s name speaks of major guilt trips to come.

 

Jack’s expression as he watches Daniel and Kira discussing their findings is interesting, and has been the subject of much speculation.  I think he’s seeing the first inklings of their attraction, and he’s worried about Daniel getting hurt.  He doesn’t deal with it all that well later on, but his concern for Daniel is clear here.  I also like the way he glances over at Sam, as if trying to find out if she’s noticed, too.

 

A rare Daniel smile gives this scene even more poignancy with knowledge of what happens later, and can it be – is he flirting?  Awwwww!  It’s good to know that looks aren’t everything for Daniel, and that he’s much more interested in intellect and personality – of course, Sha’re and Kira are both very attractive, but you couldn’t find two women more physically different.

 

I love the way Daniel tells the story of Prisoners – it’s got so many levels.  Obviously the guilt he’s feeling over letting Linea go, but there’s also the emphasis on telling Kira that they were imprisoned unjustly, the fear that revealing their part in Linea’s escape will turn Kira against them, and the pain of the bad memories from his time on Hadante.  So much stuff going on in one little speech!

 

Daniel goes all nervous again once he’s alone with Kira in her room, but Kira’s directness solves that problem!  Daniel does rather seem to attract direct women – reference the kiss from Sha’re in the pilot, Hathor’s obvious dominance, and Shyla’s story of the prophecy in Need.

 

Whatever my feelings about the episode, I really can’t blame Kira for taking the opportunity to kiss Daniel.  The man is irresistible, especially when he’s all cute and nervous and trying to do the right thing.  Then of course we get the ubiquitous fade to black – do they sleep together or do they not?  Not a decision I’m going to make…

 

I love Jack’s reaction to Sam’s suggestion that Kira could be Linea – not only because him singing with his fingers in his ears is really funny, but also because of the implied concern for Daniel.  He doesn’t want to see Daniel get hurt any more than we do, and he’d rather deny the possibility altogether than be forced to ruin Daniel’s fledgling happiness.

 

I hate it when Daniel lets his emotions get in the way of his reason – partly because I hate to see him upset, but mostly because I don’t like seeing him irrational.  It doesn’t suit him.  He actually makes a good point in the briefing room, but he makes it very badly.

 

Daniel: Let me ask you a question.  Who would you trust with your life more than anyone else in the world?  Don’t worry, I won’t be offended if you don’t pick me.

What is up with that?  There’s a world of hurt and bitterness behind these words, and it’s not just related to the current situation.  I’m not sure what the background is to the tension between Jack and Daniel – but there’s definitely a story in there somewhere.

 

Jack’s very good at suppressing his emotions, and can sometimes come across as a little cold – but you can tell from his eyes and the set of his mouth that he can’t bear hurting Daniel, and he hates the necessity of it, but he has to maintain his stony attitude in order to get through it.

 

Teal’c’s loyalty and concern for Daniel is lovely, and I’m glad Daniel acknowledges it.  It shows the progression of their relationship from the previous episode.

 

It’s just so unfair!  I know, as a major character in a TV series, Daniel’s never allowed to be happy, but do the writers have to make him so damn miserable?  Hasn’t he suffered enough?  The scene where he holds Kira after she’s figured out she’s Linea is very reminiscent of the one in Secrets when he holds Sha’re after he’s told her he’s not angry with her – there’s a similar desperation in his face, and a realisation that the situation is only going to end badly for him.  He’s so passionate about protecting the happiness of others, and so *resigned* to the fact that he’ll never experience happiness of his own, it just breaks my heart.

 

I love the way Jack and Teal’c wordlessly dash off to save Daniel after Janet says the vial and ampule are missing.  Their unspoken understanding and simultaneous reactions are brilliant.

 

The scene where Daniel talks Kira down from killing herself is wonderful – Daniel at his best.  It’s also a scene that’s repeated with varying degrees of success in The First Ones and Menace – trying to stop Jack from reacting as a soldier while at the same time trying to stop someone dangerous from harming themselves or anyone else.

 

Daniel’s denial of his relationship with Kira is so sad – he never gets to keep anything that makes him happy, but he can still find it in himself to be happy for her that she has a future, and also ensures she leaves with a clean slate and no residual sense of losing him.

 

And so Daniel gets emotionally clobbered *yet* again.  Sigh!

 

 

Best Bits

 

Jack’s well-founded faith in and respect for Sam:

Sam: Well, still don’t detect anything out of the ordinary, sir.  Neither did the MALP.

Jack: Well, the MALP is worthless.  You, I’ll trust.

 

Sam’s misguided faith in and respect for Jack:

Sam: You realise, of course, the implication.

Jack: [Slight pause] No.
