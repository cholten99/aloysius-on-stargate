Demons

 

 

Writer: Carl Binder

Director: Peter DeLuise

 

I’ve probably said this before, but I just love the noises the gate makes – the slightly icky sucking sound as they exit the wormhole, and the wonderful whoosh-like hiss as it disengages.  The sound effects really do add weight to the credibility of the gate.

 

Jack’s in a particularly silly mood at the start of this one, eliciting long-suffering glances from Sam and Daniel, while Teal’c just pointedly ignores him – poor Jack gets no appreciation!  He then gets shown up by Teal’c revealing that he’s read the Bible, which seems to make Jack feel slightly inadequate since he clearly hasn’t.

 

At last we get a group of humans that it makes sense to have speaking English!  Shame about the American accents, though…

 

SG-1 are interfering little things, aren’t they?  They just waltz into the village and start releasing people who are chained up in the town square.  Does it not occur to them that the people might have a legitimate reason for chaining up the girl?  She might be a mass murderer like Linea for all they know!

 

Being mistaken for demons really makes Jack uncomfortable.  He’s never been sure how to deal with people they meet who either bow down to them or run screaming, which I suppose is understandable.  He has a fairly relaxed attitude towards the members of his team – obviously subservience doesn’t do anything for him.

 

Brief aside – I’d be interested to know how Simon achieved such a neat buzz cut using only medieval hairdressing tools…

 

Once SG-1 have finished ruffling feathers left, right and centre – suddenly Mr Unas wants five sacrifices and, guess what?  The villagers have got one possessed girl and – oh look!  There are four suspicious strangers who’ve turned up and started making trouble – that’s convenient!

 

Daniel doesn’t do a very good job of supporting Jack’s attempt to allay Simon’s fears about the Unas.  He just keeps on pointing out how hard the Unas is, and how scary Sokar is – it takes him ages to get the message from Jack’s glares and finally agree that it’s not a demon!

 

For once, it’s Jack who’s insisting on helping the people, and Daniel who’s pointing out all the drawbacks to his plan – weird shift.  Daniel really seems to be in negative mode, here, while Jack is in ‘save the people no matter what’ mode.  If their saviour complexes ever really co-incided, they’d be a force to be reckoned with!

 

The Canon is really slimy – I’m not keen on him at all.  There’s nothing worse than someone who uses other people’s beliefs against them to manipulate them.

 

Sam puts her stating the obvious hat back on to tell Jack she’s been disarmed when she wakes up in the cage.  Speaking of hats, she seems fairly attached to hers in this episode, or rather it seems fairly attached to her.  The first shot is of her making sure it’s on properly, when they normally take off their hats at the first opportunity, and Sam doesn’t often wear one at all.  She removes it when she’s inside, but it’s back in place out in the square, and still on her head when she regains consciousness in the cage.  I wonder if she was having a bad hair day when they shot all the outside scenes…

 

The whole devil’s mark being impervious to pain thing has always seemed a bit silly to me – all the demon would have to do is say ‘ow’ and he’d be spared.  Of course, Teal’c isn’t prepared to do that, which turns out to be a really bad idea.

 

I do like the range of reactions we get from the other three when Teal’c is about to be thrown in the river:

Daniel: We’ll go back where we came from!

Sam: He’s innocent!

Jack: You sons of bitches!

The negotiator, the righteously indignant, and the antagonist.

 

Now suddenly Jack and Daniel switch places – Jack’s awfully quick to hold grudges, and even consign innocent people to suffering just because their leader is bad.  It doesn’t take much for Daniel to persuade him, though, and I guess his reluctance is understandable considering he thinks Teal’c is dead.

 

The reunion with Teal’c is really sweet.  It’s nice to see them expressing real affection for one another – we know it’s there, but we don’t get to see it very often.

 

This episode goes up and down an awful lot – they’re evil, no they’re not, sacrifice them, let them go, sacrifice them again!  And SG-1 don’t actually have a whole lot to do with it – they just get bandied about from pillar to post.  Ah, the dangers of gate travel!

 

Why Daniel decides to sit on the bottom step with his arms raised above his head is beyond me – it must be incredibly uncomfortable.  And we know he wasn’t chained up like that, because he was sitting on the top step in the scene before.  In actual fact, Jack and Mary are the only sensible ones who aren’t trying to cause themselves severe pain – Sam and Teal’c are sitting on the bottom step when the Unas arrives too.

 

Jack’s usual sarcastic banter with the bad guy results in him receiving his usual whumping from the bad guy – you’d have thought he would have learned better by now.

 

The silent communication thing is working well again – and Daniel’s got much better at interpreting Jack’s military plans.  He does brilliantly warning Mary about their imminent trip down the hill.

 

Poor old Simon gets a bit of a rough deal.  He finally gets his act together and does the right thing, and he gets whumped for his trouble.  But his courage is pretty admirable in the end, since he tries to shield the evil Canon from the Unas – what a guy!

 

Jack kneels down next to an unconscious Simon, feels for a pulse, and declares “He’s alright.”  Alive, maybe, but alright?  He doesn’t look alright to me!

 

I like the extra twist at the end of this ep.  It makes it all the more interesting and also gives them an excuse to kill the slimy Canon guy!  Much better than him really having a change of heart.  Bloodthirsty, me?  Never!

 

Two things at the end –

 

Simon says, “We will bury the gate.”  But even Jack started calling it the circle of darkness earlier on, so there’s no way he picked up that terminology from them.

 

Simon only brought them one pack plus Teal’c’s staff weapon.  I’m pretty sure those rifles they have are fairly expensive – and isn’t it dangerous to leave them with the medieval villagers?  I know it’s a long walk back to the village, but is Hammond going to accept laziness as an excuse?

 

 

Characteristic Moments

 

Actually this is one I consider to be an uncharacteristic moment – Jack proves me wrong yet again when they first arrive in the village:

Daniel: Hello?  We’re not going to hurt you.  We’re – peaceful travellers.

Jack: They’re not buying it, Danny.

He just throws it on in there for no apparent reason, and it sounds totally natural, which is something I never thought I’d say.  Fanfiction has corrupted me yet again – acclimatising me to Jack calling him ‘Danny’ where before it always really jarred.

 

Best Bits

 

The Jackson opinion of medieval Britain:

Daniel: They drilled a hole in the person’s head.  By drilling the hole, the evil spirits are released, thus saving the person from eternal damnation.

Jack: Thus saving the person?

Daniel: Well, they didn’t call them the Dark Ages because it was dark.

 

Jack’s attitude to helping people deteriorates throughout the episode:

Jack: Carter, if I ever get the urge to help anybody again, feel free to give me a swift kick.

 

And later:

Jack: Major, next time Daniel gets the urge to help someone, shoot him.
