Family

 

Writer: Katharyn Powers

Director: William Gereghty

 

Classic unscheduled offworld activation opening, but no, it’s not our heroes returning in pieces earlier than expected, though it is an SG-1 iris code.  Round-robin exposition lets us know it must be Bra’tac, another of those weird conversations where the characters take turns telling each other things they all know already so that the audience can get up to speed.  Daniel says rather indignantly, “But he’s not supposed to use it unless it’s an emergency!”  Soooo – maybe it’s an emergency, eh, hon?  Considering the likelihood of the situation being an emergency, they take an awfully long time to open the iris; I would have thought Bra’tac would be an iris pancake by the time they let him through!

 

Oh, good.  Another episode about Teal’c’s family.  Whoopee.  Seriously, though, does anybody really care even the tiniest bit about what happens to them?  Or is it just me that finds Dreya’c and Rya’c incredibly boring?

 

We do get the revelation that Apophis survived them blowing up his ship after all.  Seems somewhat unlikely given my recollections of that ep, though.  Given the timer-induced tension of Daniel escaping the ship in the last second, there is no way Apophis and Klorel could have made it to the gate before the explosion.  Unless, of course, they were on the other ship – but then why does Daniel say they must have reached the gate just after him?  Hmmmm….

 

The briefing is good, because Jack gets to show his intelligence and special ops expertise for a change.  There’s nothing scientific or cultural about this mission, so Jack gets to do most of the talking and suggesting of tactics – like it!  He’s very compelling when he’s being all intense and strategic.

 

I’ve never understood why they don’t take Teal’c’s family back to Earth the first time round!  They’ve made contact with the Land of Light by that stage, so it shouldn’t have been a problem.

 

The gas canisters thrown through the gate are all very well, but rather risky in my opinion.  Seems lucky that all the guards were within range – there might have been a gun post further away from the gate or some guards patrolling the woods or something.

 

It sounds ever so weird when Jack introduces Daniel to Fro’tac as “Jackson” – I don’t think he ever calls him that before or since, except probably in the movie.  It goes with his introduction of Sam as “Carter”, I suppose, but it’s weird all the same.

 

Many thanks to Sheryl for pointing out the nice symmetry between Daniel being the voice of reason in the light of Teal’c’s family troubles here, and Teal’c doing the same for Daniel in the very next episode.  It’s nice that they can both be there for each other, and are prepared to say unpopular things when needed to bring perspective to an emotional situation.

 

It’s always annoying when minor characters return and are portrayed by different actors, and I’m not very keen on this Dreya’c.  However, she does have a good point when she goes outside to tell Teal’c off for his unreasonable reaction to the situation.  His rather lame response of “Woman, be silent!” shows he knows it too, but just isn’t ready to admit he’s wrong.

 

I think it’s very clever that Teal’c and Daniel look much more comfortable in the robes than Jack and Sam.  I’ve always rather liked Daniel in robes, and it’s a good look for Teal’c too.

 

Okay, so Fro’tac works in the Hall of Records at the palace – so why does he have a First Prime tattoo???

 

When Fro’tac tries to betray them, we get another case of the fluctuating effect of a single zat blast.  It usually renders enemy Jaffa unconscious, but rarely causes loss of consciousness in our heroes.  Fro’tac seems fairly immune as well, since it leaves him able to call for help, thus forcing Jack to kill him.  I guess it does make sense that a zat blast would affect different people to a different degree, though, so maybe I’ll let them off!

 

When they’re discussing whether Rya’c is really himself, Daniel doesn’t say anything, but his expression in his close-up clearly says, “I don’t care if it’s a trap or not – let’s just get out of the rain, please!”  He has totally the same attitude during the scene at the gate, as well – “quit arguing – I’m getting wet here!”

 

I feel rather sorry for the Jaffa walking in the grove and guarding the gate.  Apophis has basically sent them out to get killed by SG-1 so that his plan to destroy Earth can be put into effect.  You really don’t want to work for this guy – the job prospects are not very good!

 

Question: if a zat blast reverses the Apophis brainwashing here, why don’t they just do that with Teal’c in Threshold?  It would save them an awful lot of deprogramming!

 

Good old Tuplo and the Land of Light!  Despite the rude rejection by the Tollan, they’re still willing to offer their planet to refugees!  It doesn’t seem much better than Chulak for Dreya’c and Rya’c, though, since Teal’c still can’t live with them.  He does seem quite snuggly with Dreya’c by the end of the ep though, so I imagine him visiting for weekends.  However, if this is the case, how come he goes all goo-goo-eyed over the priestess in Crossroads?

 

Hey ho!  Not a favourite episode of mine, and there are no bits that stand out as being particularly great or characteristic of the show.
