Bane

 

 

Writer: Robert C Cooper

Director: David Warry-Smith

 

Interesting opening on a mysteriously deserted planet.  It’s good to see a city – usually we just get trees and moorland, so this makes a nice change.

 

I just love Sam’s grimace when Jack steps in the bug poop!

 

This is a good team dynamic scene, with each of them chiming in with theories as to what’s happened on the planet, and all of them listening to each other’s ideas.

 

The first sighting of the bug always makes me laugh, though – it’s not very convincing.

 

Jack goes all out for eloquence in this scene, starting with his “Whoa!” when the bug flies over his head, and continuing with the great “Euch!” noise he makes when he sees it land on Teal’c’s back.

 

The look Teal’c gives Jack when he’s aiming his gun at the bug is great – total confidence that his friend can get rid of the bug without hitting him.

 

Back on Earth, Jack and Daniel do their talking at the same time thing, both responding with “What does that mean?” when Janet tells them about the DNA.  I love the look Jack throws at Daniel when they say the exact same words – as if he’s annoyed that Daniel is stealing his lines!

 

Once again, Sam just happens to have worked with the perfect guy ‘during her time at the Pentagon’.  Is there anything she *didn’t* do while she was there, or while she was at college?

 

Daniel’s stubborn expression at the end of this scene is really cute!

 

There’s a great Jack/Teal’c friendship moment when Teal’c asks Jack to kill him before he turns into some icky – it shows great trust.

 

I love the way Jack says, “Hello, Maybourne.”  Interaction between those two is always good entertainment.

 

The universal sense of frustration with regard to Teal’c being taken is nice to see:

Daniel: How can we just let this happen?

Jack: What do you want me to do?

 

Jack’s question shows he understands Daniel’s anger at the way the military works, and Daniel’s lack of response to it shows he understands there isn’t anything Jack can do.  It’s a nice moment, where they refrain from arguing with each other as they realise they’re both on the same side and both impotent in the situation.  I love the shot of Hammond, Janet, Jack, Sam and Daniel all lined up against the wall, watching Teal’c being led away.  Very nicely constructed.

 

Every time I watch this episode, I find myself hoping that Maybourne will be dead when they reach the truck, but he never is…

 

Now, I make no secret of the fact that I hate kids.  But time and time again, Stargate presents me with child characters I can’t help but like, and Ally is no exception.  The fact that she grows up to be Cassie in Season Five is a little bizarre, but hey, this is television after all!

 

It’s not often that we get interaction between SGC people and members of the outside world on Earth, but it’s usually a good scenario.  I’ve never been overly keen on Teal’c episodes, but I do like this one, and his scenes with Ally are quite sweet.

 

Jack does a great Action Man impression when they bring the bug back through the gate – very impressive, particularly considering his dodgy knees!  I do notice that he takes his gas mask off at the first opportunity, though, even though they haven’t yet used the gas.  Vanity over health and safety?  Surely not!

 

Jack really looks like he’s trying not to be sick when they watch the video of the baby bugs breaking out of the cocoon.

 

Woohoo!  Maybourne gets totally stonewalled by a pre-adolescent girl, whilst surrounded by marines – absolutely classic!  I bet it takes him a long time to live that one down!

 

I love the whole of the last scene with the water guns, though it does seem that Daniel has no sense of fun.  He really doesn’t look all that impressed when Teal’c squirts him – spoilsport!

 

All in all, a fun episode – with loads of great moments – see below!

 

Characteristic Moments

 

Jack: [entering Hammond’s office] You wanted to see me, sir?

Hammond: [indicates Maybourne with a contemptuous nod of the head.

Jack: Oh, for crying out loud…

 

On hearing that they have to go back to the planet to get a venom sample:

Jack: Pack your bug spray, kids.

 

Great Moments

 

After Teal’c has been stung at the beginning:

Daniel: Uh, we’d better get outta here.

Jack: [with heavy sarcasm] No! Really?

Daniel: [pointing at the swarm of bugs coming over the building] No.  Really!

 

Jack: [referring to Maybourne] General Hammond, request permission to beat the crap out of this man.

Maybourne actually looks scared, as if he’s half expecting Hammond to give Jack the go ahead!

 

Common sense vs desire for exploration:

Sam: There may be valuable technology on that planet.

Jack: Carter, there are bugs on that planet.  Big, huge, ugly, honkin’ bugs!!

 

Teal’c’s still taking things literally at this stage:

Ally: Listen, you’re a sitting duck down here.

Teal’c: And ducks are bad?

Ally: Uh, the sitting kind are…

 

Ally’s certainly not fooled by Maybourne’s friendly smile:

Ally: I ain’t seen nobody and, even if I did, I wouldn’t tell you.

Maybourne: And why’s that?

Ally: Because I don’t like the way you smell.

You go, girl!

 

After implementing the electrical current plan, inspired by Jack’s random wafflings:

Janet: [referring to the symbiote] It’s getting better.

Sam: It was so simple.

Jack: [with false modesty] Well, I guess my work here is done!

 

After it’s all over:

Hammond: Maybourne has ordered the insect specimen and all related research transferred to the Area 51 facility.

Jack: I shoulda shot him.

 

Teal’c showing his playful side:

Ally: Do you have any time to play?

Daniel: Actually… [Interrupted by Ally squirting Teal’c and then running off]

Daniel: Guess we shouldn’t have loaded it.

Teal’c: [Producing a pair of shades and another supersoaker] How else will she defend herself? [Squirts Daniel then goes off after Ally]

Daniel: How else?
