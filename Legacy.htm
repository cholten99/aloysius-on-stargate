Legacy

 

Writer: Tor Alexander Valenza

Director: Peter DeLuise

 

Take any group of Daniel fans, ask them what their favourite Daniel episodes are, and I can pretty much guarantee Legacy will feature prominently in the ensuing discussion.  Then ask them *why* they love to see him reduced to a gibbering wreck in a padded cell, and they probably won’t be able to tell you – I know, because I’m one of them!  Anyway, I’m getting ahead of myself…

 

The ep opens with spooky, dark tunnels illuminated only by SG-1’s flashlights – never a good sign – perfect recipe for something mean and ugly to be lurking around.

 

Jack asks Daniel for his opinion, and Sam feels the need to jump in with the announcement that the MALP showed no life signs – I’m pretty sure that information would have been in the briefing.  Give the girl a gold star for stating the obvious!

 

Having Daniel trip over one of the dead bodies seems a bit unfair to me, but I guess it *is* a good method of dramatic discovery.

 

At this juncture, may I just take a moment to say – WOOF!!  Daniel in a loosely tied blue robe, damp hair and no glasses – yum, yum, yum!!

 

Okay, so now on with the plot – the portrayal of Daniel’s gradual deterioration is very effective.  He wanders around looking cute and confused for a bit, and the spooky music makes sure to alert the unsuspecting viewer to the fact that *something* is not quite right.  Then we discover he’s hearing things before we move on to fully blown hallucinations.

 

In the briefing, they establish that the Linvris hosts most likely died of starvation, locked in the room.  Then they discuss the possibility that the killer was also trapped in the room, and they just let it out – so what was the invisible killer living off?  I know they’re just throwing ideas around, but none of it really makes much sense!  In actual fact, there’s never an explanation as to how and why the door of the room was sealed shut from the inside.  The fate of the Linvris and their hosts remains pretty much a mystery…

 

The fact that Daniel sees SG-7 as dead Linvris in SG uniforms when they come back through the gate isn’t all that clear.  It’s a brief shot and they’re far away, and I didn’t spot it the first time I saw it.  Maybe it’s just me being unobservant, though!

 

Having a wormhole form in your closet must be a pretty freaky experience.  Daniel’s pronouncement of “This isn’t real” is remarkably calm – of course, all that changes when the decomposing arm grabs him and pulls him into the event horizon.  I love the fact that we have no idea at this point what’s going on.  Being as clueless as the characters is always fun.

 

In many, many fanfics, writers have Jack sitting by an unconscious Daniel’s bedside in the infirmary, waiting anxiously for him to wake up (I’ve written it myself more than once!).  Of course, in actuality, Jack is across the other side of the room, playing with an IV bag.  He just can’t leave things alone, can he?

 

I just love the way they say hello to each other – it’s so sweet and so totally inconguous.  Then Jack immediately starts getting nervous when Daniel starts talking about ghosts.  Physical injury he has no problem with, but anything psychological and it totally freaks him out.

 

Poor Jack has enough trouble dealing with Daniel when he’s talking sense, but when he starts waffling about the Linvris not being dead and the possibility of them having infiltrated his closet, Jack really doesn’t know what to do.  The way he just keeps on repeating the last thing Daniel says, shows how out of depth he is, and it’s also kinda cute.  You can tell he’s trying desperately to get Daniel to justify his theory rationally – even at this stage, he can’t handle the idea that Daniel might be losing his mind.

 

Of course, Daniel’s not too keen on the idea either.  The little laugh he gives after saying the alternative is that he’s having a nervous breakdown is positively desperate.

 

Then we get the reappearance of Dr Mackenzie – yeuch!  I really can’t stand this guy!  There are bad guys I quite like because they’re cool or funny, but Mackenzie I just hate, no question.  You can tell Jack’s not overly keen on him, either, but the way he comes to an abrupt halt in the doorway as soon as he lays eyes on him.

 

They’re all so tense and defensive at the briefing.  I feel very sorry for Janet, being lumped in with Mackenzie on his side of the table.  Jack looks at her like she’s a traitor when Mackenzie asks him about his headaches, and she looks decidedly uncomfortable about the whole thing.  There’s some very good characterisation of Janet in this episode – the doctor versus the friend.

 

Jack tries to pass Daniel’s hallucinations off as stress, while Janet and Mackenzie leap to the conclusion that gate travel causes schizophrenia – after all they’ve seen, and all they’ve done, and all the things that have happened, why on *earth* does nobody even *suggest* the idea that it might be something from the planet that’s affecting him?  Janet’s totally wrong when she says schizophrenia is the only logical explanation – whenever the Stargate is involved, the most logical explanation is *always* alien influence!  Do they not *watch* the show????

 

I love that Daniel can totally trounce Jack at chess even when he’s infected by an alien organism that’s making him appear schizophrenic.  The fact that Jack is in the VIP room, playing chess with him in the first place is very sweet, though.

 

Jack so desperately wants Daniel to be okay – mental illness is something over which he would have absolutely no control, and he hates the idea of one of his kids being ill and him not being able to do anything about it.

 

We get an almost-hidden Daniel smile when Jack insists they play cards next.  This really is a lovely scene, with both of them showing they really care about the other one in a subtle and affectionate way.

 

The way Jack automatically grabs the deck of cards and starts shuffling, while Daniel automatically grabs the pad and prepares to keep score suggest that they’ve done this a million times before, and speaks very convincingly of the easy intimacy that has developed between them since COTG.

 

Of course, then it gets totally ruined by Daniel watching a symbiote infect Jack.  The eye glow is particularly effective – it freaks *me* out, so no wonder it sends Daniel over the edge.

 

I’ve never been able to figure out why Jack makes Daniel lie down on the floor when there’s a perfectly good bed just behind them, but hey…

 

And then, just before they cart Daniel off to the padded cell, it’s revealed that, of course, he’s *not* going mad, but that there’s something crawling about under his skin.  Way to get all the Daniel fans screaming at the TV – “He’s not crazy!!!  You idiots!!!  Don’t lock him away!!!”

 

The first sight of Daniel in the padded cell is heartbreaking, but you’ve got to admit MS does a fantastic acting job here.  It must have been great for him to have a challenge like this and stretch his acting muscles a bit.

 

Of course, as always, it’s Jack’s name that springs to Daniel’s lips first when they come to visit him.  Then we get the horrible realisation that he knows exactly what’s going on, as he tells them about the removal of his glasses.  There are some great reaction shots of Sam – being female, of course, she’s allowed to let her emotions show, whereas Teal’c and Jack have to be macho and stoic.  You can tell they’re just as cut up about the situation, though.

 

Apologising for going insane is such a totally Daniel thing to do!

 

Then, *finally* Jack says the schizophrenia might be something to do with the Linvris, but he then just dismisses the idea again as soon as Daniel starts throwing himself around the room.

 

Teal’c’s calm pronouncments that there is no-one else in the room are very sweet – he’s showing his softie side again, and he really does seem genuinely fond of Daniel by this point.

 

The problem with appearing insane is the nobody believes you when you say you saw an alien organism invade your friend.  Bit of a bummer, really…

 

Daniel even hugs himself when he’s asleep.  Though I guess a defensive, self-comforting gesture would be a totally natural response to being locked away in a mental institution.  Showing him pounding on the cell door, from outside, is incredibly effective in showing just how cut off he is in there.

 

Jack demonstrates without a doubt that his characteristic malapropism is entirely deliberate.  In a serious situation, where Teal’c is dying and Janet can’t do anything, he gets ‘kel-no-reem’ right first time.  There’s an intelligent, attentive guy hiding under that flippant, sarcastic, dumb façade – but we don’t get to see him all that often.

 

Daniel fighting past the drugs to reason his way through what’s happened is just brilliant.  And I know Mackenzie is only doing his job, and everything he says in response to Daniel is perfectly reasonable, but he does it in such a dismissive, patronising and frankly slimy fashion, that I just can’t stand him!

 

Jack’s attitude when he comes to see Daniel again is really bad.  According to Teal’c, he was starting to think Daniel’s illness might be something to do with the Linvris, and now, when Teal’c’s afflicted too and it should make him more inclined to listen to Daniel, Jack is acting as if he’s just humouring the crazy person.

 

The aggression in Daniel’s voice when he tells Janet he’s back to normal goes to show how little he enjoyed his trip to Mental Health.  There are so many things about this episode that’s wrong, or not developed properly, and Daniel’s anger at Janet here is most definitely one of them.  The anger extends to Jack and Sam too.  It’s not just that they’re reluctant to accept his theory – he raises his voice when he says he had a lot of time in the padded cell to think about it, and they both squirm.  That’s definitely a dig at them for visiting him *once* for about two minutes, and then abandoning him there to rot.  I’m guessing it took them all a while to get over this – one reason for lots of fixit fanfic based on this episode.

 

Interestingly, as soon as Daniel starts talking about Machello’s trap in military terms, Jack starts paying attention and Sam heads off to check out the database.  Theory is all very well in archaeology and anthropology, but soldiers need practical applications for ideas in order to understand them, even scientist soldiers like Sam, it seems.

 

The idea of planting the organisms in a page-turning device is a very clever one – Machello was pretty ingenious!  Unfortunately, the theory that there was one organism left in the device that went into Daneil when he used it doesn’t fit with what happens when they test the device from Area 51 – because all ten slug things come out at once.  Still, I guess the extra one could have just been crawling around on the floor and wandered into Daniel’s boot, but the way they actually present what happened doesn’t make sense!

 

Question – why is Jack in the lab with Janet and Sam?  I guess it enables him to suffer for not listening to Daniel in the first place along with them, but there is no logical reason for him to be in there – shouldn’t he be up in the observation bit with Daniel?

 

Dr Warner’s rather a narrow-minded pessimist, isn’t he?  He completely refuses to entertain the idea that there might be more than one way to get the protein out of Sam’s blood.  He even tells her to stop following Janet’s instructions because she’s wasting her time – does he expect them just to stay in there and starve to death?

 

They save Teal’c, we get triumphant musice, and the camera pans out for a team shot – and that’s it.  This is one of those episodes that ends way too soon, and leaves tons of stuff unresolved – luckily, there’s a wealth of excellent fanfiction out there to fill in the holes.

 

 

Best Bits

 

Jack trying to reason with Daniel:

Jack: Why aren’t they coming through my closet, aside from the fact that yours is cleaner?

 

Jack being cute and supportive:

Daniel: I don’t fee off!  I feel – I feel fine.  No headaches, no tension, I mean, I feel – normal.

Jack: That’s because it was just stress!  And I have a very calming effect on stressed out people.

 

I know this is a bit long for a quote, but it’s such a great scene, I just had to reproduce it in full:

Daniel: Look, Doctor, I know you probably hear this from patients all the time, but I think I’m cured.

Mackenzie: You’re right, I hear it all the time.  But I’m afraid it doesn’t work that way.  You don’t get well from something like this overnight.

Daniel: You do, if there were an alien organism inside you, making you think you were sick when you really weren’t.

Mackenzie: Mm-hmm.  And you found this out – how?

Daniel: Machello told me.

Mackenzie: Machello?

Daniel: He’s an alien old man we met on P3C-599.

Mackenzie: Dr Jackson, you haven’t had any visitors.

Daniel: No, of course I haven’t.  But, you see, Machello is dead.

Mackenzie: Dead?  And yet he told you that there was someone inside you making you appear crazy?

Daniel: That’s a good point!

 

Jack being polite in the face of adversity:

Jack: I’d just like to apologise in advance for anything I say or do that might be construed as offensive as I slowly go – NUTS!!!
