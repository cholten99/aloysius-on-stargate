The Gamekeeper

 

Story: Jonathan Glassner + Brad Wright

Teleplay: Jonathan Glassner

Director: Martin Wood

 

I’m really not that keen on the first half of this ep.  The Jack virtual reality situation gets rather tedious after a while, and the Daniel scenario is just too horrible for words!  The Keeper himself is really annoying, too – he needs a good hard slap if you ask me.  Most people will probably now recognise him as Lieutenant Barclay from Star Trek: The Next Generation, but he’ll always be Murdoch from The A Team to me…

 

Anyway, I don’t actually know how this episode opens as *someone* (who shall remain nameless, but you know who you are, mother!) cut off the start when recording it, but I’m guessing not an awful lot happens before SG-1 get grabbed by the machines.  It’s a great example of them messing with stuff they don’t understand.  One minute, it’s “ooh, doesn’t this look interesting?” and the next minute, they’re immobilised and unconscious.  Oops!

 

There’s much scratching of heads and exchanging of confused looks after the opening credits, which is always entertaining.  But how does Teal’c know the place where he and Jack have appeared is Earth?  Doesn’t look that much different to countless alien worlds, in my opinion!

 

It’s always fun to have Kawalski back, but his appearance here doesn’t make much sense to me.  In Children of the Gods, when he finds out that Jack had a son, it seems as if they only met on the Abydos mission, so how come he’s on this operation from 15 years ago?  If he knew Jack this far back, there’s no way he wouldn’t know about Charlie.  And the fact that Jack says the situation could be real suggests that he’s not surprised Kawalski is there.

 

The revealing of Teal’c’s hair is hilarious, because it’s so unexpected and it looks so silly.  I wonder if Christopher Judge ever had hair and, if so, what it looked like!

 

I have rewound my tape on John’s last words so many times it must be almost worn through, but I still can’t make out what he actually says.  I’m sure it’s not that important, but it’s still really annoying.

 

Then of course we cut to the New York Museum of Art and the agony begins.

 

First of all, what is Sam wearing?  Daniel actually has an excuse for his outfit for a change, since he’s meant to be eight years old, so I’ll let him off this time.

 

The woman who tells them that area of the museum isn’t open to the public doesn’t do her job very well.  She just says her line and wanders off, but then again the look Sam gives her would be enough to scare away most people!  Also, she later morphs into the Keeper, who actually wants them to go into the restricted area so I guess it does make sense.

 

Watching Daniel watching his parents die is definitely not something I enjoy – why am I watching this episode again???

 

The first appearance of the residents is fairly freaky, but it doesn’t make a whole lot of sense.  If they need to be visible in order to watch, why aren’t they there the first time round and, if they don’t, why do they appear and give away that it isn’t real?

 

Then John dies for a second time and I still can’t make out what he says – grrrrr!

 

Back the museum for more painful viewing – now, I’m all for a bit of Daniel angst, but this is too much.  And Sam doesn’t exactly help.  I mean, listening to her spout excitedly about what she thinks has happened to them is just what you want after watching your parents die for the second time in five minutes, isn’t it?  Daniel’s sarcastic “Oh super!” when Sam explains it must be a simulation says it all.  Poor Honey Pie!!

 

Then, as per usual, Daniel loses the ability to articulate as he gets angry, doing his best Porky Pig impression when the Keeper suggests the situation ought to be fun.

 

The transportation devices the Keeper uses to make Jack and Teal’c appear are rather funky – ooh, something I like about this ep!

 

By this stage, Daniel is hugging himself and being flippant, both classic signs that he is very far from being a happy bunny, and it’s no wonder given the circumstances.  We also have typical Sam, wanting to know how the machines work, and typical Jack, wandering off and poking one of the residents while the others argue about the situation.

 

I love the way Jack jumps out of the machine when they are seemingly released.

 

Back at the SGC (only they’re not really), the fake Janet is obviously programmed for stock responses.  As soon as Jack starts to challenge General Hammond, she suggests they return to the infirmary for more tests - classic!

 

Once they escape the holding cell, how come the residents can find them to have a chat about the planet?  If the Keeper is in control of everything, wouldn’t he be monitoring them quite carefully in case they try to cause trouble?  Which, of course, they do!

 

When the Keeper (in the form of Hammond) starts to run away from them, it always makes me laugh – who knew Hammond could move so fast?

 

Okay, so the second half of the episode is pretty funny, but it doesn’t make up for the first half, in my opinion, and this isn’t an episode I watch very often.  It’s just too horrible!!

 

Characteristic Moments

 

Jack and Daniel do their speaking in unison thing right at the end, which is always cute.  Jack asks Daniel if the planet is real this time, then they watch the Keeper stressing about the garden, turn to look at each other and say together: “It’s real”.

 

Best Bits

 

Jack just being Jack – and don’t we love him for it?

Seemingly back at the SGC:

Jack: General, without meaning – this time – to sound like a smartass, are you cracked??

 

The expression on Daniel’s face when Jack outlines his plan towards the end is really wonderful.

When the residents ask SG-1 to show them the planet:

Daniel: Where are we going?

Jack: Back through the gate to show them what their planet looks like… In our memories… Before the virtual reality?  Oh, leave me alone!

It actually seemed like a pretty good plan to me!
