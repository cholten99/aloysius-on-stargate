The Serpent’s Lair

 

Writer: Brad Wright

Director: Jonathan Glassner

 

Daniel gets to start off the new season by being unpredictable for a change.  Understandably, Jack thinks he’s going to protest when he says “Wait!” as they prepare to blow up the ship.  But he’s actually just wanting to set more C4.  The look on Jack’s face when he realises what Daniel’s doing is great – a mixture of admiration for Daniel’s resolve and regret that his mild-mannered archaeologist has been driven to such a violent attitude.

 

The pace of the action doesn’t give us much time to digest the fact that they’re about to blow themselves up – no sooner do they decide on this than they are attacked and captured – again!  As Jack says repeatedly – they’re not really having a very good day!

 

Back on Earth, Samuels has turned up and we discover that he’s been working on a plan with Maybourne – it’s a good job SG-1 have the situation in hand, because if we had to rely on these two to save the planet, we’d all be doomed!

 

Apophis puts on his cliché hat to say that the human race will pay a hundredfold for our insolence.  I guess it fits for the Goa’uld to be unoriginal, since they steal their bodies, their identities and their technology from other people – why not their threats too?

 

Then Bra’tac turns up and things start to look up again.  I love this guy!  Within two seconds of revealing himself, he’s punched Jack on the nose and insulted our heroes!  There’s no question that he’s going to help them, but he’s damn well going to make sure they know how inconvenient it is for him!

 

The echoes from Bloodlines are cool – “cross that bridge” and “not bad”, as well as Bra’tac calling Jack “human” and the pair of them continually trying to impress one another.  The interaction is very funny, as usual.  I particularly like the way Jack lies blatantly about the shuttles so he doesn’t have to confirm Bra’tac’s low opinion of humans.

 

The shuttles conversation also introduces a little thing that Jack and Daniel do now and then, namely speaking in unison.  They do it three or four times across the seasons and it’s very cute.

 

The opening of a sarcophagus is always a little disappointing, I feel.  For such an impressive piece of machinery, it opens incredibly jerkily, rather ruining the effect.

 

The tension of the battle for control between Klorel and Skaara is good, though – an interesting subplot that introduces ideas of Goa’uld possession that are explored later on.

 

When his wonderful plan fails, Samuels turns out to be a weasly coward as well as an obnoxious idiot.  This guy just has no redeeming qualities whatsoever, though I guess it would be hard to maintain them with Maybourne and Kinsey as friends.

 

Daniel’s look of slight confusion as Sam hands him a gun and Jack tells him to watch their backs is very sweet.  He may have come a long way in a year, but he’s still no soldier.  He’s certainly not short on courage, though, as evidenced by him insisting Jack leaves him behind.  This is a great scene – a huge amount is said with looks and gestures.  They don’t have time for a proper goodbye, but they manage to convey a great deal nonetheless.

 

A lot of people seem to think it’s quite funny that the sarcophagus mends Daniel’s clothes as well as healing him, but why shouldn’t it?  It fixes flesh, so why not cloth as well?

 

However, one thing is certain – Daniel jumps through the gate with only one second on the detonators, so there is absolutely no way that Apophis and Klorel could escape after him.

 

There’s a good close-up on Daniel when Hammond asks him where the others are.  Classic scenario where they think he’s dead and he thinks their dead – makes for a great reunion at the end.

 

Before we get to that, though, one or two questions.  How do the guys in the shuttle get them out of the gliders?  Wouldn’t Daniel and Hammond go to meet them when they get back to Earth, rather than waiting for them to arrive at the SGC?  And why does some random guy drag Bra’tac off for a debriefing – this just always seemed a bit weird to me.

 

Then, of course, we get the famous Space Monkey moment.  I mean, what on earth does it mean anyway?  And did Jack know when he said it that those two simple words would lead to endless teasing and torment for poor Daniel in fanfiction?  Probably not!

 

Best Bits

 

Eloquence has never been Jack’s strong point:

Jack: Okay.  Well, I guess now is the time for me to say something profound.  [Long pause while he thinks about it] Nothing comes to mind.  Let’s do it!

 

It’s hard to be optimistic when Teal’c’s around:

Daniel: We’ve failed.

Jack: Alright, take it easy, Daniel.  We’ve been in worse situations than this.

Teal’c: Not to my knowledge.

Jack: Thanks, Teal’c.

 

Bra’tac fails to consider survival as an objective:

Sam: So that’s it?

Teal’c: That is it.

Jack: I think what the Captain’s asking is, what now?

Bra’tac: Now we die.

Jack: Well, that’s a bad plan.
