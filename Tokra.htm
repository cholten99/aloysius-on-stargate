The Tok’ra

 

 

Writer: Jonathan Glassner

Director: Brad Turner

 

It’s all weird and trippy at the start of this ep – evidently, Jolinar’s memories include a fair amount of drugs!  It seems terribly unfair that our first glimpse of Martouf should be blurry and distorted, but that is quickly replaced by a big shout of YAY! because he’s finally here and I love him to bits!

 

There’s much running and shrieking and weapons fire going on in the vision, and lots of people seem to be ahead of Jolinar in the fleeing stakes – so is she the only one who knows the new address?  Seems rather risky to me!  And surely they wouldn’t go straight to their new base if there are already Jaffa in the tunnels – what if one of them saw the address?

 

The transition back to the real world is marked by the classic “sitting up really fast” move used by anyone who has a nightmare or vision on TV.  Does anyone in real life actually sit straight up in bed like that when they wake up?  I’m pretty sure I never have…

 

Jack comes right in with traditional scepticism – way to make your subordinates feel good about themselves!  Besides – Sam had electrodes on her head and everything, so it must have been a scientifically-induced and spotlessly accurate memory-vision!

 

It’s good to have Sam spouting touchy-feely theories instead of Daniel for a change, and I like the way Teal’c comes in with knowledge to support her.  It’s a good team solidarity moment, especially when Jack agrees to the mission – the look of faith he gives Sam at this point is very sweet.

 

Sam is very well characterised in this scene – her frustration at not being able to explain her thoughts scientifically is clear and convincing.  She hates having to base things on faith.

 

I like the way Daniel looks across at Sam when she goes to call her dad, then glances at Teal’c – it shows his concern for her nicely.

 

General Hammond shows his sweeter side when he talks to Sam after the phone call.  He’s quite prepared to jeopardise the mission if she wants to stay on Earth.  He’s quietly comforting in his offer of support, which is nice to see.

 

Our second glimpse of Martouf is annoyingly obscured by his stupid Spaniel-hat.  I also pretty much missed him completely the first time I watched this ep because I was too busy trying to figure out where I’d seen Cordesh before.  I eventually remembered that he’s the long-haired counsellor who runs the ranch in Neon Rider!

 

First clue to Cordesh being the traitor – he switches from normal to symbiote without the head-nod or extended blink.  However, we don’t know about that mannerism yet, so it’s not too obvious.

 

I love the Jack’s little grin as he says, “Take me to your leader.”  And I thought he hated cliches!

 

What’s really amazing is that Jack gets Martouf’s name right first time when he asks Sam about her.  I guess it’s the stress and seriousness of the situation that makes him forget to pretend to be an idiot!

 

The scene between George and Jacob in the hospital is great – they’re both wonderful characters and they play the difficult issues of the scene extremely well.

 

There’s a classic Daniel moment when they meet Garshaw.  He bounces forwards excitedly when he figures out what Tok’ra means, then give Jack a quick sideways glance and falters slightly as he realises this probably isn’t the right moment for exchanging vocabulary – so cute!

 

At the other end of the spectrum, I love Daniel’s depth of expression when they talk about the relationship between host and symbiote – you can tell he’s thinking about Sha’re, but trying not to let that interfere with the situation.

 

Lantesh gets my vote as by far the nicest of the Tok’ra symbiotes, but we don’t get to see much of him, as he seems to let Martouf keep control most of the time.  He has a very cute smile, though, and he’s very sweet when explaining to Sam about his relationship with Jolinar.  I like all of this scene – both Sam and Martouf are trying to deal with difficult feelings, while trying to make friends at the same time, and it’s very nicely played.

 

However, it turns out that men are all the same the universe over – Martouf’s line where he suggests Sam could communicate in a way other than words – please!  I guess it was only a matter of time before he started hitting on her…

 

Daniel does a repeat of the bit in Enigma where he interrupts Sam and Narim, though he doesn’t seem as embarrassed this time.  I guess they aren’t actually kissing this time, at least!

 

I love the way it’s always Cordesh who brings up the issue of spies and infiltration.  Obviously a guilty conscience, or maybe the host trying to break through from within!

 

By the end of the first part, you can pretty much see why Jack’s not overly keen on the Tok’ra – things really don’t get off to a very good start, do they?

 

However, Garshaw’s point of view that the humans find their very existence distasteful is certainly warranted, and it’s understandable that they don’t see a successful alliance in their future.

 

Back on Earth, Jack’s faith in Sam runs up against a wall of good sense.  He can see that she’s being influenced by her emotions, and he’s not prepared to accept her blind defending of the Tok’ra when it’s clearly unfounded.  The little apologetic wince he throws her way when he disagrees with her is very sweet, though.

 

I love the scene in the hospital where Sam tells Jacob about the Stargate.  His transition from sarcasm to disbelief to outright astonishment is great, and Sam’s reactions to his comments are nice to see.

 

Watching people see the wormhole engage for the first time never gets old – and it’s always good to get an injection of wonder into the show.  We’re so used to the Stargate now, that it’s easy to forget just how cool it is.

 

Jacob does remarkably well, dealing with all this in such a short space of time, though I suppose he does have rather an incentive.  I love the fact that he says, “Holy Hannah” a couple of times, showing where Sam gets the expression from.

 

The conversation between Jacob and Selmac/Sarouche is really cool.  They make a great pair.  However, Sarouche says that Selmac has a great sense of humour and has kept her laughing for 200 years – in later episodes, Selmac never really comes across as being all that jolly…

 

I love the little exchange between Daniel and Teal’c after Jack tells them to stay put while he goes after the traitor.  I mean, why does Jack tell them to stay in the council chamber – it makes very little sense, and Daniel doesn’t seem to understand it either!  Shouldn’t they really go back to helping with the evacuation, instead of sitting around doing nothing?

 

They set up the idea of looking for willing hosts as the basis of the alliance between Tok’ra and Tau’ri – Jack says it’s worth exploring, but they never do!  They do provide Charlie in Show and Tell, but that’s another ‘no choice’ situation rather than an active offer of a host.  I suppose the alliance is cemented by Jack revealing Cordesh as a traitor – twice – and by Jacob becoming Selmac’s new host.

 

I like the way Daniel shows his reluctance to leave Sam behind.  He doesn’t get a lot to do in this pair of episodes, but he demonstrates support of and concern for Sam throughout, with looks and gestures.

 

While the rest are waiting for Sam, Martouf and Jacob to arrive on Earth, Hammond looks up at the control room and the female technician says, “Still no SG-1 signal, sir.”  There isn’t even a wormhole at this point, so he certainly shouldn’t be expecting there to be a signal yet!

 

I really like the way Jacob talks about being a host – it’s a very convincing portrayal.  This is about as much information as we get about the relationship between host and symbiote, and it’s not much, but it’s very well done.

 

I always wonder what happened just off screen to make Jack smirk the way he does when he and Garshaw come back from entering co-ordinates into the dialling computer.

 

All in all, not a fantastic pair of episodes, but there are some nice moments, and they set up an awful lot of stuff for the future – and Jacob/Selmac makes another great minor character.

 

Characteristic Moments

 

Oz reference when they first arrive on the Tok’ra planet to find a desert:

Jack: Yeah, where’s that Yellow Brick Road when you need it, eh, Dorothy?

(Followed by a classic eyebrow-raise from Teal’c”

 

Jack finally losing his rag when Cordesh starts interrogating Sam about Jolinar’s death:

Jack: Why should we trust you?  You are, after all, Goa’ulds!

Daniel: [rushing forwards to prevent a disaster] Jack…

Jack: [raising the customary finger] Ah!  Don’t!  We’ve done nothing but get interrogated here.  Now, are we prisoners, and are you Goa’ulds?

Daniel: J-

Jack: [raising his finger again] Nuh!

 

Best Bits

 

When discussing the Tok’ra:

Teal’c: They are hated and despised by the Goa’uld.

Jack: Yes!  My kinda guys!

 

Typical Jack response on trying to diffuse the stand-off:

Jack: Assuming you are the Tok’ra

Cordesh: And if we’re not?

Jack: Well, I guess we all start shooting, there’s blood, death, hard feelings – it’d suck.

 

Universal child response to parental ego:

Jacob: Apparently, I’m one of the oldest and wisest among us!

Sam: Oh, jeez.
