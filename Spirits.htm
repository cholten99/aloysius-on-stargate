Spirits

 

 

Writer: Tor Alexander Valenza

Director: Martin Wood

 

This ep starts off with Hammond telling SG-1 about SG-11 finding the trinium and being sent back for more, and now being 48 hours overdue.  Seems odd that SG-1 don’t know all of this already.  I would have thought they’d keep up to date on the activities of the other teams, especially scientific activities and overdue check-ins.

 

The gate starts to activate and the female technician pops up from nowhere to say, “SG-11’s returning.”  How does she know???  The wormhole hasn’t even been established yet!  They just leave the iris open and wait to be attacked!

 

The arrow coming through the gate is really cool – but why does everyone fall over?  I can understand them ducking when they see the projectile, and obviously Jack falling over is fair enough, but there isn’t any reason for the other to go down like bowling pins!

 

And so Jack gets impaled by an alien weapon again – seems to be a trend with him, which he actually points out himself in the next scene: “Aliens are always poking me full of holes.”

 

I love the looks that are exchanged between the others when Jack claims to be diplomatic.  Hammond wisely demonstrates his own skills in diplomacy by moving swiftly onwards and ignoring the comment entirely.

 

I’m not sure the assignment of SG-1 one to this mission quite makes sense – they need to negotiate the release of SG-11 and preferably also a mining treaty, so a three-man team with an untried commander doesn’t seem like a good choice.  I guess they have already been briefed and it is fairly urgent, though…

 

Sam gives Jack such a cute grin when he points out that it’s her first command and says he knows she’ll do her best.  However, I’m not sure commanding Daniel and Teal’c is exactly a normal command experience!

 

Daniel gets a good opportunity to show off his knowledge in interpreting the clan crest, and Sam is much more interested in what he has to say than Jack is usually.

 

I have to say that Tonani irritates the hell out of me, and I’m not quite sure why.  He’s the spokesman for his people, but he seems almost childlike at times – there’s just something about him that really grates on me, as if the actor isn’t taking the part seriously or something.  I’m probably being overly harsh with that assessment, though.  I just don’t like the character much.

 

After everything they’ve seen, our heroes are still remarkably narrow-minded about the spirits.  I would have thought the combination of the clan crest and their previous encounters with the Asgard would make the presence of an advance alien race really obvious, particularly considering the marks of technology juxtaposed by the simple lifestyle of the Salesh.

 

I can’t imagine Jack being quite so willing to go along with a ceremony as Sam is when Daniel warns her about what might happen.  She does have trouble talking to the animals, though, and the little grin he gives her when she looks to him for help is really cute.

 

It’s good to see Captain Connor again, and nice to know that the First Commandment incident hasn’t harmed his career any.  In fact, he’s now got his own team!

 

Back on Earth, it’s interesting to see Jack fighting the morality corner when Hammond tells them about the orders to steal the trinium.  This is a very different Jack to the one we see in The Other Side in Season Four.

 

I find it extremely bizarre how the aliens end up facing the opposite direction when they shapeshift into their natural form – does this mean they spend most of the episode wandering around backwards???  Strangely, it doesn’t seem to be universal among them, since Tokaya is the right way round as the wolf.

 

Daniel gets to do his stuff again – once again, stopping to talk to the alien invaders sorts out the problem, while all the soldiers get zapped for automatically raising their weapons.

 

Then we get the now common debate over whether it’s okay to deceive people into worshipping you if it’s for their own protection.  Jack and Daniel don’t seem to approve in this case, but they’ve never expressed disapproval of the Asgard in this respect.

 

I have to admit I’m a little confused about numbers in this episode.  SG teams usually have four members.  When it’s revealed that Connor is really Zales, and he gathers the others in the briefing room, there are six of them, which is fair enough considering they were setting up mining operation.  However, at the end, there are seven aliens – so where did the other one come from?  I’m assuming they had to come through the gate in the shape of someone else in order to get to Earth, so how did the seventh get in undetected?  Also, they make SG-11 appear with the rest of the personnel at the end – but SG-11 were on the planet, so how did they get them through the Stargate?  Hey ho – aliens, eh?  Who knows what they can and can’t do?  It’s just a little confusing, is all…

 

There are a few good bits, but generally this episode is all a bit nonsensical for my liking.

 

Best Bits

 

After the Salesh have made their anger fairly clear:

Hammond: Obviously, we started our relations on the wrong foot.

Jack: But that’s so rare, sir.

 

The classic ‘are you really you’ sequences:

First of all, it seems as if Jack and Daniel aren’t communicating very well:

Daniel: Jack?

Jack: Daniel?

Daniel: Are you you?

Jack: Yeah, You?

Daniel: What?

Jack: Never mind.

 

Later, though, they seem to be communicating extremely well:

Jack: How do I know you’re really Daniel?

Daniel: Because.

Jack: Yeah, okay.
