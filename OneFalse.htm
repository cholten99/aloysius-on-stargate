One False Step

 

Writer: Michael Kaplan and John Sanborn

Director: William Corcoran

 

At the start of this episode, Jack seems to be trying his hardest to get interested in the science stuff.  I figure maybe he pissed off Sam on the mission the day before and is trying to make it up to her – he’s the only one of the other three who’s there to watch her playing with her new toy, he suggests an explanation for the heat spots and just generally seems to be trying extra hard.

 

The little bald guys are just so cute – especially the one who carries of the UAV – he’s definitely my favourite.

 

The way Jack keeps saying “Bless!” when Daniel sneezes shows how much his attitude to him has changed since the movie, when Daniel’s allergies were one of the sources of Jack’s contempt.

 

The necklines and wrinkling of the fabric on the aliens kinda gives away the body stockings, but I still really like them, especially the way they move.

 

This is most definitely a ‘Daniel in his element’ episode, at least to begin with, which is really cool because he doesn’t get to do real first contact all that often.  His impression of the UAV is very cute.

 

One of the things I like about this episode is that it acknowledges a huge risk in gate travel – the very likely possibility of introducing a contagion to an alien society that they cannot combat.  Of course, that isn’t actually what’s happened, but at least it finally addresses the issue.

 

The first of the aliens they meet has the most expressive face – he’s so cute when they take his friend through the gate, and he has tremendous character throughout, despite the fact that he never speaks.

 

The Fight is one of the best sequences in all five seasons.  Jack’s sarcasm goes totally overboard, Daniel’s frustration levels increase exponentially, Jack reaches new depths of name-calling with Plant Boy, and it all culminates in the best Daniel moment of all time – the spinning hop!  Classic from start to finish.  I love the way the alien tries to stop them arguing by walking in between them and smiling, and Teal’c’s straight-man concern is very funny, too.

 

The scene between Jack and Daniel in the infirmary is great too – typical men, completely unable to express their regard for each other or to apologise adequately for their behaviour.  It’s a classic example of emotional constipation from both of them.

 

It’s also very indicative of Daniel’s character that he doesn’t gloat when Teal’c says he saw the plants grow.  If it had been Jack how had seen it originally, he wouldn’t have been able to resist saying ‘I told you so’ when it was corroborated.

 

There’s another wonderful Daniel moment when he finally figures it all out.  He says, “That’s it” and just walks out of the gate room.  Jack asks him where he’s going, he turns round, just says “Come on”, and walks out again.  You can tell he’s totally focused on his idea – it’s a brilliant Daniel light-bulb moment.

 

I love the very end of the episode too – Sam’s embarrassment when she admits to talking to her plants is great.

 

All in all, a wonderful episode, as will be evidenced by the length of the Best Bits section!

 

Characteristic Moments

 

Upon their return to the planet, Teal’c does his ‘taking things literally’ thing:

Daniel: Déjà vu.

Teal’c: I am unfamiliar with that term.

Daniel: It means I feel like I’ve been here before.

Teal’c: That is correct, Daniel Jackson – yesterday, when we first arrived on this planet.

Daniel: Right.  What was I thinking.

 

And again, when Daniel leaves him in charge of the video camera:

Daniel: Will you keep an eye on this plant thing for me?

Teal’c: I will keep both my eyes on it, Daniel Jackson.

 

Best Bits

 

A rare chance for Jack to score points against Daniel:

Jack: So, they’re a little less evolved than we are.

Daniel: Well, we don’t want to jump to any conclusions – remember the Nox.

Sam: And they did build these – homes.

Jack: That’s a kind appraisal.  What about the UAV?

Daniel: Well, I’ve tried everything I know about rudimentary communication and, to be honest, I’ve had more successful conversations with dogs.

Jack: So…?

Daniel: So, they’re probably a little less evolved than we are.

 

Jack and Daniel failing, and yet succeeding, to communicate:

Jack: Listen, I, uh…

Daniel: No, no – um, sorry, you were gonna say…

Jack: No – it’s just that, uh, well, you know…

Daniel: Uh… no, I know, I know.  You know that I…

Jack: I know.  It’s obvious there’s something…

Daniel: Something, something wrong with us…

Jack: Physically…

Daniel: Physically…

Enter Janet.

Janet: Well, there’s nothing wrong with you.

Daniel: What?

 

The ‘logic doesn’t apply here’ argument:

Jack: The plant is making that sound?

Sam: Sir, it’s an alien organism on another planet.

Jack: Good point.
