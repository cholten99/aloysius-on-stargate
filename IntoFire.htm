Into The Fire

 

(This review is continued from the previous one, as part of a combined review I did for the two-part season cliffhanger/first ep of new season – explanation for its tone can be found at the start of the previous review…  However, I would like to point out that this review was written with a specific purpose in mind, and that I really have nothing against Sam as a character at all!)

 

Into The Fire is an episode of contrast – namely Hero!Sam as opposed to Victim!Daniel.

 

In the whole forty minutes, Daniel utters precisely 38 words (Yes, I counted them!) and, bearing in mind that you can do that in less than 30 seconds (especially at the speed he normally talks!) that’s really not an awful lot.  Worse than that, he gets absolutely nothing to do.

 

Jack gets zatted by the Tok’ra agent – Sam rushes to put her hands all over him, Daniel does nothing.

Hathor removes the flashback device from Jack’s head – Sam gets a wincing reaction shot, Daniel is nowhere to be seen.

Jack is implanted with a Goa’uld – Sam closes her eyes, winces and turns away, Daniel stares into space.

Makepeace and SG-3 confront the Jaffa guarding Sam and Daniel – Sam hits the Jaffa and dives out of the way, Daniel falls over.

The rescue squad are forced to flee from the gate – Daniel is injured and Sam has to pick him up off the floor and help him to safety.

 

From this point on, Super Sam is the main attraction.  She rapidly acquires a rifle (Daniel remains unarmed until Makepeace gives him a diddy hand gun) and launches into theories and explanations about the energy barrier with little or no provocation.  She figures out that there will be generators that will have to be destroyed and selflessly volunteers to go back alone to perform this task.  Of course, Daniel says he’ll go too, but he’s been arbitrarily injured, which leaves him limping round in the tunnels while Sam gets to be a hero.

 

Major shippiness ensues with Sam defrosting Jack, Jack killing Hathor and the longest hug in history.  Meanwhile, down in the tunnels, Daniel follows the marines about for a while, getting pulled out of harm’s way by Makepeace before being captured by Trotsky.

 

So, in conclusion:

Teal’c and Hammond get to stride around declaring undying loyalty to SG-1 and then do the macho bit, “threading the needle” and arriving at the last second as rather impressive reinforcements.

Jack and Sam get to kill Hathor, destroy the shield generators and indulge in as much physical contact as possible.

Daniel gets to wander about looking decidedly shell-shocked, limp after the marines in a useless fashion and finally kneel in front of the gate as the prisoner of the Jaffa – again.

 

Even the final reunion scene reduces Daniel to the “little kid” in the SG-1 family, as Sam pats him on the back and Jack ruffles his hair.  Given his role in the previous Hathor episode, you would have thought his reaction to seeing her again might have been explored just a teensy bit or he might have been given more of an opportunity to interact with… well, anybody!  As it is, the whole two-parter reminds me of the bit in “Thor’s Chariot”, when SG-1 persuade Hammond that the plight of the Cimmerians is their responsibility.  In the case of “Out of Mind” and “Into the Fire” – Jack killed Hathor, Sam figured out how to disable the energy barrier, Teal’c flew a ship through the gate, and Daniel was, um, there.

 

Scoring: You have to give these two episodes some bonus points for having Daniel sans clothing – but considering that only lasts for about five seconds and then the rest of the time he’s just a glorified mannequin, I’d have to grant a final score of about 4.
