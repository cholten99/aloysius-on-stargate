A Body Worth Guarding


Starsky's reaction to Hutch waxing philosophical is interesting:
"It's too early in the morning for that Hutch."
"You're very werid, you know that?"
It's actually similar to Hutch's reaction to Starsky playing "What If" in an earlier episode - I don't think they actually disagree with each other half the time, it's just such a habit to make fun of each other that they don't think about it.

As per usual, Starsky looks very uncomfortable in a suit when they go to the theatre, and I think the emphasis on Hutch's superior cultural knowledge is a bit overdone.

Huggy's obviously making money - The Pits now has a neon sign instead of the old board that used to be over the door.

Huggy: You look weird.
Starsky: What do you mean?
Huggy: A little lopsided. I know! Cos Starsky without a Hutch is like a pig without the poke.
The little smile Starsky gives at this is really, really cute.

The way Hutch rolls Anna off the couch and flattens her, and then nearly shoots the bellman is hilarious - not exactly on top of his game, though, is he? The liquorice gun excuse is pretty funny, too.

Starsky identifying himself as Hutch's "better half" is really cool. Regardless of slash, they're way closer to each other than they ever will be to any girl.

I love the way Starsky wanders round the hotel room collecting up Hutch's clothes - it's so obvious he knows what's gone on, and Hutch's failed attempts at being casual are great.

Hutch's guitar playing is really lovely - and it's a cute song, too.

I love the way Starsky figures out that Hutch is home by failing to find the key on top of the door - Hutch obviously still hasn't learned about that! However, previously, Hutch has always replaced the key on the doorjamb after using it to open the door, so Starsky's logic is a little faulty. It also means that someone could potentially lock Hutch in his own apartment if they knew about the key!!

My earlier point is proved in the tag - the girl leaves, and Starsky ends the episode falling on top of Hutch - girls may come and go, but nothing is ever going to break up those two!
