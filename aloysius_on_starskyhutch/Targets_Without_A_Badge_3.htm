Targets Without A Badge - Part 3


I have to admit to being thoroughly confused by the mix of the McClelland storyline, the Thomas May storyline and this new bizarre mortgage thing with Huggy - maybe I've not been concentrating properly, but I have *no* idea what's going on by the start of Part 3.

It's a real shame Starsky was wearing underwear in the steam room - I got quite excited for a moment when his towel fell off!

I like the fact that Huggy attempts to clarify things slightly by re-iterating where McClelland fits in when Hutch spots his name on the letterhead - this plot is just confusing enough that I might have forgotten who he was!

What is with Hutch's stupid cowboy hat? He complains about being seen in the pimp's car but he actually fits pretty well with the image in that hat!

Next comment written as I was watching, and before I knew what happens later: Clayburn's great - I love him, he's such a sweetie! [For a reviewer, noted for her observational skills, I'm really not that bright...]

Soldier: Judge McClelland?
McClelland: Yes, what can I do for you?
Soldier: Die!!
It's a classic! Corny, cliched, but brilliant.

The teamwork in rescuing Alison is great. Hutch isn't even in on the plan, but he reacts at just the right moment and follows along perfectly. Excellent action sequence.

The twist is revealed, eliciting a cry of shock and disappointment from me: Oh no! I really liked Clayburn! They really played me well on that one - I can't believe I had no idea he was a baddie. Shame - he was cute!

Hutch makes excellent use of the raised index finger and assertive attitude on Clayburn's secretary - I certainly wouldn't want to get on his bad side. He's very intimidating when he wants to be.

S+H are losing informants, witnesses and suspects all over the place - the body count is really piling up by the end of this one!

Alison sums things up perfectly at the end, though:
Alison: Well, I think you are both wonderful!

My sentiments exactly!

Who cares if I haven't got a sausage of a clue what actually happened? S+H were wonderful, and that's all that matters...
