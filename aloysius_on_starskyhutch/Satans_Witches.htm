Satan's Witches


How on Earth did Hutch persuade Starsky to go on vacation with him to a cabin in the middle of nowhere! The way they bicker on the way up is really funny, and very sweet. I love the way they both say exactly the same things about the location when they get there, but Starsky makes it a complaint while Hutch is rejoicing. I have to say I'm with Starsky on this one - give me a shopping centre and a multiplex cinema and I'm a happy woman. Cabins and countryside are a definite no-no!

Of course it's impossible for S+H to go on holiday without something happening, and earning the wrath of a Satanic cult is just about par for the course for them!

Hutch is being mean again, but the way he creeps up behind Starsky and tickles him is absolutely brilliant.

Hutch is incredibly cute when he's in holiday mode - it's rare that we get to see him so relaxed and happy.

The scene back at the cabin is so wonderfully domestic - Hutch cooking while Starsky 'investigates' the fireplace. This image of them off duty together is great, despite Starsky's complaining.

Hutch really does enjoy winding Starsky up, doesn't he? I have to admit Starsky brandishing the ketchup bottle when he switches the lights back on is hilarious, though!

Then they go out into the night, armed with a spatula and a very small frying pan! They're both very cute when they're nervous.

Hutch tipping Starsky off the couch and then running for it, and Starsky throwing a boot at him and then going back to sleep on the floor is just classic - what a great scene!

I figure if Hutch walks straight past a 'No Trespassing' sign, he pretty much deserves whatever he gets. For a cop, he's not very solicitous of people's rights, is he?

Starsky certainly fills out his underwear rather nicely!

The way Starsky grabs onto Hutch when he's scared is adorable, and their subsequent reaction to the ridiculously attractive Satan worshippers is brilliant. I love the way Starsky leans casually against the wall and then suddenly legs it into the cabin when he realises what he's wearing!

There's another classic exchange of glances when Hutch comes in to find Starsky pinned down by the snake - great moment.

Hutch does at least have the grace to look apologetic when Starsky bemoans their predicament after they get arrested. I assume he then lets Starsky have the top bunk to make up for it!

Starsky: What's our plan?

It doesn't have quite the same impact when they go tearing away in the LTD, as opposed to the Torino, does it! I guess Starsky refused to risk getting his baby dirty in the nasty woods.

Starsky: Watch out for that tree!

LOLOL!!

Does Starsky really say "Holy Cabbage" when they see the Satanists? I've never heard that one before!

Hutch: Get him out of position, and lead him into the woods.
Starsky: To the woods? Why don't you?
Hutch: Cos I'm gonna save the girl.
Starsky: Oh. Why don't I?
Hutch: Cos I thought of it first.
Ah, the advantages of quick thinking!

Lizzie is remarkably calm and practical for a sixteen-year-old who's been held by a Satanic cult for two days and was about to be wed to Satan in a probably fatal ritual. Good on her for not being hysterical.

The sequence when they clobber the bad guys while Lizzie switches the lights on and off is brilliant - clever and funny!

S+H standing back to back, ready to take on the whole cult together is great.

The tag is really funny, too - Hutch's expression when they hear the bear totally cracked me up.

A wonderfully silly episode - I really enjoyed this one!
