The Snitch


(These observations are as noted whilst watching for the first time, which will explain the apparent lack of knowledge of what happens in the later parts of the episode...)

S+H on stakeout is pretty much as I would have expected. The banter is right on form. I can just imagine Starsky failing to do card tricks for five hours and Hutch getting more and more annoyed with him.

I quite like Lionel - he's certainly a cut above S+H's normal brand of informant, and he's also pretty cute.

The DA's cool, too - this is a good ep for minor characters.

In the scene with the DA in Dobey's office, Hutch has got a new jacket - and it's *gorgeous*. It looks great with the black shirt and tight jeans, by far the nicest clothes he's worn in a long, long time - YUMMY!

Showing Lionel's family is a nastily effective way of getting the viewer attached to him. His wife and daughter are really lovely - I have a very bad feeling about this...

I love Starsky's expression when Hutch calls the femals judge 'sir', and Hutch is ever so cute when he starts stammering under pressure on the witness stand.

It's never a good sign when S+H are forced to expose someone by their superiors - I have a *very* bad feeling about this, and I see major guilt trips ahead.

Bringing Dobey into the circle of guilt by Lionel's wife confronting him is clever, too. They're really setting this one up for a huge fall - by this stage I'm absolutely certain Lionel's not going to make it to the end of the episode, and the hints are getting a bit heavy handed.

The hitman isn't exactly inconspicuous in that silver jacket, is he?

Starsky dashing down to check on Hutch after the car explodes is really cute - but I do think leaving Lionel alone is a bit stupid. I guess it's par for the course that Starsky won't be able to think straight if he thinks Hutch is hurt, though.

And it's bye-bye Lionel - no surprise there, but still very sad. The guilt and blame certainly gets spread around after his death - a bit heavy, this! The bit with Huggy and Lionel's daughter is a step too far, if you ask me.

Then we get the famous beach scene - and it's even better than I was expecting! Wow - what a scene. Best show of solidarity between S+H we've ever seen - fantastic stuff.

And if that wasn't enough, we then get a cliffhanger of people trying to kill S+H, including one of Hutch's brilliant leaps into the moving Torino. Exciting, or what?

This is looking to be a really good build up to the finale.
