After the Torino overheats on their way to work:
Hutch: [brandishing the Hutchinson Finger]: Look, Starsky, I think it's about time you made a choice between this piece of junk and me!
Starsky: [positively stricken] Don't make me choose.

It's a bit unfair if you ask me - Hutch's piece of junk goes wrong way more than the Torino, and although Starsky complains about it the whole time he never gives Hutch an ultimatum - and in fact purchases an exact copy of the piece of junk after it gets destroyed in Survival.

I must wax lyrical for a moment in praise of the beauty that is Hutch - in the scene where S+H go to thank Blaine, Hutch smiles three or four times, and it's like an angel has flown down from heaven and manifested itself on screen (well, maybe I'm exaggerating just a little bit, but it’s still lovely to see).

Peter: You see two men together and think "How ugly", right?
Umm, no. :o)

The classic pincer movement S+H use when they're chasing Nick is really cool. Hutch follows him in the car while Starsky circles round behind him on foot. Very smooth - and actually a variation of a chase I put in the S+H fic I wrote before watching the episode. It's nice to see my version of police technique corroborated in the show!

Starsky's leap over the piano in the bar at the end is brilliant, but obviously his counting skills need a little work if he can't even count to six!

Not a particularly great episode, but it has it’s moments.
