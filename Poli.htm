Politics

 

Writer: Brad Wright

Director: Martin Wood

 

Ah, the inevitable flashback episode!  Some TV shows manage to do these quite well, but this isn’t one of them.  It’s too early for this – only the end of Season One.  Not only that, but we get another flashback episode at the end of Season Two – very poor, in my opinion.

 

We’ve just had the start of the build-up to the end-of-seaon-cliffhanger with the previous episode, and now we get an episode that it confined entirely to the briefing room – boring!  Really slows down the pace and excitement set by There But For The Grace Of God.

 

We also get a disturbing lack of faith in Daniel.  He’s just spent the whole of the last episode safe in the certainty that “his” SG-1 would trust and respect him, and act on his information and views.  Then, he comes back to our reality and none of them believe him!

 

And another thing – once Daniel tells Jack and Sam about them being engaged in the other reality, you can literally see the cogs turning in Jack’s head.  The whole Jack/Sam ship business is Daniel’s fault!!  He’s the one that give Jack the idea in the first place – silly boy!

 

The combination of Senator Kinsey and Lt Colonel Samuels really doesn’t help this episode, either – can’t stand either of them!  Unreasonable asshole and slimy sycophant respectively – nuff said!

 

Yadda, yadda, yadda – let’s have a look at what’s happened in the rest of the season, Kinsey doesn’t like it, SG-1 try to defend themselves (rather badly in actual fact), SGC gets shut down.

 

And that’s it!  It’s all over!  End of show!  No more Stargate!  Yeah, right…

 

Characteristic Moments

 

The catchphrase is back, prompted by frustration at Samuels:

Jack: For crying out loud, Samuels, what did you tell this man?

I personally would have used stronger language than this, and am impressed by Jack’s restraint!

 

Best Bits

 

Nobody is immune to the O’Neill wit!

Hammond: Costs nearly a billion dollars just to turn the lights on around here.

Jack: How about a bake sale?  Yard sale?  Garage?

Hammond: This is what I look like when I’m not laughing, Colonel.

Jack: Carwash?

And then of course Hammond does laugh after all!

 

The philosophy of Teal’c:

Kinsey: If they’re so strong, why did you switch sides in the first place?

Teal’c: Because what it right cannot be measured by strength.

That’s right – you tell him, Teal’c!
