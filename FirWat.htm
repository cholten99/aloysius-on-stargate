Fire and Water

 

Story: Brad Wright and Katharyn Powers

Teleplay: Katharyn Powers

Directory: Allan Eastman

 

This is by far my favourite episode from Season One.  I could probably write the review without even rewatching it, but that just wouldn’t be any fun, would it?  I’ll make an effort not to restrict my comments simply to “I love this bit”.  Embarrassing revelation, though – I think it was during this episode that I first started actually talking to the TV and referring to Daniel as “my little honey pie” – you have been warned!

 

Daniel being separated from the rest of the team and getting whumped is pretty much the perfect format of an episode, in my opinion.  This is why most of my fanfiction tends to run along similar lines!

 

The opening shows that General Hammond must have been reading fanfiction too.  SG-1 come back early and he’s calling for a medical team before they’ve even arrived, despite the fact that none of them have actually been injured so far – except in The Nox, of course, but they were fine when the came back to Earth in that ep.

 

First appearance of desert camouflage – my favourite!  It doesn’t get a very good representation here, though, because they’re all wet, but it’s generally much better than the standard green stuff they wear.

 

Jack’s pronouncement of Daniel’s death loses some of its effect due to the fact that he’s already died twice before (counting the movie).  So this is the hat-trick – though, of course he doesn’t actually die here.

 

I like the way the other three all react to the situation differently.  Sam shakes and rocks back and forth, Teal’c gets aggressive, while Jack withdraws into himself.  And Janet finally gets to do more than just spout medical technobabble.  In this episode, she not only gets to do her “concerned doctor” bit, but also demonstrates her mettle by standing up to General Hammond and insisting he put SG-1 on stand down for a week.

 

I know people who can’t watch Daniel’s funeral without crying (you know who you are!), but it’s never really gotten to me that much.  I think I’m just impatient to see the man himself – this episode goes on way too long without even a glimpse!  Jack's speech is wonderful, though, and I like the fact that Teal’c accepts the flag – the other members of SG-1 are basically Daniel’s family, as Hammond points out later on.

 

Then we finally get to see Daniel, albeit a rather soggy Daniel.  So he’s not dead, after all – just lost and alone and totally adorable!  Finally we have the first instance of him calling Carter “Sam”.  We also get that from Jack for the first time – it’s a nice touch at the wake, conveying their increased closeless in the face of Daniel’s apparent death.

 

The wake also gives us the first of Teal’c’s many hats – I don’t think any of them actually suit him – as well as Sam in a truly hideous dress.  Most of the time after this, I think she wears trousers when we see her in civilian clothes and, judging from this outfit, I would say the trousers are a wise choice!

 

The first appearance of Nem is pretty scary – Daniel turns round and he’s just standing there, all fishy – very creepy!  I just love the little shrug Daniel gives him when he points to the first piece of writing on the wall.  It’s as if he’s consciously throwing off the initial failure to communicate and turning all his attention to whatever he’s given to work with.

 

Then Nem lashes out, and we get poor little honey pie crouching in a corner, faced with a scary, threatening monster who won’t talk to him.  I just totally melt when Daniel says, “I don’t know what you want me to do!  I don’t understand!”  How Nem can possibly think he’s a servant of the Goa’uld when confronted by those wide, blue eyes is completely beyond me!

 

We get some nice insight into Daniel when we see his apartment – journals, artefacts, fish, Jackal and Hound game, lots and lots of books.  It’s all very Daniel.  Sam’s perusal of the Abydos journal doesn’t make much sense, though – an entry written whilst on the original mission is on the page right before an entry written on their return to Earth over a year later…

 

Daniel’s little tantrum is just so adorable!  The eyebrows come down, the hands go up, and he actually bounces!  He’s incredibly expressive – I like the way he gets up and starts pacing as soon as Nem gives him some information to work with.  The intellectual challenge completely over-rides his fear – his frustration at not being able to remember much about Bellus is borne out of annoyance at the failing of his memory rather than fear for his life if he can’t answer Nem’s question.

 

First introduction of Dr Mackenzie – I don’t like him even before Legacy.  He’s very condescending.  Super Sam strikes again here – there’s no necessity for any of them to be familiar with hypnosis, but of course she just happens to have some experience in the area!

 

Shippers get a real kick out of Jack hugging Sam, I’m sure – but the glint in his eye and the determined tone of voice as he says, “We’re going back” is purely for Daniel…

 

Then comes the Daniel-whumping section – my favourite scene!  I know I’m not alone in my love for Daniel-whumping, but so far nobody has been able to explain it to me.  What is it about us that makes us want really horrible things to happen to TV characters that we love?

 

And even after being imprisoned, isolated, abused, threatened and subjected to a very painful interrogation, Daniel is still trying to establish peaceful and diplomatic relations.  Barring the Goa’uld, he has an amazing ability for tolerance, compassion and forgiveness.

 

The ending of this episode has always been slightly disappointing – what I really want to see is a group hug!  The other three react to Daniel’s reappearance with remarkable equanimity, though I guess the banter on the way back to the gate is quite sweet.

 

Characteristic Moments

 

Jack’s usual response to people pointing out the obvious.

On shining a pen light in his eyes and seeing him flinch away:
Janet: I’m sorry – you must be photo-sensitive.

Jack: Ya think?

 

Best Bits

 

Flippancy in the face of despair.

Hammond: You know that’s my car, don’t you?

Jack: You should get that window fixed…

 

One of the best close-ups of Daniel ever:

Daniel: I can’t tell you what I don’t know.

Nem: You will – or you will die.

Cue close-up that has me drooling – sorry!

 

Cute reunion scene – in lieu of group hug:

Jack: About that apartment…

Daniel: Oh, you didn’t!

Sam: The day after the memorial service.

Daniel: Memorial service?

Sam: Yeah, the Colonel said some nice things.

Daniel: (at first sarcastic) Oh, he did. (Brief pause, then suddenly interested) He did?
