Empty Spaces

by

Aloysius


There are spaces where once my heart and soul resided. I feel them as voids within me, a gnawing emptiness that can never be filled. It seems strange to be aware of an absence, but I have heard tell of people who feel pain in limbs that have been amputated, and I imagine the ache inside me is the same.


A century I have existed without these things that define the presence of life. And yet the ache is still there. I had hoped it would ease with the passing of the years, but it remains constant. Surely the absence of a soul should lead to callous disregard for the warmth and passion that saturate the living. I should revel in my immortality, take pleasure from my invulnerability, but I find I miss mortal existence.


It's not just the joy and happiness that is lacking, either. I crave the little hurts and disappointments that used to be so much a part of daily life.


Anything would be better than this endless, monotonous void I now endure.


THE END
