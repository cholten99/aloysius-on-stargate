Need

 

Story: Robert C Cooper + Damian Kindler

Teleplay: Robert C Cooper

Director: David Warry-Smith

 

This is the third episode in the trilogy of consecutive Daniel-whumping episodes – we have physical in Prisoners, emotional in Gamekeeper and psychological here.  The poor love really does have a hard time of it at the start of Season Two, doesn’t he?

 

You could say he kinda brings it on himself here, though.  I mean, why does he go running off after Shyla in the first place?  She splits off from the procession, granted, but why does this grab Daniel’s interest and cause him to follow her?  He calls the attention of the others to her even before its clear she’s sneaking off into the forest – don’t get it, I’m afraid!

 

And then it’s also partly Jack’s fault for announcing that Shyla is going to jump – I suspect Daniel could have figured it out for himself, but Jack giving voice to the conclusion really doesn’t help.  Did he really think Daniel would just stand behind a tree and watch?  Jack’s expression as he lowers his gun when threatened by the fake Jaffa is classic – poor Jack hasn’t quite got the hang of keeping Daniel on a leash yet!

 

As for Shyla, she may be a selfish, manipulative, evil bitch woman (issues? who, me?), but you’ve got to admit the circumstances are a little extenuating!  I mean, just think about it for a second.  You’re poised on the edge of a cliff, fully prepared to throw yourself off if the man of your dreams doesn’t suddenly appear to save you, and – lo and behold – who should suddenly appear to save you but a gorgeous hunk of archaeologist going by the name of Dr Daniel Jackson!!  Maybe it’s just me, but I think in this situation underhanded methods of keeping hold of him are the teensiest bit understandable.  Not that I’m sticking up for her – she is an evil bitch woman after all – I’m just saying I can see her point of view…

 

Interestingly, when SG-1 are brought before Pyrus, Daniel kneels down quite happily even before he’s told.  Jack, on the other hand, goes straight into ‘prisoner with attitude’ mode, in an attempt to protect his team.  Notice the way he answers the question Pyrus directs at Sam – taking the captor’s attention away from her.  We also have a canon reference to Jack’s bad knee – something that gets a lot of coverage in fanfiction.

 

Daniel actually seems most upset by the fact that his good deed is rewarded by Shyla failing to stick up for them.  He seems to find the idea that she would lie about what happened rather baffling, poor sweetheart – this is a naivete on Daniel’s part that is lost as the seasons progress.

 

Jack seems to lose his good commander abilities momentarily during the escape attempt.  A good commander would always wait for the last member of the team before moving off, but Jack is halfway down the tunnel before he looks back for Daniel.  It may be mostly Daniel’s fault that they are prisoners, but that no excuse for leaving him behind to get splatted by rocks!!

 

When I saw Michael Shanks in London in January, he said the rockfall scene was one of his least favourite to shoot.  Obviously the rocks that actually fall on him are fake, but he said they then placed real rocks on him to make it look more authentic when Jack starts lifting them off.  Apparently, the scene took a long time to get right, and poor MS got rather cranky having to lie under a pile of rocks for take after take!

 

Having said that the opportunity of keeping Daniel might lead any woman to amorality, I really can’t stand Shyla.  She’s another minor character who would benefit from a good hard slapping if you ask me!  The way she smarms up to Daniel when he wakes up is truly sick-making.  And the gleam in her eye when Daniel tells her he’s used a sarcophagus before – she can actually see her realising this will make it all the easier to get him hooked.  Gah!  Hate her, hate her, hate her!!  (Okay, deep, calming breaths – I can get through this…)

 

Back down in the mines, Sam tells Jack the rockfall wasn’t his fault – but it so was!  He left Daniel behind – again!  I’m really beginning to think he can’t be trusted with my honey pie – he doesn’t take very good care of him, does he?  I know it can’t be easy to watch out for Daniel, though, and what Jack does later in the episode is pretty amazing, so perhaps I’ll let him off just this once.

 

There’s another extenuating circumstance regarding the whole enslavement thing.  Shyla says Pyrus believes SG-1 have a nefarious purpose on the planet – and they do!  They were planning on stealing naquadah!  However, the “aren’t trees great” speech completely destroys any possibility for rational thought about the situation – I mean, please!  I do love the noise Daniel makes when Shyla says he’s her destiny, but then we get the story of Shyla and her mother watching the stars – excuse me while I just go and throw up!  (Are you getting the impression I’m not keen on this woman – evil bitch die die die – ahem… sorry.)

 

Daniel’s first trip down to the mines is pretty funny.  He does seem rather embarrassed by the whole situation, but he really doesn’t do himself any favours here, managing to present it in the way most likely to piss off the others!  His diplomacy skills in general rather suck in this episode – first he really annoys his friends and then he pisses off Pyrus at the banquet too.  The only explanation that I can think of is that the sarcophagus is already affecting him and turning him into an idiot!

 

The effects of the sarcophagus have caused a lot of discussion, and I do think the way it curls Daniel’s hair is rather odd!  However, the whole issue of his sight makes perfect sense to me.  The way I see it is this – if you use a sarcophagus when you’re injured, it puts you back the way you were before.  If you use it when you’re completely healthy, it improves you, but only as long as you keep using it, after which the effects gradually wear off.  So, repeated use means Daniel no longer needs his glasses but, once he stops using the sarcophagus, his sight goes back to how it was before, which is why he needs his glasses again afterwards.  See?  Completely logical!  And hey, it’s a piece of alien technology – who knows how it works!!

 

It worries me that I find Daniel incredibly sexy when he flips out at Jack on his second visit to the mine…  Too much information?  Sorry, but YUM!

 

Anyway, the interplay between Jack and Daniel in this scene is interesting.  Yes, Daniel is under the influence of the sarcophagus, but everything he says must come from somewhere.  The expression on Jack’s face as he leaves is positively murderous – very scary!  All is not well in the Jack/Daniel friendship here – again, all credit to Jack for his later support.

 

The final straw with Shyla is the little smile she gives when Daniel tells her he’s never felt better anywhere in his life – evil bitch woman!!  This is where I start throwing things at the TV!!

 

We do get a couple of rare Daniel grins in this ep – but they don’t really count because he’s drugged up so they’re not sincere happy smiles.

 

If Shyla is expecting to get our sympathy with her whole “Daniel must be free to make his own decision” speech, she is *sadly* mistaken.  Free??  Hardly!  Shackled to the sarcophagus, more like – yes, she lets him go, but only after she’s ensured that he’ll have to come back.  She says he can’t live without her any more – without the sarcophagus she got him addicted to, more like – evil bitch woman!!!  (Sorry…  I’ll try and calm down a bit…)

 

Teal’c, Sam and Jack look totally unimpressed by Shyla’s apology at the gate, and you can’t really blame them, can you?  I’m amazed at their self-restraint in not clocking her one!

 

Back at the SGC, we get more interesting revelations of the darker thoughts that run through Daniel’s mind – how long is he expected to wait for Sha’re and what are the chances he’ll ever get her back the way she was anyway?  It must really upset him after this to have to acknowledge that those thoughts were there all the time, however human and understandable they are.

 

Daniel in withdrawal is way scary!  Who knew he had it in him to be so aggressive!  It’s no wonder Sam looks so frightened!  What doesn’t make sense to me, though, is that he should have superhuman strength and be able to break free from the restraints when his body is in the process of shutting down.

 

Then we get the storage room scene!  Jack is really wonderful here!  His comment about knowing what Daniel is going through is rather intriguing, though, and goes unexplained…  And then the hug – awwwww!  It’s every slash fan’s dream – and even the gen fans have to heave a happy sigh at this one, I’m sure.

 

The end section (not counting the bit with the hugging and forgiving of Shyla – I like to pretend that doesn’t happen) is great.  Sam comes up with a military objective to support Daniel’s desire to go back to the planet, Daniel appeals to Jack as a friend for his support and gets “we can back him up, sir” in response – woohoo!  SG-1 family at its best!

 

Then of course the great ending is *totally* ruined by Daniel *apologising* to Shyla!!  Gah!!  He’s hopeless!!  She says – “I don’t deserve to live after what I’ve done to you” – and for the first time in the episode I’m totally in agreement with her!  Kill the bitch!!  (Ahem! Sorry!)  Daniel is way too forgiving for his own good, in my opinion, but I guess we wouldn’t really want him any other way, would we?

 

Phew!  I made it!  This is always a hard episode to get through – but well worth it, as far as I’m concerned.  It’s got lots of great bits, and a really interesting exploration of Daniel’s character, as well as a good team response to a crisis.
