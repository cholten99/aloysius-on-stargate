Hathor

 

Story: David Bennett Carren and J Larry Carrol

Teleplay: Jonathan Glassner

Director: Brad Turner

 

A rare opening completely away from SG-1 and the SGC, featuring silly archaeologists who obviously don’t watch sci-fi shows, and so don’t know that opening a mysterious sarcophagus is never a good idea!  Their dialogue exposition always seems rather stilted to me – like those adverts on TV that try to persuade us that people talk about the technical advantages of certain washing powders in ordinary conversation.  All the stuff about Daniel’s paper on cross-pollenisation of ancient cultures, Egyptian hieroglyphs in Mayan Temples, and the symbol for Hathor, it just doesn’t sound natural to me.

 

However, I do like the shot of Hathor’s fingers reaching out of the sarcophagus – she must have had a great manicurist in Ancient Egypt; her nails are perfect!  In fact, I like most things about Hathor (well, like is probably not quite the right word!) – the hair, the clothes, the attitude – she’s just really cool!

 

Daniel’s gentlemanly side works against him here.  He’s trying to be polite to Hathor even before she zaps him with the happy gas and I can’t help wondering how far she would have got if she’d only had paranoid, cynical soldiers to deal with.  They’re still men, though, so I suppose she would have managed!

 

Hammond demonstrates that he wouldn’t make a good teacher, since he shows unfair discrimination against Daniel in this scene.  When Daniel attempts to answer Hathor’s question about the whereabouts of Ra, Hammond stops him by saying, “Need to know!”  However, Jack’s just spouted a whole line about monks on Chulak and alternative terminology for the Stargate – essentially confirming its existence – and Hammond didn’t even bat an eye.  Very unfair – telling one pupil off for talking in class, but not the other!

 

The way Daniel acts once he’s had a sniff of the pink stuff is great – it’s not totally obvious, or the others would notice, but he looks rather confused and slightly spaced out from here on in.  It’s particularly effective when juxtaposed with Jack’s sarcastic cynicism.

 

Hathor may be evil, manipulative and unscrupulous, but you have to admit she’s got great taste!  The choosing of Daniel as her beloved demonstrates that.  I love the little smirk he gives her when he realises she’s happy Ra is dead and that he can claim credit for it.  Typical man!

 

Jack gets to show off his mental strength – resisting alien mind control more than the others, just like he did in Fire and Water.  While Hammond and Daniel meander about with expressions bordering on worshipful, Jack continually looks just a bit confused, as if he’s trying to remember something really important.  The happy gas wears off more quickly with him, too, as evidenced by him turning up in Hathor’s quarters later on to question her.

 

This is one of my favourite Sam episodes; her frustration at being the only one not affected is very well played.  The look she gives Jack when he dismisses her by patting her on the arm is priceless!  We also get another great scene where Sam and Janet work out what’s going on – I always like the way they bounce ideas off each other, and each use their own expertise and insight to further their theories.  They make a great team.

 

Then we get Daniel fulfilling his “beloved” role…

Hathor: We do so enjoy the method of procuring the code in your species.  It is much more pleasurable than most.

 

First of all – ewwwww!  The way she says this and the way she removes Daniel’s glasses makes my skin crawl – it’s very creepy.

Second of all – so what does procuring the code involved in other species?  Don’t Unas have sex?

Third of all – go Daniel!  I love the way he grabs her arm when he realises that she wants him to help her create more Goa’uld.  Jack isn’t the only one who can resist her.

This scene in particular always makes me think this must have been a really fun episode to make.  The set-up is just so preposterous but such good fun!

 

When Sam leads her girl power unit into the guest quarters, why does she sweep the ceiling?  Does she think Hathor might be hanging from it, waiting to pounce like a “licker” from Resident Evil – now that’s a horrible image!

 

Also, I don’t believe Janet would just leave Daniel sitting there in a clearly catatonic state.  She’s not exactly essential to the military side of their plan, being rather uncomfortable with a gun, and I’m sure she’d want to make sure he was okay before moving on.

 

Then of course comes Jack’s introduction to the wonder of being Hathor’s first new Jaffa – poor Jack, he gets to be a Jaffa and a host – no wonder he hates the Goa’uld so much.  However, considering Hathor chooses Daniel as her beloved and Jack as her incubator, it seems of that it’s Jack we see with his shirt undone – odd and also very unfair, in my opinion!

 

It’s cool the way the women use the same tactic as Hathor on the men.  The use of womanly wiles to manipulate men has always been a favourite tactic of mine.  I’m much more like to play the dumb blonde (apart from the fact that I’m not blonde!) in order to get men to do stuff for me than I am to march in with a feminist chip on my shoulder.  So I heartily approve of Janet’s plan!

 

Sam: I guess they do train us to take advantage of the enemy’s weakness.

Janet: Exactly!

Exactly indeed!  Good one, ladies!

 

Brief aside: Hathor’s amazing waterproof hair is one of the silliest things I have ever seen.  The shot of her rising up out of the bathtub is so obviously run backwards – it’s ridiculous.

 

The issue of what the men do and don’t remember is rather unclear – or just plain doesn’t make sense.  When he comes out of the sarcophagus, Jack doesn’t realise that he’s been out of it, but he knows they need tranquilliser guns to take out their own men.  Then, after Hathor leaves the base, neither Daniel nor Jack knows what Sam is talking about when she mentions Hathor having control of them.  Later, though, Daniel knows that most of the DNA in the larvae goo will be his – do they know what happened or not?

 

We get all three of the guys in civvies at the end.  Teal’c, as usual, looks rather uncomfortable and unnatural in his jacket.  Jack has nice trousers and an attractive sweatshirt, but Daniel gives us the first glimpse of his hideous anorak.  I really wish somebody would take the boy out and buy him a decent wardrobe!  He’s gorgeous (for crying out loud!) but he nearly always looks positively awful when he’s wearing his own clothes.  Oh well, at least most of the time he’s restricted to military gear…

 

Characteristic Moments

 

This is actually pretty much the opposite of a characteristic moment, but I’m going to put it in here anyway.  When I first started reading Stargate fanfiction, the frequency with which Jack refers to Daniel as “Danny” really annoyed me, because for some reason I was quite convinced he never did that in the show.  This episode is the first instance of me being proved wrong on that count.

 

Daniel: General, I’d like permission to have Hathor come down here and debrief us.

Jack: Whoa, Danny!  I don’t think so.

 

Best Bits

 

Classic Jack/Daniel differing ways of looking at things.

When Hathor tells them who she is:

Daniel: Hathor was the Egyptian goddess of fertility, inebriety and music.

Jack: Sex, drugs and rock and roll?

Daniel: In a manner of speaking, yeah.

 

Jack teasing General Hammond – he does like to push it!

Hathor increasing her control:

Hathor: May we take your hand?

Hammond: Why?

Hathor: We wish to kiss it – to bless you with fertility and joy.

Jack: Well, you can’t pass that up, sir!

Hammond: Yes I can!

 

Classic Jack ego after he comes out of the sarcophagus:

Sam: (looking at Jack’s stomach) Wow – that’s a miracle!

Jack: Crunches.

 

I know there’s a lot of really great angst fic that’s come out of Daniel’s experience with Hathor (I’ve written some myself), but I still always find the exchange between Jack and Daniel at the end of the ep really funny – it’s just they way they say the lines.

Janet: Maybe we’ll at least get a cellular level analysis of the Goa’uld, maybe even find some DNA information.

Daniel: A lot of that will probably be mine.

Jack: Ewww!

Daniel: Yeah.
