Emancipation

 

 

Writer: Katharyn Powers

Directory: Jeff Woolnough

 

Classic going off-world to meet new people episode, obviously with the focus on establishing Sam as an equal member of the team.  Why they feel the need to do this is beyond me, but hey, for some reason an empowerment of women episode was deemed necessary so that’s what we get.

 

This episode really makes me wish I could lip read.  Just after the dogs chasing Abu first appear, there’s a shot of SG-1 preparing to intervene.  Jack and Daniel both say something, and Sam grins – rather inappropriate in the circumstances.  However, there’s no actual sound and I really want to know what they said!  I know they had some sound problems in this episode – some of it had to be dubbed in later – and obviously the decision was made not to bother with this dialogue, which makes me want to know what it was even more!

 

Anyway, once the story gets under way, we get the first instance of first contact on a new planet.  I know they’ve been to Chulak and met the priests, but that was a bit different, since they were going after Apophis at the time.  Unsurprisingly, Daniel steps forward as spokesman, but he hasn’t got his “we are peaceful explorers” patter sorted yet.  He can’t decide how to introduce Jack and allows Jack to agree that they are from the Sea of Ogada.  Later in the series, Daniel generally introduces Jack as “Colonel Jack O’Neill” and usually tells people the truth about their arrival through the Stargate.  Another sign that the details of what they call each other haven’t been worked out yet is that Daniel introduces Sam as “Doctor Carter”.  This is understandable, since this is how she introduces herself to him in Children of the Gods, and he hasn’t started calling her “Sam” yet.

 

Sam herself is initially very irritating.  Her “have I got something growing out of my nose?” line is positively cringe-worthy, but she quickly regains my approval with her desire to rectify whatever she may have done to upset the Mongol warriors.  After this first encounter, I find her general attitude throughout the episode quite admirable, particularly when at the mercy of Turgan – but more of that later.

 

This episode highlights the differences between the team members on several occasions.  Obviously, Sam is singled out for being the only woman, but Daniel’s civilian status is also emphasised, allowing Sam to join Jack and Teal’c in the military category.  When the Mongols threaten them, the three of them immediately pull their weapons, but Daniel is left just standing alongside them with no gun.  I’m assuming General Hammond wouldn’t send him off-world without one, and this is just to show that his instincts don’t prompt him to defend himself automatically like the others.

 

There’s a general continuity problem at this point.  Sam suggests they quit while they’re ahead but Daniel insists the opportunities for cultural analysis are worth pursuing.  The next thing we know, they are in the Mongol camp.  Now, in the very next episode, Sam and Daniel together can’t get Jack to stay in the Land of Light on cultural grounds, but he says nothing here, even though there is clearly a risk to Sam in this society.

 

The scene in which we first see Sam in the native outfit is worth watching three times, so you can focus on each of the guys in turn to see their hilarious reactions!  Jack, in particular, is very funny, clearly finding the situation very amusing, while the other two seem rather lost for words.  This is great characterisation – Daniel and Teal’c are stunned by seeing Sam out of uniform probably for the first time, while Jack is entertained by the sight of a fellow soldier being humiliated.  In actual fact, this seems to me to reveal that his attitude to Sam has changed – he no longer views her in terms of being a woman, but as an equal team member.

 

Daniel, on the other hand, plays the nervous gentleman – it’s really cute the way he averts his gaze when he comes into Sam’s tent in the morning.  I’d be willing to bet that Jack wouldn’t have if he had been the one to go and rouse her!

 

Once it has been established that Sam is missing, we get the civilian vs. military debate between Jack and Daniel.  Daniel, of course, wants to try talking about the situation first, while Jack is ready for war now that one of his team has been compromised.

 

Daniel: How is it that you always come up with the worst-case scenario?

Jack: I practice.

 

As funny as this exchange is, it’s probably also true.  As a military commander, Jack probably has practised at identifying the worst-case scenario.  He must be trained to prepare for the worst so that nothing can take him by surprise in potentially dangerous circumstances.

 

Sam shows courage, intelligence and true spirit when faced with captivity.  She hides her fear well and refuses to admit inferiority, always looking for an opportunity to improve her situation or escape.  On the other hand, she doesn’t endanger herself with stupid defiance.  She does subscribe to the Jack O’Neill “pain-in-the-ass prisoner” routine to a certain extent, but she knows when she’s gone too far and doesn’t invite trouble.  That is, until the other women are threatened, at which point she deflects Turgan’s anger back onto herself, which is what Jack is generally doing when he antagonises their captors in other episodes.

 

One of my favourite lines in this episode wouldn’t stand out to most viewers.  It comes during the scene in which Daniel discusses the history of Mongol women with Mughal.  At one point, he turns to Jack and Teal’c and says, “Mongol women might have been the legendary Amazons.”  See?  You have no idea why that line appeals to me, do you?  Well, allow me to explain!  I went to the Evening With Michael Shanks in London in January 2002, and somebody asked him what was the most takes he needed to get something right.  He said that often the hardest thing he had to do was “looping”, which is where the actors re-record dialogue in post-production if the sound quality of the scene is bad.  He pointed out, as Daniel, he often talks really fast, and this line in particular was extremely difficult to get right – he certainly says it rather speedily!  So, whenever I hear this line, it reminds me of that event (which was very, very cool!), as well as highlighting a cute aspect of Daniel’s character.

 

I only have one minor plot question for this episode: why does Mughal suggest that they wait until the morning before going to trade for Sam?  I know the script reason for doing it is so that the guys can refuse to leave Sam in Turgan’s clutches overnight, but I don’t see the logic of it at all.

 

We get more contrast between Jack and Daniel in the bargaining scene.  I love the way Jack and Teal’c raise their eyebrows in sync when Daniel starts spouting about how Sam is a shaman.  Then comes Jack’s reaction to the situation, which is to pull out his gun and fire it to demonstrate what they can offer.  Interestingly, Jack’s approach is actually the successful one (although Daniel gets to provide solutions to later problems) – he is in a better position to understand what will convince Turgan.  Daniel appeals to reason and compassion, whereas Jack offers a superior weapon, which obviously sways the Mongol warrior.

 

I’m dying to know what happened on P3X-595!  Why didn’t we get to see that episode?  The glance that is exchanged between Daniel and Jack when they tease Sam about whatever it was she did whilst “under the influence” on that mission certainly makes it seem like it would have been a lot of fun to see!  I expect there’s fanfiction out there somewhere detailing that mission – I’ll have to go in search of it!

 

When Abu comes to beg their help in rescuing Nya, there’s more emphasis on Daniel not being military.  Teal’c is on watch but, as soon as the galloping hooves become audible, Jack, Sam and Mughal are immediately awake and alert.  Daniel, on the other hand, takes much longer to get up.

 

However, the argument that follows pits Sam against Jack with regard to whether they will help.  Strangely, Daniel comes in on Jack’s side and demonstrates that he is a Star Trek fan.  His argument that they have no right to interfere with this culture is pretty much a paraphrase of the Prime Directive!

 

Here is where Daniel finally gets to be useful, in that he comes up with the legal technicality idea.  His previous establishment of Sam as a chieftain also allows her to present herself as Turgan’s opponent in the fight.  This is a good team episode, in fact – Teal’c uses his tracking skills, Jack shows his excellent command abilities and insight into the warrior mentality, Daniel demonstrates the usefulness of his academic point of view, and Sam holds her own in a male-dominated society.  They all get stuff to do and they work well together, showing the beginnings of real team bonding.

 

I also like the way Turgan is saved from being a two-dimensional bad guy.  The conflict between love for his daughter and the necessity of maintaining strong leadership by upholding the law is excellently portrayed by his moving forgiveness of Nya followed immediately by the order to stone her.

 

It’s interesting that Sam is still trying to prove herself to Jack, even though he has long ago stopped doubting her.  As she prepares for the fight, he spouts some random cliché advice, which she misinterprets.

 

Sam: You don’t think I can win.

Jack: Sure I do.

 

Jack has real faith in her abilities by now, but she hasn’t yet gotten accustomed to his teasing, still seeing it as lack of respect when, in fact, it probably indicates the very opposite.

 

All in all, a very balanced, team-oriented episode, showing that it is possible to focus on one character without pushing the others into the background.  They haven’t really relaxed into the show yet, and some aspects are a little awkward, but this episode demonstrates real promise.

 

 

Characteristic Moments

 

Yet another outing of Jack’s catchphrase in the bargaining scene – it’s been in every episode so far!

Turgan: I may make her a wife.

Jack: Oh, for crying out loud – five hundred!

 

Best Bits

 

First impressions:

Mongol Warrior: That one is a woman!

Jack: (aside to Sam) Observant bunch!

 

Jack demonstrating his evil sense of humour with a grin of pure delight at the prospect of Sam in native dress:

Sam:  I’m not an anthropologist!

Jack: You are today!
