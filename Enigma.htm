Enigma

 

Writer: Katharyn Powers

Director: William Gereghty

 

Just how much time passes between them sending a probe to check out a planet and actually going themselves?  I don’t know how long it takes for a planet to go from perfectly habitable to raining ash but I would have thought they would check out the video feed again just before they step through, regardless.

 

When Jack tells Daniel to dial up the gate so they can go straight home again, his response is, “No argument.”  It’s almost as if he has to point out that he agrees with Jack, because Jack is probably expecting him to argue!  It’s also funny the way Jack automatically expects Sam to know how long it will be before they’re in danger – if it’s even remotely scientific, of course Sam will have all the details at her fingertips!  Nicely refreshing that she doesn’t have a clue – I’m surprised she didn’t ace a paper on volcanoes sometime during her education…

 

And so we are introduced to the Tollan, yet another superior race with attitudes to match their technology level!  I expect our guys find Omac particularly annoying because he’s human, and they’re meant to be top of the tree as far as humans go.  Even Daniel manages to sound a smidge bitter when he talks about the Dark Ages.  He’s totally irrepressible though – even in the face of Omac’s hostility, he’s still trying to establish a dialogue so he can learn all about Tollan culture.  But then this is the guy who later makes friends with an Unas who’s dragging him off to be the main course at family dinner, so I guess the Tollans are just the rubber ring he uses for teething!

 

Along with Omac and his remarkably silent little band of followers, we of course also get Narim.  He wins the award for the Longest Surviving Love Interest for Sam.  He actually makes it through four whole seasons – and even at the end of Season Five we’re not absolutely sure he’s dead.  We’ve seen no body and, as we know from Apophis, there’s no proof anyone’s dead even if we have seen the body on this show!

 

I have conflicting feelings about Narim.  To begin with, I really quite like him, even if his introductory scene is rather silly.

 

Sam: Why don’t we make it Samantha?

Narim: You may call me Narim.

 

I mean, what else was she going to call him – that’s the only name he’s got!  However, when they go up to the surface, he manages to take a really old, really bad pick up line – the angel comparison – and turn it into a rather sweet compliment.  I note that Sam is more interested in hearing that Daniel called her ‘a brilliant scientist’, but whether this is because she prefers compliments about her mind or because she prefers compliments from Daniel is not made clear…

 

Anyway, all the stuff with the cat is quite cute but, by the time Narim starts talking about Sam’s heart filling in the holes in her head (what is that all about anyway?), the sugar levels are getting a bit much for me, and the whole emotion recorder thingie is taking it a step too far, in my opinion.  Hey ho – Sam so rarely gets to kiss anyone, and it always ends badly eventually, so I guess I should cut her some slack and just let her enjoy it.

 

Similarly, my opinion of Omac shifts as we progress through the episode.  He may have a bit of an attitude problem, but he has good reason to be suspicious and his assessment of Earth society is pretty accurate.  That’s probably another reason why Jack and Hammond get so frustrated with him – he’s basically right about what Tollan technology would be used for on Earth and they just don’t want to admit it.

 

However, Omac loses all my sympathy when he suspects Daniel’s offer of help as being a trick to get information.  I say again – how can anyone look at that face and think there’s any capacity for deceit in Daniel?  Omac doesn’t resist the Daniel effect long, though – by the time they reach the surface, he’s happily trying to explain his technology and giving Daniel little indulgent smiles when he doesn’t understand.  Nobody is safe from the Daniel Effect!

 

The best thing about this episode is that Maybourne turns up!  I just love him!  As the resident antagonist, he beats Samuels and Simmons hands down as far as I’m concerned.  He’s fighting a losing battle the moment he walks in the door, with even Hammond joining in with SG-1 to thwart him at every turn.  At this stage, he still thinks he’s cleverer – in fact, I don’t think he ever loses this conviction, no matter how many times they defeat him!  You’ve gotta admire his perseverance in the face of overwhelming odds, and maybe even feel sorry for the guy in a way.  No?  Oh well – me neither!  He’s always great entertainment when he comes up against Jack, though.

 

Maybourne proves rather ineffectual in the end.  He actually orders the marines in the gate room to shoot Daniel – several black marks against his name for that one!  But his threat to have Daniel removed from the programme forever turns out to be empty, since we never hear anything more about this.  It does seem a tad odd that Daniel just gets away with everything here – Jack points out that there are no civilian laws to cover what happens, but Daniel must be answerable to someone somewhere, being in the position he is in.  I would have thought so, anyway – but hey, having a totally untouchable civilian on the team does turn out to be quite handy every now and then!

 

I’m totally with Jack on loving the Nox.  The look Lya gives Daniel when she comes through the gate is wonderful, and the way she reforms the wormhole and backs casually through it is sooooo cool!

 

We end up with a cute Jack/Daniel moment – “You did good, Daniel!” – and Jack picking up on Daniel’s references to what Antaeus told them about ‘the very young’.  It’s actually a really nice finishing shot, with the four of them lined up on the ramp, looking up at the gate.  Job well done, team!

 

Best Bits

 

When Hammond’s appeal to the President fails:

Daniel: The Pentagon, Intelligence – those I can understand, but the President?  I voted for him!

Poor sweetheart!  It must be very hard for him to have his innate faith in people shattered.

 

Lya’s assessment of the situation:

Lya: Your race has learned nothing, but you have.

Appreciation for our wonderful archaeologist – YAY!
